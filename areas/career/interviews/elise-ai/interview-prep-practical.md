## Task 1: Diagnose the AE Team

**The situation:** AE team has been running for 9 months. Inconsistent performance — most AEs missing quota the last 2 quarters. Identify root causes.

### Given Data

**Team context:**
- 10 AEs, all started same day 8 months ago
- 3-month ramp (everyone hit or came close to ramp quota)
- Enterprise segment only
- Quarterly quota: $400k ARR
- Avg rep closing: $300k ARR across 3 deals/quarter
- 100% outbound (no inbounds)

**Prospecting metrics:**
- 30 working days/month
- 50 calls/day average
- Call → DM connect rate: 10%
- DM connect → Demo Set (SAL) rate: 8%
- Monthly SALs per AE: 6
- Monthly SAL pipeline created per AE: $1.5M

**Opportunity funnel:**
- SAL → Demo Completed/SQL: 85%
- SQL → Evaluating & Pitching: 90%
- Evaluating & Pitching → Pricing & Negotiations: 47%
- Pricing & Negotiation → Closed Won: ~47%

### Andrew's Analysis Framework

**The math tells the story.** Walk through the funnel:

**Top of funnel (prospecting):**
- 50 calls/day × 30 days = 1,500 calls/month
- 1,500 × 10% connect = 150 DM connects/month
- 150 × 8% SAL rate = 12 SALs/month... but data says 6 SALs/month
- **Gap #1:** The SAL number (6/month) doesn't match the math from the activity metrics (should be ~12). Either the activity numbers are inflated (reps aren't actually making 50 quality calls/day) or there's a data integrity issue. This is the first thing to investigate.

**Mid-funnel looks healthy:**
- SAL → SQL (85%) and SQL → Eval (90%) are strong. Reps are setting real meetings with qualified prospects.

**Bottom of funnel is where deals die:**
- Eval → Pricing: 47% — half of evaluated deals never make it to pricing. Why?
- Pricing → Won: ~47% — half of deals that get to pricing don't close. Why?
- Combined late-stage conversion: ~22%. That's the problem.

**Root cause hypotheses:**

1. **Activity inflation / data integrity** — The top-of-funnel math doesn't add up. If reps are logging 50 calls but only producing 6 SALs instead of 12, either the calls aren't quality (dialing, hanging up, logging) or the CRM data is unreliable. First thing to audit.

2. **Enterprise deal complexity vs. rep maturity** — Team is 8 months in, selling enterprise ($400k ARR deals). Enterprise sales cycles are long, multi-threaded, and require navigating complex orgs. These reps may have ramped on smaller/simpler deals and are now hitting the wall on true enterprise complexity. The 47% drop at Eval→Pricing suggests they're getting to the table but can't navigate the buying committee.

3. **No inbound = harder pipeline quality** — 100% outbound enterprise is brutal. Every opportunity is created from cold outreach. Reps need to be excellent at creating urgency where none exists. The 47% Pricing→Won rate suggests prospects are exploring but not compelled — classic outbound problem where the prospect never had a burning need.

4. **Lack of deal coaching at critical stages** — The two 47% conversion points (Eval→Pricing, Pricing→Won) are exactly where a sales leader's coaching matters most. Multi-threading, building business cases, negotiation tactics, creating urgency. If the team is 9 months old and the leader hasn't installed playbooks for these stages, reps are freestyling through the hardest parts of the deal.

5. **Average deal size too small for quota math** — $300k/quarter across 3 deals = $100k average deal. At $400k quota, they need 4 deals/quarter at $100k. Currently closing 3. The gap isn't massive — it's 1 deal/quarter. This could be a pipeline coverage issue (need more at-bats) or a deal-size issue (need to push larger deals).

6. **Pipeline coverage is thin** — 6 SALs/month × $250k avg pipeline per SAL = $1.5M/month pipeline. Over a quarter that's $4.5M in pipeline against $400k quota. That's ~11x coverage... which actually looks fine. But if the late-stage conversion is only 22%, the effective coverage is more like 2.5x. Need 3-4x minimum.

## Task 2: The "Bob" Problem

**The situation:** 3 months later, team has improved rapidly. One rep, Bob, has made progress but still consistently misses quota by 25%. Despite coaching and feedback, he's growing much slower than the team.

### Andrew's Approach (3-5 immediate next steps)

1. **Diagnose before deciding** — Pull Bob's funnel data and compare to the team average. Where exactly is he falling short? Is it activity (not enough pipeline)? Conversion (pipeline but can't close)? Deal size (closing but too small)? The intervention depends entirely on where the breakdown is. Don't assume it's "effort" or "skill" without data.

2. **Have the honest conversation** — Sit down with Bob 1:1. Not a coaching session — a candid conversation. "Here's where you are. Here's where the team is. Here's the gap. What do you think is happening?" Listen more than talk. Sometimes the rep knows exactly what's wrong but hasn't been asked. Sometimes there's something outside of work affecting performance. You need to know before you can act.

3. **Build a 30-day performance plan — with Bob, not for him** — Co-create specific, measurable targets for the next 30 days. Not "improve your close rate" — "move 3 deals from Eval to Pricing this month by doing X." Make it achievable. The goal is to give Bob a clear path to success and a framework to measure himself against. If he's bought in to the plan, he's more likely to execute it.

4. **Pair him with a high performer** — Not a formal mentorship. Put him in a room (or on a call) with the rep who's crushing it. Have him shadow 2-3 deals through the late stages. Sometimes the gap isn't coaching — it's exposure. Reps learn fastest by watching someone else do the thing they're struggling with.

5. **Set a clear timeline and be honest about consequences** — If Bob doesn't hit the 30-day plan targets, the next conversation is about fit. Not as a threat — as transparency. "I want you to succeed here. This plan is how we get there. If we can't close the gap in 30 days, we need to talk about whether this role is the right fit." This is the hardest part of management, but it's fair to Bob and fair to the team.

**The meta-point:** The answer to "what do you do with an underperformer" reveals your management philosophy. Andrew's approach is: diagnose with data, communicate with honesty, give a real chance with a real plan, and make the hard call if it doesn't work. No surprises. No dragging it out for 6 months. That's not kind — that's avoidance.
