# EliseAI — Call Notes: Natalie Ngo

## Who is Natalie Ngo
Contact at EliseAI involved in the hiring process. Provided detailed context on the company's current stage, needs, and healthcare vertical specifics.

## Key Takeaways

### What EliseAI Needs Right Now
1. **Repeatable sales playbook.** They've moved past early PMF into scaling mode — lots of customers onboarding, but no consistent GTM playbook yet.
2. **Knowledge sharing.** Top-performer behaviors aren't being captured or replicated across the team. They need a system for that.
3. **Accountability under a trusted leader.** The sales org needs consistent execution driven by someone the team trusts.
4. **Better data and signal loops.** They're not capturing and using sales data consistently. Need structured feedback from sales back to product and marketing.

### What Andrew Demonstrated
- **Individualized coaching approach:** Leads by understanding what each rep is trying to achieve, not one-size-fits-all. Told the story of the skeptical senior rep. This landed well.
- **Data-driven growth decisions:** Argued growth should come from real sales and unit-economics data, not generic pressure to scale. This aligned with Natalie's point that investors are hands-off and the real challenge is internal data discipline.
- **Sales as a signal provider:** Pitched the idea that sales should be both a consumer and provider of information — structured feedback loops to product and marketing. This is a core strength Andrew can bring.
- **Onboarding framework:** New hires spend time in CS/support first, learning from customer problems before selling. Practical, differentiating idea.
- **Team cadences for cross-functional signal:** Regular structured feedback on assets, product gaps, market reactions — packaged back to partner teams.

### Healthcare Vertical Complexity
Natalie made clear that different healthcare segments need different motions:
- Women's health, dermatology, ophthalmology all differ in patient needs, visit patterns, practice organization, tech adoption willingness
- Appointment economics and ownership structures vary meaningfully
- This isn't "one playbook for all of healthcare" — it's vertical-within-a-vertical specialization

### Executive Transition Issue
- Andrew shared a situation at TrackForce where a leadership transition slowed deal approvals and created frustration
- Natalie acknowledged the difficulty and discussed perception mismatches
- Shows Andrew is dealing with some instability at current role (relevant to urgency of finding the next thing)

## What This Tells Us About the Role

This is a **sales leadership role on the healthcare side** where the core mandate is:
1. Build repeatable playbooks across multiple healthcare verticals
2. Install knowledge sharing and accountability systems
3. Create structured data/signal loops between sales, product, and marketing
4. Scale what's working while respecting that each vertical is different

Andrew's background maps almost perfectly to this. The individualized coaching, data-driven decision making, cross-functional signal sharing — these aren't things he's making up for the interview. This is what he actually does.

- **The analytics feedback loop from Facts Unlocked** (measure → score → suppress → decompose → generate briefs) is philosophically the same thing Natalie described needing: capture what works, suppress what doesn't, make it repeatable. Andrew could literally describe this pattern in the interview.
- **The high-ticket sales psychology framework** (trust → certainty → urgency → identity) applies to how EliseAI sells to healthcare practices — these are high-stakes decisions for practice owners.
