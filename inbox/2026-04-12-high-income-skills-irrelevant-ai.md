# Most High-Income Skills Will Be Irrelevant in 10 Years

**Author:** Hussain Ibarra (engineer-turned-writer, 80M+ views)

## Core Thesis

Traditional high-income technical skills (programming, data entry, legal research, financial analysis, medical diagnostics, copywriting) are being eaten by AI. The real split isn't skilled vs unskilled — it's **sovereign individuals** (who leverage AI + own their output) vs **dependent workers** (who rely on employers for income and identity).

## The Four Skills That Matter

1. **Agency** — Identify problems, solve them independently, generate income without employers. Not taught in school. The ability to *act* rather than wait for instructions.

2. **Taste & Perspective** — Your unique life experience is an unfair advantage AI can't replicate. The combination of *what you've seen* and *how you filter it* is the moat.

3. **Judgment** — High-quality decision-making, especially when you have significant leverage. Bad judgment + AI leverage = catastrophic. Good judgment + AI leverage = exponential.

4. **Deep Generalism** — Cross-domain knowledge that lets you synthesize insights others miss. Specialists get automated; generalists who connect dots across fields become more valuable.

## Recommended Path: One-Person Business

The author argues a one-person business provides everything employment used to offer (identity, structure, connection, status, contribution, meaning) while maintaining autonomy.
