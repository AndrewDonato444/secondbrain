# Anthropic Mythos & Project Glasswing

## What Happened

Anthropic announced Claude Mythos Preview — their most powerful model to date. It represents a "step change" in capabilities, particularly in cybersecurity. The model autonomously discovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS, browser, and critical software. It found and exploited a 17-year-old FreeBSD RCE vulnerability with zero human involvement after the initial prompt.

## Project Glasswing

Instead of releasing Mythos publicly, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing — giving the model to major tech companies (Microsoft, Apple, Amazon, Google, CrowdStrike, Cisco, NVIDIA, JPMorganChase, Linux Foundation, Palo Alto Networks) to fix their software before attackers build equivalent capabilities.

- $100M in usage credits committed
- $4M in direct donations to open-source security orgs
- 50+ organizations getting access
- No plans for general availability

## Why It Matters

- First time a major AI lab withheld a model because it was too capable in a specific domain
- Federal Reserve and Treasury Secretary met with bank CEOs about the cyber implications
- Sets a precedent for how frontier AI capabilities get deployed — controlled release to defenders first
- The race is now: can defenders patch faster than attackers can build their own version?
