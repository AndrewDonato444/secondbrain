# Entrepreneurship & Escape Velocity — Pulse

> The interest that drives most of Andrew's after-hours work: building toward W-2 independence. The escape velocity number is $15k/month — once side projects reliably hit that, the day job becomes optional.

---

## Thesis

Companies are fragile. Andrew has watched too many "stable" employers change their plan overnight to keep believing in W-2 stability as a long-term strategy. The escape isn't a single big bet — it's a portfolio of compounding side bets that each teach something the next one needs. Every shipped project is tuition; every shelved project is also tuition. The pattern Andrew has been running for a year — "build something, learn from it, apply those lessons to the next thing" — is the actual strategy. The specific projects are interchangeable; the build-loop fluency isn't.

The current AI moment is a once-in-a-generation tailwind for solo operators. The cost of building a real product is collapsing toward "a Saturday." The cost of acquiring users isn't. So the leverage point isn't building skill — it's distribution skill plus the discipline to ship something useful enough to be talked about. The convergence with the sales-product thesis is exact: the sovereign individual era is real, and the people who make it work are the ones who can both build and sell.

The debt situation (~$100k) is real but not the bottleneck. Time and attention are. The job pays the bills today; every hour outside the job is invested in eliminating dependence on the job tomorrow.

## What I'm watching

- **MSA progression toward $1k/mo subscriptions** — primary path to escape velocity. Beta closing, agent-branded reports, referral mechanics.
- **Facts Unlocked monetization** — passive income channel; modest views growing. Self-optimizing pipeline is the real asset; whether the channels themselves scale is secondary.
- **AutoLinkedIn / outbound-as-a-service productization** — the friend project that's quietly a repeatable B2B service.
- **Sovereign individual narratives** — Naval-shaped takes, Sahil Lavingia, Pieter Levels, the indie hacker stack — what's actually working at $10k–$50k/month MRR.
- **The "everything is buildable" thesis playing out** — when the production cost goes to zero, what's the new edge? (Distribution + judgment + taste, per ai-frontier and convergence pulses.)
- **Andrew's own time allocation** — what's working, what's not, where attention is leaking. Pattern: when 3+ projects all want attention, the brain itself becomes the bottleneck.

---

## What would change my mind

- MSA fails to convert beta users at the $1k/mo target — would force a real reckoning on whether B2B SaaS for niche verticals is the right vehicle vs. something further from his sales background.
- A W-2 opportunity emerges (Elise, Coram, or other frontier-AI co) that meaningfully accelerates learning + compensation + optionality more than independent work would.
- The AI tailwind compresses the indie window faster than expected — i.e. solo operators get leapfrogged by venture-funded AI-native challengers before $15k/mo is reachable.
- The portfolio approach is actually noise — Andrew would learn more from going deep on one project for 12 months than continuing to rotate.

- **2026-04-24** — DeepSeek V4 open-source at frontier quality with dramatically lower pricing makes the "cost of building collapses" thesis more concrete — but GPT-5.5 input tokens are now 8x more expensive than GPT-5 was 8 months ago ($0.63 → $5.00/M input). The cost of building with commodity models is falling; the cost of building with frontier models is rising. The arbitrage between the two is where the indie builder opportunity lives.
