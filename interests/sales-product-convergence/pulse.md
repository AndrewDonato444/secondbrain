# Sales–Product Convergence — Pulse

> The wedge of Andrew's career trajectory. The thesis that the boundary between "selling" and "building" is collapsing, and the operators who position themselves at that intersection will own the next decade of GTM roles.

---

## Thesis

The functions on the org chart — sales, product, marketing, RevOps, CS — are dissolving into each other faster than any company is willing to admit. CSMs are shipping product copy. AEs are writing demand briefs. PMs are joining sales calls to debug deals that are actually roadmap problems. We give people performance review credit for "stretch assignments" instead of acknowledging that the chart no longer maps to the work.

The cause is two-sided: AI is collapsing the production cost of every functional output (copy, decks, briefs, code, design) so the bottleneck moves from *who can produce* to *who is closest to the customer when the question lands*. And distribution is becoming more important than product because every product is becoming buildable — your competitor will match your features; they cannot match your users.

The career play: be the operator who has *seen* convergence happen — pipeline-shaped observations, not theoretical takes. The sales seat is the highest-signal vantage point because deals reveal where every other function is failing. A pipeline stage-2 stall is a marketing problem dressed as a sales problem. A stage-4 stall is a product problem dressed as a sales problem. Most companies don't read their pipeline as a cross-functional diagnostic. The ones that do pull ahead.

---

## What I'm watching

- **Companies quietly rebuilding "post-sale prospecting"** — renewals/expansions that look more like net-new outbound than they used to. The CSM-as-SDR collapse is happening before anyone names it.
- **PMM → demand gen → sales handoffs that aren't handoffs anymore** — single-owner motions emerging at AI-native companies (Notion, Linear, Zebriez case studies).
- **Comp plan vs. roadmap mismatches** — when SPIFs reward a different motion than the roadmap claims, the truth is the comp plan. Watching for this in companies hiring for "hybrid GTM."
- **The agent layer** — when the first prospect interaction is with an in-product agent, the marketing function and the product function are owning the same surface. Whoever owns the agent prompt owns the funnel.
- **Voices:** @businessbarista (knowledge-layer framing), @aakashgupta (AI product strategy + funding signals), @fuckgrowth (distribution supercycle), @gregisenberg (product gap identification), @andrewchen (workflow time allocation).

## What would change my mind

- Org charts re-stiffen as companies hit a complexity ceiling on hybrid roles and revert to functional specialization (would suggest convergence is a phase, not a permanent shift).
- AI capability stalls long enough that the production cost collapse stops being the forcing function (less likely but worth holding).
- Distribution moats turn out to be cheaply portable — sophisticated AI-native challengers can match incumbent distribution in <12 months.
- Sales-as-operator-advantage gets eaten by automated sales agents fast enough that "operator who sees convergence" becomes a less valuable career position than "operator who builds the agent."
