# Content Sourcing — Baby Facts V2

Reference for manual step 1 (finding high-performing posts to repurpose). Keep this open when scrolling IG/TikTok for source material.

## Format fit

A post is a good candidate if **all four** are true:

- **≤10 seconds** of actual voiceover content (~25–35 words max)
- **Single-person delivery** — no two-person demos, no baby-in-frame required
- **No technique demo required** — pure verbal explanation works
- **One fact, one beat** — not a list, not "5 things moms need to know"

## What to skip

Things that *look* like our format but break it:

- Anything titled "how to ___" (swaddle, burp, latch, bottle-feed, soothe) — needs demo
- Product comparisons (need the product in frame)
- Before/afters, transitions, trending audio formats (our character can't do those)
- Multi-fact listicles ("3 things about newborns") — compress to the single strongest beat or skip
- Anything with overlaid text that's the actual content (we can't easily reproduce complex text animations)

## Content categories (with seed examples)

### 1. Surprising biology / development
Single-fact, "wait what?" energy. Perfect for this format.

- "A newborn's stomach at birth is the size of a cherry. That's why they eat every 90 minutes."
- "Babies recognize their mom's voice before they're born."
- "Your baby's brain doubles in size in the first year of life."
- "Babies are born with about 300 bones. Adults have 206."
- "Newborns don't produce tears for the first three to four weeks."
- "Babies can smile in the womb at 33 weeks."
- "A baby's heart beats almost twice as fast as an adult's — 120 to 160 bpm, all day, every day."

### 2. Myth busters
Takes a thing everyone does or believes and flips it.

- "You don't have to sterilize bottles after the first few weeks. The dishwasher is fine."
- "Breast milk actually changes composition at night to help babies sleep."
- "Hiccups don't hurt babies. They're a sign of a healthy diaphragm."
- "Babies don't need a bath every day. Twice a week is plenty."
- "Your baby's first poop will be black. It's called meconium, and it's normal."

### 3. "They don't tell you" (new parent reality)
Honest, slightly wry. Baseline Baby Facts voice.

- "No one tells you that newborns lose 10% of their body weight in the first week. That's normal."
- "Formula being expensive isn't a marketing thing. The FDA testing alone adds months to production."
- "The 'cluster feeding' phase at 3 weeks is real, and it's not your milk supply."
- "Baby acne peaks at about 4–6 weeks. It's hormonal, not hygiene."

### 4. Counter-intuitive science
Reflex / instinct / weird evolutionary stuff. Very high-scroll-stop.

- "Babies can hold their breath underwater by instinct until about six months old. It's called the mammalian dive reflex."
- "Newborns cry in different accents depending on where they're born. French babies cry with a rising pitch, German ones with a falling pitch."
- "Babies are born with a grip reflex strong enough to support their entire body weight. It goes away by 6 months."
- "Your baby's eyes don't produce their adult color until about 9–12 months."

### 5. "The reason why" (micro-explainer)
One phenomenon, one quick cause.

- "Babies sleep so much because their brain is literally rewiring itself every few hours."
- "The reason newborns have that weird soft spot? Their skull has to compress to fit through the birth canal."
- "Babies breathe through their nose almost exclusively for the first 6 months. That's why a stuffy nose is a big deal."

## Account-type shortlist

When you find a good one, follow the account and check their back catalog. Good signal:

- Pediatricians and pediatric RNs making "did you know" content
- Speech-language pathologists on child development
- Lactation consultants who do myth-buster posts
- NICU nurses sharing counter-intuitive facts
- OB/pregnancy educators on the baby-side facts

Avoid: lifestyle mom influencers (usually demos/routines), product accounts (sponsored), parenting "coach" accounts (usually multi-fact scripts).

## How to pick a winner

Scan a candidate post against this checklist:

- [ ] Voiceover or text is under ~30 words?
- [ ] One clear fact, one clear payoff?
- [ ] No demo required to make sense?
- [ ] No second person / no baby required in frame?
- [ ] Performing well (50k+ views, >3% engagement ratio)?

4/5 yes = good candidate. 5/5 = ship it.

## Where to log what we've used

Each produced video has `metadata.json` in its bundle folder with the `source_url`. If we start hitting the same source twice, the check is a grep across `videos/*/metadata.json`. No separate tracking needed until the library gets bigger.
