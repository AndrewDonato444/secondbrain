# Building Out Loud — Series Overview

## The Concept

Video series where Andrew builds in public — showing the tools, workflows, and thinking behind what he's creating. Morning walk videos on LinkedIn, 3-4x/week. Raw, real, conversational. No editing, no slides — just Andrew talking.

andrewdonato.com becomes a companion wiki: super simple, organized markdown plus resources. Watch a video, go to the site to go deeper.

## The Narrative

Andrew Donato is a 17-year sales leader who's constantly learning — tools, frameworks, habits, ways of thinking. He shares what he's picking up that might help other people. No pitches. No "look at what I built." Just honest, useful takeaways from someone who's in the trenches figuring things out.

**Positioning line:** "Things I'm learning that might help you."

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a pitch for Andrew's projects (don't name MSA, don't name specific products)
- Not an AI hype channel — AI comes up naturally when relevant, but it's not the thesis of every episode
- Not "look how smart I am" — it's "here's something I found useful, maybe you will too"

## Format

- Morning walk videos, shot on phone
- 60-90 seconds (LinkedIn sweet spot for video)
- No editing, no slides — just Andrew talking
- 3-4x per week
- Raw, real, conversational tone — not polished corporate content

## Content Pillars

### 1. Things I Learned the Hard Way
Sales lessons, management lessons, career lessons — stuff that took years to figure out. Frameworks, habits, mistakes. The kind of thing you'd tell a younger version of yourself.

### 2. Tools That Actually Changed How I Work
Not every new shiny thing. Tools Andrew has genuinely adopted and that made a real difference. Why they matter, how he uses them, who they're for. Honest — including what doesn't work.

### 3. How to Think About [X]
Mental models, ways of framing problems, questions to ask. Sales-adjacent but broadly applicable. "Here's a question that changed how I run discovery calls" or "here's how I think about prioritization."

### 4. What I'm Watching
Trends, shifts, things that are changing fast. Not hype — observable things that are already affecting how people work. AI comes up here naturally when it's relevant, but so does anything else worth paying attention to.

### 5. The Move
One specific thing you can do today. No prerequisites, no two-minute setup. The whole episode is the action — here's the thing, here's why it works, go try it. Shorter format. The closing question is "did it work?" not "what do you think?" Aimed squarely at sales reps, AMs, and sales leaders who want to leave the video having actually done something.

### 6. Steal My Prompt
A recurring mini-series. One prompt per episode — something Andrew actually uses. Show what it does, why it works, give it away in the first comment. No setup required, no tool install — works in ChatGPT or Claude out of the box. Format is always the same: here's the problem, here's the prompt, steal it. 2x/week cadence target. Launched Apr 21 with the "Voice of ___" episode (ep. 11) which is tracking to go viral. Backlog lives in episode master under `## Steal My Prompt — Backlog`.

## Posting Strategy

- Shoot in the morning on the walk
- Post by 8-9am ET (peak LinkedIn engagement)
- Always end with a question or a take that invites comments
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags (not more)
- No links in the post body (kills reach) — put links in first comment if needed

## Wiki Vision (andrewdonato.com)

- Markdown-based, minimal, fast
- Organized by topic (not just episode)
- Resources, links, tools mentioned in each video
- Authentic to how Andrew actually works

## How the Second Brain Feeds This

Every session, the brain accumulates new intake — tools, articles, insights, project updates. Each week, pull from what's new in the brain to generate that week's episode outlines. The brain is the content engine; the walks are the distribution.
