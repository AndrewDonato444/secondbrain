# Week 1 — Episode Outlines

## Episode 1: Wednesday April 9 — "I'm building my own second brain"
**Pillar:** Building in Public

**Hook (first 3 seconds):** "I'm building an AI system that knows everything about me — my projects, my goals, what I'm interested in — and I want to tell you why."

**Talking points (60-90 seconds):**
- I've been saving bookmarks for years and never looking at them again. Sound familiar?
- So I started building what people call a "second brain" — but not with Notion or some rigid organizer
- It's an AI-powered system where I throw stuff at it — links, articles, ideas, YouTube videos — and it figures out what matters and connects it to what I'm working on
- The magic isn't the filing. It's that the system knows my projects and goals, so when I save something, it tells me "this connects to that thing you're building" in ways I wouldn't have seen
- I'm building this in the open. I'll share what works, what doesn't, and what I learn
- Question to end: "How do you capture the stuff you find interesting? Bookmarks? Notes app? Or does it just disappear?"

**Why this works for the series:** It's the origin story. Sets up the "building in public" narrative. Relatable problem (bookmark graveyard). Teases future content.

## Episode 2: Thursday April 10 — "Sales leaders should be building products"
**Pillar:** AI × Sales (The Convergence Thesis)

**Hook:** "I've been in sales for 17 years. Last year I taught myself to code. Here's what that changed."

**Talking points:**
- The line between product and sales is disappearing. Fast.
- I started teaching myself to build with AI tools — Cursor, Claude — not to become an engineer, but because I realized the best salespeople in the next 3 years are going to be the ones who can also build
- I've now shipped a full intelligence platform, an automated content pipeline, and internal tools for my team
- The skills transfer both ways: understanding product makes me a better sales leader. Understanding sales makes me a better product builder.
- If you're in sales leadership and you're not experimenting with building, you're going to get left behind. Not by engineers — by other sales leaders who figured this out first.
- End with: "What's stopping you from building something? Seriously — I want to know."

**Why this works:** This is Andrew's thesis. It's provocative enough to get engagement. Establishes credibility in both worlds.

## Episode 3: Friday April 11 — "The $14 million question"
**Pillar:** AI × Sales / Tools & Discoveries

**Hook:** "One company saved $14 million by having AI handle 1.5 million customer conversations a year. Let that sink in."

**Talking points:**
- That's a real number from a real company using AI to automate the entire customer communication lifecycle — leasing, maintenance, renewals, billing
- This isn't future stuff. This is happening right now in real estate, healthcare, security
- What I find interesting isn't the technology — it's what it means for sales teams
- When AI handles the repetitive conversations, what's left? The high-value, complex, relationship-driven stuff. The stuff that actually requires a human who understands the customer.
- Sales isn't going away. But the sales roles that survive are the ones that look more like advisors than order-takers
- That's exactly why I built an intelligence platform for luxury real estate brokers — to make them advisors, not salespeople
- End with: "What part of your sales process should AI be handling that it isn't yet?"

**Why this works:** Big number hooks attention. Bridges Andrew's industry knowledge with a forward-looking take. Naturally mentions MSA without being salesy. The EliseAI stat makes it topical (Andrew doesn't name them — just uses the proof point).

## Episode 4 (optional Monday): "The feedback loop nobody builds"
**Pillar:** Sales Leadership Takes

**Hook:** "Most sales teams have no idea what's actually working. Here's the fix."

**Talking points:**
- I built an automated video pipeline that measures every single variable — hook type, video length, pacing, CTA style — and self-optimizes based on what performs
- Then I realized: why don't sales teams do this?
- Most orgs track win rate and quota attainment. That tells you who's winning, not WHY they're winning.
- The fix: decompose your best deals the same way you'd decompose your best content. What discovery questions did they ask? When did they establish trust? How did they handle the "I'll think about it"?
- If you can name the variables, you can test them. If you can test them, you can teach them.
- I've been thinking about applying this to coaching reps using AI on call transcripts — that's a project I'm working on
- End with: "Do you actually know why your top rep wins? Not 'they're good at relationships' — the specific things they do differently?"

**Why this works:** Bridges the tech world (analytics loop from Facts Unlocked) with the sales world (coaching and enablement). Practical and actionable. Sets up future content about the Gong automation idea.
