# Building Out Loud — LinkedIn Walk Series

## Format
- Morning walk videos, shot on phone
- 60-90 seconds (LinkedIn sweet spot for video)
- No editing, no slides — just Andrew talking
- 3-4x per week
- Raw, real, conversational tone — not polished corporate content

## The Narrative
Andrew Donato is a 17-year sales leader who taught himself to build AI products. He lives at the intersection of sales and product as those worlds collide. He's building things, learning in public, and sharing what actually works versus what's hype.

**Positioning line:** "What happens when a sales leader starts building."

## Content Pillars

### 1. Building in Public
What Andrew is working on — MSA, the Second Brain, the video pipeline. Not product pitches. The lessons, the struggles, the "here's what I learned this week." The audience sees the journey, not just the destination.

### 2. Sales Leadership Takes
17 years of B2B sales wisdom. Coaching frameworks, team building, the "new hires should sit in CS first" philosophy, how to actually use data to drive decisions. This is the bread and butter that gets engagement from the LinkedIn sales community.

### 3. AI × Sales (The Convergence Thesis)
Andrew's core belief: product and sales are merging. AI is the accelerant. What does that mean for sales leaders? For reps? For companies? He's not theorizing — he's living it. He introduced AI tools at TrackForce, he's building AI products on the side, he sees both worlds.

### 4. Tools & Discoveries
The interesting stuff Andrew finds — new AI tools, frameworks, articles. Curated through the Second Brain. "I found this thing this week and here's why it matters." Positions Andrew as someone who's plugged in and filtering signal from noise.

## Posting Strategy
- Shoot in the morning on the walk
- Post by 8-9am ET (peak LinkedIn engagement)
- Always end with a question or a take that invites comments
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags (not more)
- No links in the post body (kills reach) — put links in first comment if needed

## How the Second Brain Feeds This
Every session, the brain accumulates new intake — tools, articles, insights, project updates. Each week, pull from what's new in the brain to generate that week's episode outlines. The brain is the content engine; the walks are the distribution.
