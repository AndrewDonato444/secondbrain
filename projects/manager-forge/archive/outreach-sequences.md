## Sequence Selection Guide

**Use Angle 1 (Management Gap) for:**
- First-time managers, promoted from sales
- Managers showing isolation/struggling signals
- Any manager who might feel stranded
- Signal: Recent promotion, management language, team turnover hints

**Use Angle 2 (The Boss Problem) for:**
- Younger managers (millennials, Gen X) under older leadership
- Growth-stage orgs with generational management gaps
- Managers who know their boss's approach isn't working
- Signal: "Pushing hard" language, old-school leadership mentions, millennial team composition

**Use Angle 3 (Time ROI) for:**
- Growth-stage companies, obvious scaling
- Clear hiring signals
- Budget-conscious (want fast ROI)
- Signal: Expansion announcements, new roles posted

**Use Angle 4 (First-Time Manager) for:**
- Recent promotion signals from LinkedIn
- New manager hiring roles
- First-time founder/CEO signals
- Signal: "Manager" in recent postings, promotion timelines

**Use Angle 5 (Authority Play) for:**
- Established sales orgs with mature teams
- Decision-maker skepticism (need proof)
- Mid-market and enterprise
- Signal: Established, stable, looking for optimization not rescue

**Use Angle 6 (Feedback Angle) for:**
- ANY manager with the core pain (universal opener)
- Early adopters who love shaping products
- Relationships where you can be genuine about building
- Signal: Open-minded, collaborative orgs, founders/leaders who engage in product feedback
- **STRATEGIC USE:** Test with 30-50 leads across all ICP segments to validate resonance

## Testing Notes

- **A/B Test Subject Lines:** Curiosity-driven vs. direct vs. signal-specific
  - "why your best people leave" (curiosity)
  - "turnover and your team" (direct)
  - "saw you hired 3 new managers" (signal-specific)
  
- **A/B Test CTA:** Low-commitment vs. assumptive
  - "Worth a conversation?" (low-commitment)
  - "Quick call to see if it fits?" (assumptive)
  - "Open to a 15-minute look?" (specific)

- **Timing:** Send email 1 on weekday 8am-10am recipient timezone. Follow-ups: Tue/Wed/Thu for highest opens.

- **Volume:** Start with 50 leads per sequence. Once you have 100+ replies, scale to higher-performing angles.
