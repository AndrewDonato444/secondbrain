### WORKSHEET 1: [Name]

**Time:** [5–10 min]

[Fillable worksheet with clear prompts, not busywork]

**Example worksheet tone:**
> **"Your Emotional Archetype Assessment: Understanding How You Operate"**
> 
> When you're under pressure, what's your default move?
> 1. Slow down and analyze (Analytical)
> 2. Zoom out and look at the bigger picture (Global)
> 3. Push through and keep moving (Driving)
> 4. Check in with people and make sure everyone's aligned (Relational)
> 
> Write your answer: ___________
> 
> Now think about one rep on your team. What's THEIR default? ___________
> 
> Here's the trap: You probably tried to change them to match your style. What would happen if you met them where they are instead?

### SELF-ASSESSMENT: [Name]

**Time:** [5–10 min]

[Diagnostic exercise with scoring + interpretation]

**Example format:**
> **"Diagnostic: How Emotionally Available Are You Right Now?"**
> 
> Rate yourself 1–5 on each:
> - [ ] I have energy for a rep vent right now (1–5)
> - [ ] I understand where this rep is coming from (1–5)
> - [ ] I know what action to suggest (1–5)
> - [ ] I can hold a boundary without sounding cold (1–5)
> 
> **Score:** [interpretation based on total]
> - 4–10: You're running on fumes. Take 24h before major conversations.
> - 11–16: You're solid. Go coach.
> - 17–20: You're firing on all cylinders.

[Realistic dialogue + discussion prompts]

> **Scenario:** A top rep walks into your office and says: "I don't know how much longer I can do this. The leads are trash, I'm not making commission, and I feel like you're not fighting for me."
> 
> **Your job:** Respond in a way that (a) validates their concern, (b) doesn't absorb the blame, (c) moves toward action, (d) acknowledges the tension.
> 
> Write your response here:
> ___________
> 
> Now ask yourself:
> - Did I get defensive?
> - Did I ignore the emotion?
> - Did I own the outcome without owning the blame?
