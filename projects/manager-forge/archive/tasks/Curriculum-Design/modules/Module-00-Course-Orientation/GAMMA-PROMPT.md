# GAMMA PROMPT: Module Zero — Course Orientation & Introduction

```
You are creating an orientation deck for Manager Forge, a 13-week leadership training program for first-time SaaS sales managers.

MODULE ZERO is the gateway. It's not a deep-dive into any one topic. It's here to:
1. Welcome learners and establish psychological safety ("this is a judgment-free zone")
2. Explain what Manager Forge is and why it exists
3. Map out the full 13-module curriculum (show them the journey ahead)
4. Set expectations for what they'll learn and what they'll do
5. Connect the program to their real life (the problems they're actually facing)
6. Build confidence that they can do this

---

## DECK STRUCTURE (5 Sections, ~20-22 slides)

### SECTION 1: WELCOME & CONTEXT (~4 slides)
**Objective:** Land the tone; establish that this is different from typical leadership training.

**Slide 1: Title Slide**
- Title: "Manager Forge"
- Subtitle: "The Leadership Program for First-Time Managers Who Actually Want to Get Good"
- Tone: Direct, slightly irreverent (not corporate-y)

**Slide 2: The Problem You're Solving**
- Header: "You Just Got Promoted. Nobody Told You What That Actually Means."
- Points (bullet format, conversational):
  - You crushed your quota as an individual contributor
  - You got promoted because you're good at selling
  - And now you're supposed to be good at managing
  - These are not the same thing
  - Also: nobody warns you about emotional dumps, managing upward, or how to build a rhythm
- Tone: "I've been in your seat—this is what I wish I knew"

**Slide 3: Why This Program Exists**
- Header: "There's a Better Way to Learn Management Than Failing Publicly"
- Points:
  - Most management training is generic ("5 Steps to Better Delegation")
  - This program is built from 15+ years of real sales management experience
  - It's battle-tested in SaaS, luxury real estate, and high-performance sales environments
  - It's not soft skills theater—it's practical frameworks you can use Monday morning
  - It's designed for YOUR situation: 0-12 months in role, leading a team, hitting numbers AND developing people

**Slide 4: What You'll Actually Get**
- Three core promises:
  1. **Emotional leadership tools** — Managing your emotions AND your team's emotions (they're connected)
  2. **Information flow mastery** — How to translate upward without losing credibility or throwing your team under the bus
  3. **Operating rhythm** — A repeatable cadence that creates visibility and prevents crises

---

### SECTION 2: THE FULL CURRICULUM (~9 slides)
**Objective:** Show the complete journey; build confidence that the program is comprehensive; make it clear this isn't one-off topics.

**Slide 5: The 13-Module Journey**
- Header: "Here's Everything You're Going to Learn"
- Subheader: "Organized in 5 sections, building on each other"
- Visual: A simple 5-column layout showing the sections

**Slide 6: Pillar 1 — Foundation & Mindset (Modules 1–4)**
- Title: "Get Your Head Right"
- Modules (concise, benefit-driven):
  - **Module 1: Emotional Leadership** — Understanding emotional contagion, managing dumps, psychological safety
  - **Module 2: Managing Up** — How to translate for leadership without eroding team trust
  - **Module 3: Difficult Conversations** — Firing, performance plans, critical feedback done with dignity
  - **Module 4: Identity Shift** — The grief of letting go of being an individual contributor
- Insight line: "These four modules are the foundation. Get this right, everything else is easier."

**Slide 7: Pillar 2 — Rhythm & Operations (Modules 5–7)**
- Title: "Build Your Operating System"
- Modules:
  - **Module 5: Operating Rhythm** — Weekly 1:1s, team meetings, monthly reviews, early signal detection
  - **Module 6: Effective 1:1s** — The most underestimated coaching tool
  - **Module 7: Pipeline Review & Forecasting** — Using pipeline as your #1 visibility and coaching tool
- Insight line: "Consistency beats perfection. These modules show you how to create visibility without chaos."

**Slide 8: Pillar 3 — Coaching & Growth (Modules 8–9)**
- Title: "Develop Your People"
- Modules:
  - **Module 8: Coaching Reps to Results** — Structured improvement, coachability assessment, when to exit
  - **Module 9: Hiring & Team Building** — Recruitment, onboarding, building balanced teams
- Insight line: "You can't want someone's success more than they want it. Learn to recognize it and act."

**Slide 9: Pillar 4 — Culture & Scaling (Modules 10–11)**
- Title: "Lead for Sustainability"
- Modules:
  - **Module 10: Motivation & Culture** — Building genuine culture, removing demotivators, intrinsic motivation
  - **Module 11: Time Management & Scaling** — Delegation, automation, preventing burnout (yours and theirs)
- Insight line: "You're not just managing this quarter. You're building something that lasts."

**Slide 10: Pillar 5 — Long-Term Leadership & AI (Modules 12–13)**
- Title: "Build for the Future"
- Modules:
  - **Module 12: Long-Term Leadership Growth** — Career trajectory, executive presence, mentors and sponsors
  - **Module 13: The AI Manager** — Understanding AI's role in management, where humans still win, leading through AI transformation
- Insight line: "You're preparing for a 5+ year career. The world is changing. Be ready."

**Slide 11: Three Threads That Run Through Everything**
- Header: "You'll Notice These Three Themes in Every Single Module"
- The three threads (with brief explanation):
  1. **Emotional dumps** — Your reps will vent. You need a framework for handling it without absorbing it or dismissing it.
  2. **Information flow** — You're translating between your team and leadership. This skill makes or breaks your credibility.
  3. **Operating rhythm** — Consistency creates psychological safety and prevents crises. You'll use these frameworks in every module.

**Slide 12: Program Design**
- Header: "How This Works"
- Points (clear, practical):
  - One module per week (13 weeks total)
  - Each module has 5 sub-modules (~12-15 min each)
  - Watch the video async, then join the weekly Zoom discussion
  - Worksheets, role-plays, and discussion prompts to make it real
  - Everything is designed to be immediately applicable

**Slide 13: Why This Order? Why 13 Modules?**
- Header: "This Isn't Random. Here's Why We Built It This Way"
- Points:
  - Pillar 1 (Mindset) has to come first—if your head's not right, the rest doesn't work
  - Pillar 2 (Rhythm) comes next—you need systems before you can coach effectively
  - Pillar 3 (Coaching) follows—now you have the tools to actually develop people
  - Pillar 4 (Culture) is next—these are about sustainability and long-term thinking
  - Pillar 5 (Future) is last—these are advanced moves + preparing for what's next
- Insight: "You can't skip ahead. Trust the process."

---

### SECTION 3: WHAT YOU'LL LEARN TO DO (~5 slides)
**Objective:** Make it concrete. Show actual outcomes and scenarios they recognize.

**Slide 14: The Concrete Skills**
- Header: "By the End of This Program, You'll Know How To..."
- List (action-oriented, real scenarios):
  1. Handle a rep's emotional dump without absorbing their anxiety
  2. Give your leadership the forecast they need without throwing your team under the bus
  3. Have a difficult conversation (firing, performance plan, comp change) with dignity
  4. Build a 1:1 structure that creates accountability without shame
  5. Coach a struggling rep toward improvement (and know when they're uncoachable)
  6. Design your week so you're not drowning in meetings
  7. Hire for coachability, not just experience
  8. Read a pipeline and know where your team is really at (not where they tell you they are)
  9. Build a culture in a remote/hybrid team that isn't theater
  10. Navigate AI in your role—understand it, lead through it, stay ahead

**Slide 15: This Is Not Therapy. This Is Not Soft Skills Theater.**
- Header: "What This Program Is (And Isn't)"
- Column 1 — What it IS:
  - Practical frameworks you can use immediately
  - Real scenarios from real managers
  - Tools for emotional intelligence that drive results
  - Honest about what's hard and why
  - Grounded in sales (not generic corporate training)
- Column 2 — What it ISN'T:
  - Motivational speeches
  - Generic "5 Steps" leadership advice
  - Therapy or coaching certification
  - Theory without application
  - Disconnected from your reality

**Slide 16: Common Scenarios You'll Navigate**
- Header: "Real Situations You're About to Face"
- Scenario 1: "My rep just told me their whole life story and I don't know how to move forward"
- Scenario 2: "My boss asked me to cut our territory and my team is going to lose $50K in commission. How do I tell them?"
- Scenario 3: "One of my reps is clearly checked out. I think they might leave. Should I coach them or prepare for the hire?"
- Scenario 4: "My team just missed quota by 15% and I have to report it upward. What do I say?"
- Scenario 5: "I just realized I'm in back-to-back meetings all day and I'm not getting anything done. What do I cut?"
- Scenario 6: "My rep is using AI to shortcut their work. How do I address that?"
- Callout: "Every single one of these has a framework in this program."

---

### SECTION 4: HOW TO USE THIS PROGRAM (~3 slides)
**Objective:** Set expectations; make success clear; reduce friction.

**Slide 17: The Weekly Rhythm**
- Header: "Here's What Your Week Looks Like"
- Timeline:
  - **Monday-Friday:** Watch the module videos (5 videos, ~12-15 min each; ~1.5 hours total)
  - **Complete:** The worksheets and self-assessments (optional but recommended; ~45 min)
  - **Thursday/Friday:** Zoom discussion call (60 min, live, all cohort together)
  - **Total time commitment:** ~2-3 hours per week
- Tone: "This is doable. You're busy. We know. This is built for you."

**Slide 18: You're Not Alone**
- Header: "15+ People Are Learning This Right Now"
- Points:
  - You're in a cohort with other first-time managers
  - The Zoom discussions are where the real learning happens (you'll hear their stories, share yours, practice together)
  - Facilitator is [Name], who's been in your exact seat
  - No judgment. Everyone here is figuring it out.
- Tone: "This is a safe container. Show up authentically."

**Slide 19: Success Looks Like This**
- Header: "At the End of 13 Weeks, You'll Be Able To..."
- Points:
  - Run a 1:1 that actually produces a coaching moment (not just status updates)
  - Have a difficult conversation without your stomach in knots
  - Forecast with confidence because you understand your pipeline deeply
  - Coach a struggling rep toward improvement or toward the exit
  - Design a week that lets you lead (not just manage)
  - Know what to do when your rep emotionally dumps on you
  - Understand AI and lead your team through it
- Tone: "You're going to get better. Visibly better. People will notice."

---

### SECTION 5: LET'S GO (~2 slides)
**Objective:** Close strong; build momentum; make the first step feel exciting, not overwhelming.

**Slide 20: Your First Assignment**
- Header: "Before Module 1, Do This One Thing"
- Single assignment (low friction):
  - Think about the past week as a manager
  - Write down one situation where you felt lost or out of your depth
  - Write down one situation where you handled something well
  - Bring these to the first Zoom discussion (you'll share if comfortable)
- Tone: "This is just to get you thinking. That's it."

**Slide 21: You're About to Get Really Good at This**
- Header: "Welcome to Manager Forge"
- Closing message (conversational, encouraging):
  - You don't have to figure this out alone
  - You don't have to learn by failing publicly
  - There's a better way
  - This is it
  - Let's go
- Tone: Direct, confident, warm—like a mentor you trust

---

## DESIGN NOTES FOR GAMMA

**Visual Style:**
- Clean, modern, professional but warm (not corporate)
- Typography: Use a mix of bold headlines and conversational body copy
- Color: Blue/teal as primary (trust, calm), with accent color for callouts
- Images: Use real manager moments (photos of people in meetings, one-on-ones, etc.)—authenticity over stock photos
- Layout: Mostly text + visuals; avoid death-by-bullet-points (use short, scannable language instead)

**Tone in Slide Copy:**
- Direct and honest (not cheerleading)
- Conversational (like you're talking to a friend)
- Slightly irreverent (it's okay to be a bit edgy; this isn't typical corporate training)
- Vulnerable where appropriate (acknowledge that this is hard; you've been there)
- Confident (this program works; we know what we're doing)

**What Makes This Module Zero Different:**
- It's not another boring orientation. It's a sales pitch for WHY this matters.
- It orients them to the entire journey, not just Week 1.
- It establishes the tone and promise of the program (practical, honest, real).
- It builds psychological safety ("you're not alone, this is normal").
- It makes the first week feel exciting, not overwhelming.
- It acknowledges the AI-shaped future (Module 13) without making it scary.

---

## DELIVERABLES

**Output:** A deck that's ready for live presentation OR async viewing
- Slides should be readable (big text, clear hierarchy)
- Speaker notes for each slide (if you're presenting live)
- A clean PDF that learners can download

This is Module Zero. When it's done, learners should feel:
1. **Understood** — "She gets my situation"
2. **Hopeful** — "I can actually learn how to do this well"
3. **Oriented** — "I know what's coming, and I'm ready"
4. **Safe** — "This is a space where I can be honest about struggling"
5. **Excited** — "This is going to change how I lead"

Now go build it. Make it great.
```
