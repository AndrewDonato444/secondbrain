## ROLE-PLAY 1.1: The Rep Who Says "You Don't Get It Anymore"

**Scenario:**

You're three months into your role as manager. You were promoted from within, so you were peers with your team just a few months ago. One of your top reps, Jordan, pulls you into a 1:1 and says:

> "Look, I respect you, but I don't think you get what it's like out here anymore. You're in meetings all day. You're not on the phones. You don't feel the pressure we're under. It's easy for you to tell us to 'just hit our numbers,' but you're not the one doing it."

---

### Your Task:

**Respond in a way that:**
1. Validates Jordan's concern (don't dismiss it)
2. Clarifies your role as manager (you're not supposed to be on the phones anymore)
3. Rebuilds trust without getting defensive
4. Moves the conversation toward how you can actually help

### Coaching Notes (Review After Writing Your Response):

**What to avoid:**
- Getting defensive ("I'm working hard too!")
- Dismissing their concern ("You'll understand when you're a manager")
- Trying to prove you still "get it" by jumping back into selling

**What to aim for:**
- Acknowledge the reality: "You're right—I'm not on the phones anymore. That's a shift."
- Clarify your value: "My job now is to help you succeed, not to do your job for you."
- Ask what they need: "So let's talk about what's actually making it hard. What can I do to help?"

### Coaching Notes:

**Good coaching questions:**
- "Walk me through where you're stuck."
- "What's the biggest objection you're facing?"
- "If you could close this deal, what would you need to do?"
- "What have you already tried?"

**Good coaching advice:**
- Share a similar experience you had and what you learned
- Offer to role-play the call with them (you play the prospect)
- Give them one specific thing to try, then check in after

**Bad coaching:**
- "Let me hop on the call and show you how it's done."
- "Here's exactly what you need to say." (without letting them process it)
- Taking over the deal entirely
