#### WORKSHEET 4.2: How I'll Respond When I See a Signal (Action Plan)

**Script out your response. Practice it. Use it.**

**WHEN YOU NOTICE A SIGNAL:**

**Step 1: Identify it (Be specific)**
"I noticed [specific behavior/change] in [context]."

Examples:
- "You've been quieter in team meetings the last two weeks."
- "Your pipeline moved from 3 stage-4 deals to 1."
- "You usually joke around. Last 1:1, you didn't say much."

**Step 2: Ask with gentle curiosity (Create space)**

Your script: "I noticed [signal]. Is everything okay? Like, outside of work, too?"

Do NOT say: "You seem off." or "Something's wrong, isn't it?"
Do NOT accuse: "You're not pulling your weight."
Do NOT fix: "Here's what you should do..."

**Step 3: Listen (Don't fill the silence)**

They might say:
- "Yeah, I'm fine, just a lot on my plate."
- "Actually, [personal thing] is going on."
- "I don't know, I'm just in a slump."

Your job: Listen. Ask follow-ups. Don't try to solve it.

- "Tell me more."
- "How long has that been going on?"
- "What would help?"
- "Do you need anything from me?"

**Step 4: Support (Show you care)**

If it's personal: "I appreciate you telling me. That doesn't affect how I see you. Let's figure out what you need."

If it's work-related: "Let's talk about how to tackle this. You're not alone here."

**Step 5: Follow-up (Prove consistency)**

- Note it in your calendar: "[Rep] behavior signal noted [date]. Follow-up: [date]."
- In two weeks, check in: "How are things now? That situation better?"
