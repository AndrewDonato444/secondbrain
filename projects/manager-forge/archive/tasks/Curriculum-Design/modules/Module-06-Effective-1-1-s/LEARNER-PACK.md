## SECTION C: ROLE-PLAY SCENARIOS (5 detailed scenarios)

### SCENARIO 1: The Guarded Rep — How Do You Create Safety?

**Setup:**
You manage a rep named Alex. Alex has been on the team for 4 months. He's decent at activity—15–17 conversations a week—but he rarely brings problems or asks for help. In 1:1s, he gives surface-level answers. "Things are good." "I'm handling it." "No concerns." You suspect he's more guarded than he needs to be.

**Your Goal:**
Create enough safety in this 1:1 that Alex starts opening up.

**The Conversation:**

**You:** "Alex, how's it going?"

**Alex:** "Good. Busy. Same old stuff."

*[Pause. You resist the urge to interrogate. You're genuinely curious.]*

**You:** "Tell me about this week. What stood out?"

**Alex:** "Just the usual. Some good leads, some that didn't pan out. I'm managing."

*[You notice his tone is flat. His energy is fine but not engaged. He's not asking you anything.]*

**Debrief Questions (Reflect After the Role-Play):**

1. What signal were you hearing in this conversation?
2. What did you do (or what would you do) to deepen safety?
3. Did you listen or interrogate?
4. What vulnerability or humanity could you have shared to open the door?
5. How would you know if your approach worked?

---

### SCENARIO 2: Coaching a Performance Gap — Work Through the Conversation

**Setup:**
You manage a rep named Sam. Sam's close rate has dropped from 32% to 24% over the last month. Your activity is solid, but the conversion isn't there. You've noticed it and need to coach him through it.

**Your Goal:**
Use the Coaching Architecture to help Sam identify the gap and commit to change.

**The Conversation (You Lead):**

**You:** "Sam, I want to talk about something I'm noticing. Your close rate is at 24%. That's down from your normal 32%. Help me understand what's happening."

**Sam:** [Pause] "Yeah, I know. I've been off. Just not connecting with buyers like I usually do."

*[He's aware. Now you need to understand.]*

**Debrief Questions:**

1. What's your next question using the Coaching Architecture?
2. How do you help Sam reflect on his own pattern?
3. When does Sam shift from "the market is tough" to "I need to change something about my approach"?
4. What specific action would you ask him to commit to?
5. How would you follow up?

---

### SCENARIO 3: Handling an Emotional Dump — What Do You Say?

**Setup:**
You're in a 1:1 with a rep named Jordan. Midway through, Jordan mentions that his dad had a health scare. He's been worried, not sleeping well. He doesn't know how to balance taking care of his family and focusing on work. He's scared and he's brought it to you.

**Your Goal:**
Honor his trust while setting a clear boundary about your role.

**The Conversation (You Lead):**

**Jordan:** "My dad had a heart attack last week. They caught it early, but I've been freaking out. I don't know how to focus on work when my family is going through this."

*[You feel the weight of this. You care about Jordan. You also know you're not equipped to be his counselor.]*

**Debrief Questions:**

1. What's your first response? (Listen, don't fix.)
2. How do you validate his experience without becoming his therapist?
3. When do you shift to work support? ("Is there anything work-wise that would help?")
4. How do you set the boundary? ("You need real support for this.")
5. How do you end this conversation with Jordan feeling supported and clear?

---

### SCENARIO 4: Following Up on Something They Said — How Do You Notice the Gap?

**Setup:**
You're in a 1:1 with a rep named Casey. Casey mentioned last week that she was thinking about her next career move—maybe going into management or maybe going to a different brokerage. This week, she's avoiding the topic. When you gently asked, she said, "Oh, I'm just focused on this month. Forget I mentioned it." But something shifted. Her energy is different. She's quieter.

**Your Goal:**
Notice the gap and ask the right question to understand what's actually happening.

**The Conversation (You Lead):**

**You:** "Casey, I noticed something. Last week you were thinking out loud about career moves. This week it feels like that's off the table. What shifted?"

**Casey:** [Hesitates] "I don't know. I just... I didn't want to distract you. And I'm still figuring it out."

*[You hear the hesitation. There's more here.]*

**Debrief Questions:**

1. What signal are you hearing in her hesitation?
2. How do you listen for what's NOT said?
3. What would happen if you didn't notice this gap?
4. What's the impact of following up on something unsaid?
5. How would you help Casey feel safe to bring her real thinking?

---

### SCENARIO 5: Hard Conversation About Low Performance — How Do You Stay Data-Focused?

**Setup:**
You manage a rep named Riley. Riley is at 55% of quota. This is month 3 at this level. You've had soft conversations. "Just push a little harder." "I know you can do better." But nothing's shifted. Now you need to have a real accountability conversation with clear data, your diagnosis, and a specific ask.

**Your Goal:**
Have a hard conversation that's clear without being shame-based.

**The Conversation (You Lead):**

**You:** "Riley, I need to talk about your numbers. You're at 55% of quota. That's been the case for three months. I need to understand what's happening and what we're going to do about it."

**Riley:** [Defensive] "I'm doing the best I can. The leads aren't good."

*[He's going to the external blame. You need to stay data-focused without shaming.]*

**Debrief Questions:**

1. How do you respond without accepting the blame-shift?
2. What's your diagnosis? (Stay curious, not judgmental.)
3. How do you invite him to collaborate without letting him opt out?
4. What specific action would you ask him to commit to?
5. How do you make it clear this is serious without making him feel broken?

## SECTION D: WEEKLY ZOOM DISCUSSION PROMPTS (5 prompts, one per sub-module)

### ZOOM PROMPT 1: The Safety Foundation
**"Tell me about a 1:1 that actually worked. What made it different?"**

**Instructions for Facilitator:**
Ask 3–4 learners to share. Listen for what made safety possible. Was it consistency? Listening? Vulnerability? After each story, ask: "What layer of the Safety Pyramid was at work there?"

**Follow-Up Questions for Group:**
- What did the manager do differently?
- How did the rep respond?
- What would have happened without safety?
- How can you replicate this?

**Debrief:** Identify one thing everyone can do this week to strengthen safety.

---

### ZOOM PROMPT 2: Listening Like It Matters
**"Where do you struggle most in 1:1s?"**

**Instructions for Facilitator:**
This is vulnerability. Go first if you're leading. Share a real place where you struggle. Then open it up.

**Follow-Up Questions for Group:**
- Tell me about a time you missed a signal.
- What was the signal you didn't catch?
- How did you know you missed it?
- What would have happened if you'd caught it?

**Debrief:** Have everyone identify one signal they're going to listen for with one rep this week.

---

### ZOOM PROMPT 3: The Coaching Architecture
**"Has a rep ever dumped something personal on you? How did you handle it?"**

**Instructions for Facilitator:**
This combines Scenario 3 (emotional dumps) with Coaching (responding well). Ask for a real story. Then walk through: Did they listen? Validate? Offer work support? Set a boundary?

**Follow-Up Questions for Group:**
- Did you handle it well? Why or why not?
- If you could do it over, what would you do differently?
- How did your rep respond to your approach?
- What did you learn about your own boundaries?

**Debrief:** Practice the boundary sequence together. Go around the room and have everyone say out loud: "That's beyond my lane. I want you to get real support."

---

### ZOOM PROMPT 4: Emotional Dumps & Boundaries
**"What signal have you missed in a 1:1? How will you catch it next time?"**

**Instructions for Facilitator:**
This is about the Listening Framework and pattern recognition. Ask people to think about a rep who's not themselves and they didn't catch it (or caught it late).

**Follow-Up Questions for Group:**
- What were the four channels telling you?
- What's your baseline for this person?
- What shifted?
- How will you catch it sooner next time?

**Debrief:** Have everyone commit to knowing their reps' baselines. "How will you stay connected enough to notice when something shifts?"

---

### ZOOM PROMPT 5: Accountability Without Shame
**"Who needs a coaching conversation? What's your play?"**

**Instructions for Facilitator:**
This is the action prompt. Bring people from theory to practice. Who on their team has a real gap? What will they do?

**Follow-Up Questions for Group:**
- What's the gap you're seeing?
- What's your data?
- What's your diagnosis?
- What will you ask them to commit to?
- When will you have the conversation?

**Debrief:** Have everyone commit to one accountability conversation this week. Go around and have people say: "This week I'm going to have a coaching conversation with [rep] about [gap]."
