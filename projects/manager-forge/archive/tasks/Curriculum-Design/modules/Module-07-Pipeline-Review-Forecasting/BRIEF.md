# Pipeline Review & Forecasting — Module Brief

**Module Status:** Ready for Content Development  
**Module #:** 7 of 12  
**Position in Curriculum:** Core SaaS mechanics (follows Effective 1:1s; precedes Coaching Reps)

---

## Executive Overview

**Pipeline Review & Forecasting** teaches the single most important metric in SaaS sales: the pipeline. This module is about understanding pipeline health, forecasting accurately, and using the pipeline as a coaching tool—not just as a number-generating exercise.

The core insight: **Pipeline is destiny. If your pipeline is healthy, you'll hit quota. If it's not, you won't. Your job is to know your pipeline better than anyone and to use that knowledge to coach reps and manage leadership expectations.**

---

## Core Teaching Objective: THE AHA MOMENT

**What we want managers to realize:**

The pipeline is not a number. It's a story. Every deal in your pipeline tells you something about your rep's ability to identify opportunities, qualify correctly, move through discovery, and close. By understanding the pipeline deeply, you can catch problems early and help reps improve.

**More specifically:**

1. **Forecasting is not guessing.** Real forecasting is: "Here's our qualified pipeline. Here's our historical conversion rate. Here's what could move. Here's my confidence." Most new managers either sandbag (call it low) or hype (call it high). Real forecasting is data + assessment.

2. **Pipeline health reveals rep capability.** A rep with 20 early-stage deals and no advanced deals = qualification problem. A rep with 10 deals all stuck in one stage = something's stuck. A rep with strong pipeline but low close rate = discovery or closing problem. The pipeline tells the story.

3. **Forecasting accuracy is a trust builder.** When you consistently call the real number (not sandbagged, not inflated), leadership believes you. When you miss, they lose confidence. Accuracy is your currency.

4. **You need to know why.** "We're at $400K pipeline" is worthless. "We're at $400K pipeline; historically we convert 25% which gives us $100K forecast; that's 20% below plan; the gap is because we lost two reps in Q1" is useful.

5. **Pipeline reviews reveal everything.** Rep's pipeline by stage, close rate, average deal size, sales cycle length—all of it tells you about their performance and capability. These reviews are your #1 coaching and management tool.

**By the end of this module, managers should feel:**
- Clear on pipeline metrics and what they mean
- Confident in their forecast (they can back it up with data)
- Equipped to read a pipeline and see problems
- Understanding that pipeline work is prevention, not reaction

## The Problem We're Solving

### The Mistake Most New Managers Make

New managers typically do one of two things with forecasting—both lead to credibility loss:

**Path 1: The Sandbagger**
"I'm going to call a conservative forecast so I can over-deliver and impress leadership."

What happens: Leadership thinks you've given up. They add headcount, increase territory, think you're not ambitious enough. You hit the number, and they think "of course you did, that number was easy." Then they rely on your conservative numbers for planning. When growth slows, they panic because they thought you had more upside. You lose credibility.

**Path 2: The Hyper**
"I'm going to call an aggressive forecast to show leadership I'm ambitious and driving growth."

What happens: You miss the number consistently. Leadership stops trusting your forecast. They stop relying on your input for planning. They micromanage you because they don't believe your numbers. You lose credibility worse.

### The Real Issue

**Accurate forecasting is both a science and an art.**

Science: "Here's the pipeline by stage. Here's the historical conversion. Here's the math."

Art: "But here's what I see happening. Here's what could accelerate. Here's what could stall. Here's my real confidence."

Most new managers don't have enough data history (as a manager) to do the science. So they guess. They sandbag or hype. Leadership loses trust. They start micromanaging. The manager's life gets harder.

The way out: **Be honest about what you know and don't know. Use the data you have. Update as you learn more. Build accuracy over time.**

## Key Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. **The Pipeline Stage Framework**

SaaS pipeline typically has 4-6 stages. You need to know what goes where and why.

**Stage 1: Prospect / Inbound**
- Someone who fits your ICP but no real conversation yet
- Your goal: Qualify or disqualify (don't let deals sit here)
- Rep's job: Determine if this is worth spending discovery time on

**Stage 2: Discovery**
- We've talked to them; they have a problem; we're exploring fit
- Your goal: Understand their goals, timeline, process
- Rep's job: Deep questions about their business
- Metrics: How long reps stay here (too long = discovery loop; too short = not thorough)

**Stage 3: Qualification / Exploration**
- We've confirmed they have a problem; we've shown fit; they're evaluating
- Your goal: Build consensus internally on their side
- Rep's job: Multiple stakeholders, addressing concerns, creating urgency
- Metrics: Number of stakeholders, consensus building, timeline clarity

**Stage 4: Advanced / Proposal**
- We've sent a proposal or demo; they're evaluating seriously
- Your goal: They're comparing us to competitors (if anyone)
- Rep's job: Focus on value, handle objections, move to close
- Metrics: Proposal date, timeline, decision criteria

**Stage 5: Closed Won / Closed Lost**
- Deal is done (won or lost)
- Learning: Why did it close? Why did it not?

**Why This Matters:**
- You can see where reps are struggling (stuck in stage 2 = discovery problem)
- You can see velocity (deals moving through stages too slow = friction)
- You can see quality (deals moving through too fast = not qualifying)

### 2. **The Forecasting Formula**

Real forecasting = Math + Assessment

**The Math:**
- Qualified Pipeline × Historical Close Rate = Forecast
- Example: $600K qualified × 25% = $150K forecast

**The Assessment:**
- What could move it faster? (special opportunity, good momentum, etc.)
- What could stall it? (economic news, competitive loss, champion change, etc.)
- What's your confidence? (90%, 70%, 50%?)
- Why?

**The Real Forecast:**
"Our qualified pipeline is $600K. Historically we convert 25%, which gives us a $150K forecast. Our confidence is 85% because A, B, and C. Upside if we accelerate 2-3 deals: $200K. Downside if we lose one big deal: $100K."

**Why It Works:**
- You're not guessing
- You have data to back it up
- You're showing you've thought about scenarios
- Leadership can plan around this

### 3. **The Pipeline Health Diagnostic**

A healthy pipeline tells you a rep is doing their job. An unhealthy pipeline tells you where they're struggling.

**Healthy Pipeline:**
- Deals in multiple stages (good mix)
- Moving through predictably (not stuck)
- Deals are sized appropriately (not a bunch of tiny deals or one monster deal)
- Close rate is consistent with historical average
- No surprises in forecast

**Unhealthy Pipelines:**

**Problem 1: Too Many Early-Stage**
- Lots of prospects, few advanced deals
- Signal: Qualification problem; rep is casting wide net, not digging
- Fix: Coaching on discovery and qualification questions

**Problem 2: Stuck in One Stage**
- Deals stopped moving; stuck in discovery or advanced
- Signal: Something's blocked (competitive, internal decision-maker change, budget issue)
- Fix: Ask what's stuck and why; help unstick

**Problem 3: Deal Sizes Wrong**
- Bunch of small deals or one mega-deal
- Signal: Territory issue (rep not going after right targets) or just luck
- Fix: Territory coaching or wait and see

**Problem 4: Low Close Rate**
- Historically 25%, now 15%
- Signal: Something changed (reps changed, market changed, product changed, or rep capability dip)
- Fix: Investigate what changed; coach accordingly

### 4. **The Conversion Rate Reality**

Every company/product/market has a conversion rate. You need to know yours.

**How to Calculate:**
- Take last 12 months of deals
- Count deals that went from qualified → closed (won or lost)
- Divide closed-won by total qualified
- That's your conversion rate

**Why It Matters:**
- You can forecast based on pipeline (not wishes)
- You can spot when it changes (signal something's up)
- You can coach reps who are below/above average

**Real Example:**
- Your company's conversion is 25%
- Rep A's conversion is 18% (below average, coaching opportunity)
- Rep B's conversion is 32% (above average, model, or outlier to understand)

### 5. **The Pipeline Review Meeting**

This is your #1 coaching and visibility tool. Do it weekly.

**The Structure:**

1. **Overall Pipeline (5 min):** What's the total? How does it compare to last week/month?
2. **By Stage (10 min):** How much in each stage? Any deals stuck?
3. **Close Rates (5 min):** Rep's close rate. How does it compare?
4. **Big Deals (10 min):** What are the >$X deals? What's the status? Timeline?
5. **New Deals (5 min):** Any new deals entered? Any disqualified?
6. **Forecast (5 min):** What's this rep's forecast for month/quarter?
7. **Coaching (10 min):** Any deals at risk? Any opportunities? What's the coaching?

**Why It Works:**
- Weekly cadence catches problems early
- Public review (team sees each other's pipelines, normalizes transparency)
- Coaching is natural (you see the problem in the data)
- Forecasting is accurate (you're reviewing weekly)

## Sub-Module Breakdown

### Sub-Module 1: The Pipeline Architecture (13 min)
**Objectives:**
- Help managers understand pipeline stages and what belongs where
- Teach them to read a pipeline and see problems
- Show how to use pipeline as a coaching tool

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Every deal should fit in a stage
- Deals that don't fit the stage = coaching opportunity
- Stuck deals = early signal
- Pipeline shape tells the story

**The Reading Practice:**
- Rep A: $600K total; $300K early-stage, $200K advanced, $100K close-won (healthy distribution)
- Rep B: $400K total; $350K early-stage, $50K advanced, $0K close-won (qualification problem)
- Rep C: $1.2M total; $200K early-stage, $800K advanced, $200K close-won (good momentum)

**Real Example:**
- Jamie's pipeline looked like Rep B: lots of early-stage, very little advanced
- Andrew reviewed and asked: "Walk me through how you're qualifying. Who are you talking to?"
- Jamie revealed: Talking to anyone interested, not digging on their actual needs
- Coaching: "Let's focus on prospects who actually have the problem. Skip the talkers."
- Result: Pipeline shifted to fewer, bigger, better-qualified deals

**Business Impact:**
- Early coaching leads to behavior change
- Pipeline improves faster
- Reps feel coaching is about pipeline, not personal

**Tension to Address:**
- "How much of each stage should I have?" (Depends on your sales cycle; track over time.)
- "What if a deal is on the edge between stages?" (Put it where it's most realistic.)

### Sub-Module 2: The Conversion Rate & Math (12 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach managers to calculate and track conversion rates
- Show how math becomes your forecast
- Help them spot when conversions change

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Conversion rate is your foundation for forecasting
- It's calculated from real data, not hopes
- It changes over time (that's data)
- When it changes, investigate why

**The Math:**
- Count qualified deals from 90 days ago
- See how many closed (won or lost)
- Closed-won ÷ Total closed = your conversion
- Apply that to current qualified pipeline = forecast

**Why It Matters:**
- You're not guessing
- You can spot anomalies (rep's conversion suddenly 35% instead of 25%)
- You can forecast confidently

**Real Example:**
- Company's conversion: 22% (calculated from 12 months of data)
- This month: $600K qualified × 22% = $132K forecast
- But you notice Rep A's conversion is 32% (outlier)
- Rep B's is 15% (below average)
- You coach Rep B on what's different; Rep A on what she's doing right

**Business Impact:**
- Forecasting accuracy improves
- Leadership trusts your numbers
- You get credibility

**Tension to Address:**
- "What if I don't have 12 months of data?" (Use what you have; recalculate as you learn.)
- "Doesn't conversion vary month to month?" (Yes, that's normal; look at trends, not individual months.)

### Sub-Module 3: The Accurate Forecast (14 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach managers to forecast with confidence (not sandbagging or hyping)
- Show the difference between real forecasts and guesses
- Help them build credibility with leadership

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Forecasting is science (the math) + art (your assessment)
- Accuracy over time = credibility
- Sandbagging and hyping are both credibility killers
- Be honest about what you know and don't know

**The Forecast Framework:**

"Our qualified pipeline is $600K. Based on historical conversion of 22%, our forecast is $132K. Our confidence is 80% because [reasons]. Upside scenario (if we accelerate 2 deals): $160K. Downside scenario (if we lose the big deal): $100K."

**Why It Works:**
- You're backing it up with data
- You're showing you've thought about scenarios
- You're admitting uncertainty (builds trust)
- Leadership can plan around this

**Common Mistakes:**
- Sandbagging: "I'll forecast $100K to impress leadership with over-delivery."
- Hyping: "I'll forecast $160K to show I'm ambitious."
- Guessing: "I think $130K. That feels right."

**Real Example:**
- Month 1: Andrew predicted $150K; actual was $142K (accurate)
- Month 2: Andrew predicted $140K; actual was $138K (accurate)
- Month 3: Andrew predicted $155K; actual was $151K (accurate)
- Leadership starts relying on his numbers; gives him autonomy

**Business Impact:**
- Leadership trusts you
- You get autonomy in decision-making
- You avoid micromanagement

**Tension to Address:**
- "What if I'm wrong?" (You will be sometimes. Accuracy over time is what matters.)
- "What if leadership wants a higher number?" (You give them the real number; if they want to project higher, that's on them.)

### Sub-Module 4: The Weekly Pipeline Review (14 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach the structure of an effective pipeline review
- Show how it surfaces coaching opportunities
- Help managers make it a standard cadence

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Pipeline review is your #1 coaching and visibility tool
- Weekly cadence allows early intervention
- You're not criticizing; you're reading the data
- Coaching flows naturally from what you see

**The Meeting Structure:**
1. Overall number and comparison to last week
2. Breakdown by stage (any deals stuck?)
3. Conversion rate (how's it comparing?)
4. Top 5 deals (what's happening? timelines?)
5. New deals and disqualifications
6. Rep's forecast for month/quarter
7. Coaching: any deals at risk? Any opportunities?

**How to Facilitate:**
- "Talk me through your pipeline. What do you see?"
- "This deal's been in advanced for 3 weeks. What's happening?"
- "Your close rate is 18%. What's different from last quarter when it was 24%?"
- "This deal didn't come in; what happened?"

**Real Example:**
- Weekly review with the team: Marcus's deal stuck in discovery for 2 weeks
- Andrew asked: "What's the sticking point?"
- Marcus: "They keep saying they need more stakeholder input but won't set up the meeting."
- Andrew: "What if you asked for the names of the stakeholders and sent them something directly?"
- Next week, stakeholder joined; deal moved
- Coaching in real-time on stuck deals

**Business Impact:**
- Deals don't surprise you (they're visible early)
- Coaching is natural
- Team learns from each other
- Forecast accuracy improves

**Tension to Address:**
- "Doesn't weekly review feel excessive?" (No, it's where problems surface.)
- "What if there's nothing to review?" (There's always something; ask questions.)

### Sub-Module 5: Pipeline Coaching in Practice (12 min)
**Objectives:**
- Show how to use pipeline data to coach reps
- Teach specific coaching conversations
- Help managers move from data to action

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Pipeline is your coaching map
- The data tells you what to coach
- Coaching is asking, not telling
- Follow-up is essential

**The Coaching Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Too Many Early-Stage Deals**
- Signal: Rep isn't qualifying; casting wide net
- Coaching: "Let's look at these prospects. Which ones actually have the problem we solve?" Help them see the qualification gap.
- Action: Focus on fewer, better prospects

**Pattern 2: Stuck Deals**
- Signal: Something blocked; rep not moving deal forward
- Coaching: "Tell me about this deal. What's the sticking point? What's in your control?"
- Action: Help them unstick it or disqualify it

**Pattern 3: Slow Cycle**
- Signal: Deals taking longer than historical average
- Coaching: "Your deals are taking 60 days average; used to be 40. What's changed?"
- Action: Identify friction; coach through it

**Pattern 4: Low Close Rate**
- Signal: Rep's close rate below team average
- Coaching: "Your close rate is 18%. Let's look at a deal that didn't close. What happened?"
- Action: Identify the missing skill; coach it

**Real Examples:**
- Andrew reviewed Sarah's pipeline; lots of small early-stage, no advanced
- In review: "Let's pick 3 of these prospects you think have real potential. Tell me about them."
- Sarah realized most weren't qualified
- Coaching shifted her qualification process
- Pipeline got healthier; close rate improved

**Business Impact:**
- Reps improve faster
- Pipeline improves
- Forecast accuracy increases
- Coaching feels natural (not forced)

**Tension to Address:**
- "How much coaching is too much?" (Coach what the pipeline shows; don't manufacture problems.)
- "What if a rep gets defensive about their pipeline?" (That's on them; you're reading data, not criticizing.)
