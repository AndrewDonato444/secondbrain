## SUB-MODULE 2: THE CONVERSION RATE & MATH

### Worksheet 2.1: Calculate Your Company Conversion Rate

**Objective:** Find your actual close rate from historical data.

**Instructions:** Gather data from the last 12 months of closed deals (won and lost).

```
CONVERSION RATE CALCULATION

Last 12 months of closed deals:
- Total deals closed (won + lost):     _____
- Total deals closed WON:              _____
- Total deals closed LOST:             _____

Overall Close Rate = Deals Won ÷ Total Closed
                  = _____ ÷ _____ = _____%

Example: 48 won ÷ 60 total = 80%
```

**Stage-by-Stage Breakdown (Optional but Recommended):**

```
STAGE                    | DEALS IN STAGE | ADVANCED/CLOSED | CONVERSION %
Prospect → Discovery     |      _____     |      _____      |     ____%
Discovery → Qualified    |      _____     |      _____      |     ____%
Qualified → Advanced     |      _____     |      _____      |     ____%
Advanced → Closed (Won)  |      _____     |      _____      |     ____%
Overall (Prospect → Won) |      _____     |      _____      |     ____%
```

**Reflection Questions:**
1. Is your overall close rate higher, lower, or in line with industry norms (SaaS 60-80%, Real Estate 40-60%)?
2. Where is your biggest drop-off (where do you lose the most deals)?
3. Has this rate been stable, or is it trending up or down?

## Scenario 2: Forecasting to Leadership

**Setting:** Quarterly forecast call with your VP Sales.

**Context:**
```
You are: A Sales Manager
Your team's qualified pipeline: $1.2M
Your team's close rate: 68% (12 months)
Deals at risk: $150K (buyer budgets held up)
Potential upside: $100K (two deals in Advanced moving fast)
Timing: Two reps are out on vacation next week (covers 10% of pipeline)
```

**Your Task:** Make your forecast and defend it.

**The Conversation:**
- VP asks: "What's your forecast for the quarter?"
- You need to provide: Base number, upside, downside, confidence level, reasoning

**Debrief Questions:**
1. What's your base forecast number and how did you calculate it?
2. What's your upside scenario? How confident are you in it?
3. What's your downside scenario? Why might it happen?
4. What's your confidence level (0-100%)? Why?
5. What could change the forecast between now and quarter-end?

**Sample Answer:**
1. Base: $1.2M × 68% = $816K. That's what the math says.
2. Upside: Two Advanced deals ($100K) moving fast = $100K extra. 60% confident. Total upside: $876K.
3. Downside: Buyer budgets at risk ($150K) slip, + vacation overlap ($50K) = $200K risk. Total downside: $616K.
4. Confidence: 75% on the $816K number. 15% I'm higher, 10% I'm lower.
5. If deals move as expected, I'll be at $876K. If they don't, I'll flag it by month 2.

## Scenario 5: Forecast Miss Debrief

**Setting:** End of quarter. You forecasted $800K. You closed $650K. VP asks for a debrief.

**Context:**
```
Forecast: $800K
Actual: $650K
Miss: $150K (off by 19%)
What Happened:
- Two deals slipped to next quarter ($100K)
- One deal was lost to competitor ($25K)
- One deal disqualified (no real budget) ($25K)
```

**Your Task:** Explain what happened and what you learned.

**The Conversation Setup:**
- VP asks: "Walk me through the miss."
- You need to: Explain what happened, own it, and say what you'll do differently

**Debrief Questions:**
1. Which deal slip was predictable and which wasn't?
2. What should you have flagged earlier?
3. What coaching could have prevented this?
4. What will you do differently next quarter?

**Sample Answer:**
1. The two slips: One was buyer budget held (predictable, I noted it as downside). One was unexpected legal hold-up (not predictable).
2. I should have flagged the legal risk earlier and escalated when it started.
3. For the lost deal, the rep could have qualified deeper on why they were losing. For the disqualified deal, we took it too early.
4. Next quarter: (1) I'm flagging any legal/procurement risk at day 5, not day 14. (2) I'm coaching tighter qualification in Qualification stage before Advanced. (3) I'm updating forecast every week, not just at month-end, so surprises are smaller.

## Quick Reference: Five Coaching Gaps

```
1. QUALIFICATION GAP: Early-stage heavy → "How do you decide when to move to Qualification?"
2. STUCK DEAL GAP: Deal in stage too long → "What's the next step? When are you talking to them?"
3. CYCLE TIME GAP: Moving too fast or slow → "How long does [stage] typically take?"
4. DEAL SIZE GAP: Too small/large/concentrated → "What's your typical deal size? Why?"
5. CONVERSION RATE GAP: Close rate below average → "When you lose, what's the reason?"
```
