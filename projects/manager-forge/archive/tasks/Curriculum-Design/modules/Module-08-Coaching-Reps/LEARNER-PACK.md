## SUB-MODULE 3: BEHAVIOR-FOCUSED COACHING

### Worksheet 3.1: Translate Vague Feedback Into Specific Behavior

**Instructions:** You have feedback to give a rep. Practice translating it from vague to specific.

```
THE VAGUE FEEDBACK I WAS GOING TO GIVE:
"You need to be more _______________"
or
"You're not _____________ enough"

Example: "You need to be more confident" or "You're not closing enough"

---

NOW LET'S MAKE IT SPECIFIC:

1. DESCRIBE THE CURRENT BEHAVIOR (What are they actually doing?)
   ___________________________________________
   (Be specific: "In calls, you..." or "On emails, you...")

2. WHY IS IT A PROBLEM? (What's the impact?)
   ___________________________________________
   (How does this behavior land on the customer? What result does it cause?)

3. WHAT'S THE NEW BEHAVIOR? (What will you ask them to do instead?)
   ___________________________________________
   (Observable action, not personality trait)

4. WHAT DOES IT SOUND LIKE? (Give exact language or example)
   ___________________________________________
   (If it's a conversation, write the script. If it's an action, describe it.)

5. HOW WILL WE MEASURE IT? (How will you know if they're doing it?)
   ___________________________________________
   (Count? Observation? Checklist?)

---

FINAL FEEDBACK STATEMENT (to give to rep):

"I noticed [describe current behavior]. That usually leads to [impact]. 

Instead, I want you to [new behavior]. Here's what that sounds like: [example].

Can you commit to trying that this week? On your next [calls/emails/meetings], I want you to [measurement]."

---

EXAMPLE COMPLETED:

VAGUE FEEDBACK: "You need to close more"

1. CURRENT BEHAVIOR: In calls, after you explain your solution, you say "Think about it and get back to me" and hang up

2. WHY IT'S A PROBLEM: The buyer doesn't feel moved to take action. They take it as your okay to delay indefinitely

3. NEW BEHAVIOR: After explaining, ask a direct question that requires a commitment

4. WHAT IT SOUNDS LIKE: "Based on what I've explained, I recommend we move forward. Does that work for you?" or "How does that sound?"

5. HOW WE'LL MEASURE: I'll listen to your calls. On Fridays, tell me: Did I ask for a commitment on every close? Yes/No

FINAL STATEMENT: "I listened to your calls. When you finish explaining your solution, you leave it open-ended. Instead, I want you to ask directly: Does that work for you? or How does that sound? That tells the buyer you're serious and you want their decision. This week, I want you to ask for the close on every call."
```

# B. SELF-ASSESSMENT TOOLS (with scoring rubrics)

## Assessment 1: Coachability Diagnostic

**Can you tell who's coachable? Test yourself with these scenarios.**

**Scenario A:**
A rep has missed quota for three months. You bring it up in a 1-on-1. They say:

"Yeah, I know. I've been struggling with my discovery questions. I don't think I'm asking the right things. Can you listen to a call and tell me what I'm doing wrong?"

**What does this signal?**
☐ Ownership ☐ Openness ☐ Action ☐ Accountability ☐ Progress

This rep is: ☐ Coachable ☐ Not coachable

---

**Scenario B:**
A rep has been with you for six months. They're closing at 15% (target is 22%). You ask them to spend more time on discovery. Next week, they say:

"I tried that discovery approach, but it doesn't work for my style. My close rate is actually down now."

When you ask to listen to calls, they say they've been busy and haven't recorded any.

**What does this signal?**
☐ Ownership ☐ Openness ☐ Action ☐ Accountability ☐ Progress

This rep is: ☐ Coachable ☐ Not coachable

---

**Scenario C:**
A rep's performance has dropped. They blame it on "leads being worse this month" and "the product not being competitive." You ask what *they* could do differently. They say:

"There's not much I can do if the leads are bad."

**What does this signal?**
☐ Ownership ☐ Openness ☐ Action ☐ Accountability ☐ Progress

This rep is: ☐ Coachable ☐ Not coachable

---

**Answer Key:**
- Scenario A: Ownership, Openness, Action, Accountability (likely Progress too) = **Coachable**
- Scenario B: Defensive, not implementing feedback = **Not coachable**
- Scenario C: Blame, no accountability = **Not coachable**

# C. ROLE-PLAY SCENARIOS (5 detailed scenarios)

## Scenario 1: Deliver Behavior-Focused Feedback

**Context:**
You've been coaching Sarah on her discovery questions. You listened to her last call. She asked 2 discovery questions before pitching. She should be asking 5. Her close rate is still 18%; target is 24%.

**Setup:**
You're in a 1-on-1 with Sarah. You just listened to her call from yesterday.

**Your Task:**
Give her specific behavioral feedback using this structure:
1. Describe what you observed
2. Explain why it matters
3. Give the new behavior
4. Show what it sounds like
5. Get her commitment

**Possible Sarah Responses (read these and respond naturally):**

- "Yeah, I know. I feel like I'm asking enough questions though."
- "Okay, but that makes the calls feel really long."
- "That makes sense. What was I doing wrong?"
- "I tried that last week and it didn't help."

**Debrief Questions (after you role-play):**
1. Did you describe a specific behavior or use vague language?
2. Did Sarah understand what to do differently?
3. How did you handle her pushback?
4. Would she likely try this between now and next week?

---

## Scenario 2: Present a PIP Conversation

**Context:**
Marcus has been coached for 90 days. His close rate hasn't improved past 16% (target: 22%). He's coachable but not improving—likely a capability issue. You've decided a PIP is necessary.

**Setup:**
You and Marcus, closed door. You have the PIP document prepared.

**Your Task:**
Have the PIP conversation. Include:
- Why you're putting him on PIP
- The specific standards he needs to hit
- The support you're providing
- The consequence if he doesn't improve

**Possible Marcus Responses (read these and respond):**

- "I thought coaching was working. Why a PIP?"
- "Can I get more time? 60 days seems short."
- "I need better support. What exactly are you offering?"
- "Okay. I understand."

**Debrief Questions:**
1. Did you explain the gap clearly?
2. Did Marcus understand the standards and timeline?
3. Did he ask questions? How did you handle them?
4. At the end, was he clear on what comes next?

---

## Scenario 3: Exit Conversation

**Context:**
Derek has been coached for 90 days. He's not improving. You put him on a PIP 60 days ago. He hit the first two weeks, then dropped back. By week 4, it was clear he wouldn't make the standards. You've decided to exit him.

**Setup:**
You, Derek, and HR (if applicable). You have his final paycheck and severance information ready.

**Your Task:**
Have the exit conversation. Include:
- Acknowledge his efforts
- Explain the decision clearly (not opening it for negotiation)
- Provide severance/transition info
- End with dignity

**Possible Derek Responses (read these and respond):**

- "This doesn't seem fair. I was trying."
- "Can I have another chance?"
- "When is my last day?"
- [Silence/emotional response]

**Debrief Questions:**
1. Did you stay calm and clear?
2. Did you over-explain or justify?
3. Did you allow it to become a negotiation?
4. How would Derek feel leaving this conversation?

---

## Scenario 4: Coachability Assessment Interview

**Context:**
You have a new rep who's struggling (low pipeline, missed their first month). You need to assess whether they're coachable before you commit to 90 days of coaching.

**Setup:**
1-on-1 with the rep. You're asking discovery questions to assess coachability.

**Your Task:**
Ask questions to determine:
- Do they see the gap? (Ownership)
- Are they open to feedback? (Openness)
- Will they try new things? (Action)
- Can they track their own progress? (Progress)
- Do they take responsibility? (Accountability)

**Sample Questions to Ask:**
- "What's your take on how things are going?"
- "Do you see where you're falling short?"
- "What do you think is causing that?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What would you be willing to do differently?"
- "How would you know if you were improving?"

**Possible Rep Responses (react naturally to these):**

- "Honestly, I'm not sure what's going wrong. I'm working hard."
- "It's the leads. They're not good quality."
- "I don't really see a gap. I think I need more time."
- "I'm open to anything. What do you think I should do?"
- "Yeah, I think I'm not asking enough discovery questions. That makes sense."

**Debrief Questions:**
1. What signals of coachability or un-coachability did you hear?
2. Was the rep honest about themselves?
3. Did you push back on excuses, or accept them?
4. Based on this conversation, would you coach them?

---

## Scenario 5: Week 6 Check-In (Coaching Progress)

**Context:**
You're in Week 6 of coaching Lisa. The focus has been "Ask discovery questions before pitching." She's been doing pretty well—she asked 5 questions in her last call, up from 2-3 per call in Week 1.

**Setup:**
Weekly 1-on-1. You listened to her calls from this week.

**Your Task:**
- Acknowledge her progress
- Point out where she's doing it right
- Identify one area where she slipped
- Help her figure out what to do differently
- Get commitment for next week

**Possible Lisa Responses (react naturally):**

- "I feel like I'm doing better. Did you notice?"
- "I had one call where I asked a ton of questions and didn't pitch anything."
- "I'm still not sure when to stop asking and start pitching."
- "This feels awkward. People are probably annoyed by all the questions."

**Debrief Questions:**
1. Did you celebrate the progress?
2. Did you point to specific calls and examples?
3. Did you help her identify her own patterns?
4. Did you push on the "feeling awkward" concern or accept it?
5. Is she likely to keep doing this next week?

# D. WEEKLY ZOOM DISCUSSION PROMPTS

**Use these in your group coaching sessions. Share real stories.**

---

## Week 1 (Post Sub-Module 1: Coachability)

**"Who on your team is struggling right now? Are they coachable?"**

In your Zoom call:
- Go around the room. Each person names one struggling rep and rates their coachability (1-5).
- Pick the person with the lowest coachability score. Ask that manager: "What makes you say they're not coachable?"
- Listen for specific signals: Do they blame? Are they defensive? Are they not implementing feedback?
- Discuss: What would happen if you spent 90 days coaching them anyway?

**Discussion prompt:** "Who on your team have you been coaching for more than 90 days without improvement? What's holding you back from making a decision?"

---

## Week 2 (Post Sub-Module 2: 60-90 Day Plan)

**"What's one behavior change you're coaching this week? How specific is it?"**

In your Zoom call:
- Each person shares one specific behavior they're coaching right now.
- Group rates it: Is it specific enough? Can you measure it? Is it observable?
- Example: "I'm coaching her on being more assertive" = NOT SPECIFIC. "I'm coaching her to ask one clarifying question before responding to objections" = SPECIFIC.
- If someone's being vague, help them get specific using this formula:
  - Describe the current behavior
  - Explain why it's a problem
  - Give the new behavior
  - Show what it sounds like

**Discussion prompt:** "What's the hardest part of measuring behavior change? How do you know if your coaching is actually working?"

---

## Week 3 (Post Sub-Module 3: Behavior-Focused Coaching)

**"Have you ever held on to someone too long? What happened?"**

In your Zoom call:
- Each person shares a story (anonymously is fine) about when they kept coaching someone who should have been exited.
- What were the signs you missed or ignored?
- What happened to your team while you were focused on that one person?
- What did you learn?

**Discussion prompt:** "When did you finally let them go? How did the team react? In retrospect, should you have made the call sooner?"

---

## Week 4 (Post Sub-Module 4: The PIP)

**"What's the hardest part of having the PIP conversation?"**

In your Zoom call:
- Some managers fear being "too harsh." Others fear not being clear enough.
- What's the hardest part for you?
- Role-play: Have two managers practice the PIP conversation with each other. One plays the manager, one plays the rep.
- Debrief: Did the rep understand the gap, the standards, the support, and the consequence? If not, what was missing?

**Discussion prompt:** "Has anyone ever put someone on a PIP and seen them actually improve and stay? What made that work?"

---

## Week 5 (Post Sub-Module 5: Letting Go)

**"When have you had to let someone go? How did it affect the team?"**

In your Zoom call:
- This is the reflection call. People often feel vulnerable here.
- Create psychological safety. Go first if you're comfortable sharing.
- Ask: What were the signs you missed?
- What would you do differently next time?
- What surprised you about how the team reacted after you let them go?

**Discussion prompt:** "Looking back, do you think you made the decision too early or too late? Would you do anything differently?"
