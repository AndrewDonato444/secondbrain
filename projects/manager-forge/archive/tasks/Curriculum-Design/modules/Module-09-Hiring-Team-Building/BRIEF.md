# Hiring & Team Building — Module Brief

**Module Status:** Ready for Content Development  
**Module #:** 9 of 12  
**Position in Curriculum:** Team growth (follows Coaching Reps; precedes Motivation & Culture)

---

## Executive Overview

**Hiring & Team Building** teaches the most leveraged skill in management: building a team that can perform without you micromanaging them. This module covers hiring (how to assess talent), onboarding (how to ramp new reps fast), and team composition (how to build a balanced, resilient team).

The core insight: **Your team's performance ceiling is set by who you hire. Hire wrong, and you'll spend the next 9 months trying to coach someone who wasn't a fit. Hire right, and management becomes easier.**

---

## Core Teaching Objective: THE AHA MOMENT

**What we want managers to realize:**

Hiring is not about finding someone like you. It's about finding someone who can succeed in this specific role, in this market, with this product, on this team. Most new managers hire based on "gut feel" or "I liked them." Real hiring is structured: assessing capability, coachability, and fit—then onboarding them so they succeed fast.

**More specifically:**

1. **Hiring wrong is expensive.** A bad hire costs you 6-9 months (ramp time + coaching time + replacement time), territory that goes unmanaged, team morale (watching you tolerate underperformance), and leadership trust (they question your judgment). Hiring right is the highest-leverage decision you make.

2. **"Culture fit" is often bias.** Most managers hire people they like or people like them. That's not culture fit; that's comfort. Real culture fit is: "Can this person succeed here? Do they share our values? Will they make the team better?"

3. **Onboarding determines success.** A great hire with bad onboarding underperforms. An OK hire with great onboarding can exceed expectations. Your onboarding process—first 30-60-90 days—is as important as the hire itself.

4. **Team composition matters.** A team of 5 "Andrew clones" is fragile. You need diversity of strengths: hunters and farmers, analytical and relational, fast-movers and deep-thinkers. Balance creates resilience.

5. **You're hiring for coachability, not perfection.** No one is perfect on day one. The question is: Can they learn? Do they take feedback? Do they want to improve? Coachability beats experience most of the time.

**By the end of this module, managers should feel:**
- Equipped with a structured hiring process (not relying on gut feel)
- Clear on what to assess in interviews
- Confident in onboarding new reps (they have a 30-60-90 day plan)
- Understanding that team composition is strategic, not accidental

## The Problem We're Solving

### The Mistake Most New Managers Make

New managers typically make one of three hiring mistakes—all lead to regret:

**Mistake 1: Hiring Based on Likability**
"I really liked them in the interview. They're energetic and confident. They'll be great."

What happens: Likability ≠ capability. The rep is charming but can't close. Or they're confident but not coachable. You realize 3 months in that you hired the wrong person, and now you're stuck coaching someone who isn't a fit.

**Mistake 2: Hiring Clones of Yourself**
"They remind me of me. They're scrappy, they hustle, they have my energy."

What happens: You build a team of one personality type. When the market shifts or a different skill is needed, your whole team struggles because they all have the same strengths and weaknesses. You have no diversity of thought or approach.

**Mistake 3: No Structured Onboarding**
"They're experienced. They'll figure it out."

What happens: New rep flounders for 60 days because they don't know your product, your process, or your team's rhythm. They get frustrated. You get frustrated. They underperform or leave. You wasted a hire.

### The Real Issue

**Hiring and onboarding are structured processes, not gut-feel gambles.**

Most new managers don't have a hiring framework, so they rely on "I liked them" or "They felt right." They don't have an onboarding plan, so new reps are thrown into the deep end. Then they wonder why new hires struggle or leave.

The way out: **Structured hiring (assess capability and coachability). Structured onboarding (30-60-90 day plan). Strategic team composition (balance strengths).**

## Key Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. **The Hiring Assessment Framework**

You're hiring for three things: Capability, Coachability, and Fit.

**Capability:**
- Can they do the job? (skills, experience, aptitude)
- Have they done something similar before?
- Do they understand the sales process?
- Can they operate in this market/product complexity?

**Coachability:**
- Do they take feedback?
- Do they admit when they don't know something?
- Are they curious and open?
- Do they want to improve?

**Fit:**
- Do they share the team's values?
- Will they make the team better?
- Can they work in your operating rhythm?
- Do they want what this role offers?

**Why All Three Matter:**
- High capability, low coachability = talent you can't develop
- High coachability, low capability = they want to learn but can't perform
- High capability + coachability, low fit = they'll leave or disrupt the team

**The Right Hire:**
- Capable enough to succeed
- Coachable enough to improve
- Fits the team and role

### 2. **The Interview Structure**

Most managers "wing" interviews. Bad idea. Structure reveals capability and coachability.

**The Four-Part Interview:**

**Part 1: Experience Deep-Dive (20 min)**
- "Walk me through your last role. What were you selling? To whom? What was your quota? Did you hit it?"
- "Tell me about a deal you lost. What happened? What did you learn?"
- "Tell me about a deal you closed that you're proud of. What made it work?"

**What You're Assessing:** Can they articulate their process? Do they own their results? Are they reflective?

**Part 2: Skill Assessment (15 min)**
- "Let's role-play a discovery call. I'm the prospect. Show me how you'd run it."
- "How do you handle objections? Give me an example."
- "How do you prioritize your day? Walk me through yesterday."

**What You're Assessing:** Can they actually do the job? Do they have a process?

**Part 3: Coachability Check (10 min)**
- "Tell me about a time you got critical feedback. How did you handle it?"
- "What's something you're working on improving right now?"
- "What do you not know about this role that you'd need to learn?"

**What You're Assessing:** Do they take feedback? Are they self-aware? Are they open?

**Part 4: Fit & Motivation (15 min)**
- "Why this role? Why now?"
- "What do you need from a manager to succeed?"
- "What kind of team environment do you thrive in?"
- "What would make you leave a job?"

**What You're Assessing:** Do they want what this role offers? Will they fit the team culture?

### 3. **The 30-60-90 Day Onboarding Plan**

Onboarding isn't "figure it out." It's a structured ramp.

**Days 1-30: Learn**
- Product training (what we sell, to whom, why they buy)
- Process training (our sales process, CRM, cadence)
- Team integration (meet the team, shadow calls, learn rhythm)
- First deals (small, low-risk opportunities to practice)

**Goal:** By day 30, they understand the product, process, and team. They've closed 1-2 small deals.

**Days 31-60: Practice**
- Increase deal flow (more opportunities, bigger deals)
- Weekly coaching (on discovery, objection handling, closing)
- Pipeline building (prospecting, qualifying, moving deals)
- First quota target (50% of full quota)

**Goal:** By day 60, they're building pipeline and closing deals consistently. They're at 50-75% of quota.

**Days 61-90: Perform**
- Full deal flow (normal territory, normal quota)
- Less coaching, more accountability
- Self-sufficiency (they know how to operate)
- First full quota (100%)

**Goal:** By day 90, they're fully ramped and hitting quota independently.

**Why It Works:**
- Clear expectations at each stage
- Gradual ramp (not thrown into deep end)
- Coaching built in (not left to figure it out)
- Milestones are measurable

### 4. **Team Composition Strategy**

A balanced team is more resilient than a homogenous one.

**The Archetypes You Need:**

**Hunters:** Aggressive prospectors, love the chase, high energy, close fast
**Farmers:** Relationship builders, nurture long cycles, strategic, patient

**Analytical:** Detail-oriented, data-driven, thorough, slow to decide
**Relational:** People-focused, empathetic, consensus-building, fast to connect

**Fast-Movers:** Execute quickly, high velocity, action-oriented
**Deep-Thinkers:** Strategic, see patterns, thoughtful, slower but thorough

**Why Balance Matters:**
- Hunters open doors; farmers close complex deals
- Analytical reps prevent bad deals; relational reps build trust
- Fast-movers create momentum; deep-thinkers prevent mistakes

**The Trap:**
- Hiring 5 hunters = great prospecting, poor nurturing
- Hiring 5 farmers = great relationships, no new pipeline
- Hiring 5 Andrews = fragile team when market shifts

**The Goal:**
- Build a team with diverse strengths that complement each other

### 5. **The Coachability Red Flags**

In interviews, watch for these signals.

**Red Flag 1: External Blame**
- "I didn't hit quota because the leads were bad."
- "The product wasn't competitive."
- "My manager didn't support me."

**What It Signals:** They don't own results. They'll blame you, the product, the market.

**Red Flag 2: Defensiveness**
- You give feedback in the interview; they argue or justify instead of listening
- They don't admit gaps in knowledge
- They act like they know everything

**What It Signals:** They're not coachable. They'll resist your coaching.

**Red Flag 3: No Self-Awareness**
- "What are you working on improving?" "Nothing, I'm pretty good."
- "What's your weakness?" "I work too hard."

**What It Signals:** They're not reflective. They won't improve.

**Red Flag 4: Job-Hopping Without Growth**
- 4 jobs in 3 years, all for "bad managers" or "bad companies"
- No pattern of learning or progression

**What It Signals:** They'll leave when it gets hard. They don't stick.

## Sub-Module Breakdown

### Sub-Module 1: The Structured Hiring Process (14 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach the Capability-Coachability-Fit framework
- Show how to structure interviews
- Help managers assess candidates objectively

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Hiring wrong is expensive (6-9 months wasted)
- You're assessing capability, coachability, and fit
- Structure reveals what gut-feel misses
- Ask the same questions to every candidate (compare apples to apples)

**The Interview Framework:**
- Experience deep-dive (can they do the job?)
- Skill assessment (show me, don't tell me)
- Coachability check (do they take feedback?)
- Fit & motivation (do they want this role?)

**Real Example:**
- Candidate A: Loved them in interview (high energy, confident)
- Skill assessment: Couldn't articulate a sales process; winged the role-play
- Coachability check: Defensive when asked about areas to improve
- Decision: Pass (likability ≠ capability)

**Business Impact:**
- Hire the right people
- Avoid 6-9 months of regret
- Team performance improves

**Tension to Address:**
- "Doesn't structure make interviews feel robotic?" (No, it makes them fair and accurate.)
- "What if my gut says yes but the structure says no?" (Trust the structure. Gut feel is often bias.)

### Sub-Module 2: Assessing Coachability (12 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach managers to spot coachability in interviews
- Show the red flags
- Help them prioritize coachability over experience

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Coachability > experience (you can teach skills, not mindset)
- Watch for: ownership, self-awareness, openness, curiosity
- Red flags: external blame, defensiveness, no gaps admitted

**The Coachability Questions:**
- "Tell me about critical feedback you received. How did you handle it?"
- "What are you working on improving?"
- "What do you not know about this role?"
- "Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?"

**What You're Listening For:**
- Do they own it or blame others?
- Are they reflective or defensive?
- Do they admit gaps or act perfect?
- Do they learn from mistakes or repeat them?

**Real Example:**
- Candidate B: Less experienced than others
- Coachability question: "I got feedback that I was moving too fast in discovery. I slowed down, asked more questions, and my close rate improved from 18% to 25%."
- Decision: Hire (coachable people improve fast)

**Business Impact:**
- Hire people who will grow
- Avoid hiring defensive, uncoachable reps
- Team culture improves

**Tension to Address:**
- "Doesn't hiring less experienced people mean more coaching work?" (Yes, but coachable people improve. Uncoachable people never do.)
- "What if someone's experienced but not coachable?" (Pass. Experience without coachability = stagnation.)

### Sub-Module 3: The 30-60-90 Day Onboarding (14 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach the structured onboarding timeline
- Show what should happen at each stage
- Help managers ramp new reps fast

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Onboarding determines success (great hire + bad onboarding = underperformance)
- 30-60-90 is the ramp: Learn, Practice, Perform
- Clear milestones at each stage
- Coaching is built into onboarding (not optional)

**The Plan:**

**Days 1-30: Learn**
- Product, process, team
- Shadow calls, small deals
- Goal: Understand the role

**Days 31-60: Practice**
- Build pipeline, close deals
- 50% quota target
- Weekly coaching
- Goal: Build capability

**Days 61-90: Perform**
- Full quota
- Full territory
- Self-sufficient
- Goal: Independence

**Real Example:**
- New hire Sarah: Days 1-30, shadowed Andrew on 10 calls, learned product, closed 2 small deals
- Days 31-60: Built her own pipeline, hit 60% of quota, weekly coaching on discovery
- Days 61-90: Full quota, hit 95%, fully ramped
- Result: Fast ramp, high confidence, strong performance

**Business Impact:**
- New hires ramp faster
- They feel supported (not thrown in)
- Retention improves
- Leadership sees strong onboarding

**Tension to Address:**
- "Doesn't 90 days feel long?" (For complex SaaS, 90 days is fast. Some products take 120-180.)
- "What if they're experienced and don't need it?" (Everyone needs onboarding. Tailor it, but don't skip it.)

### Sub-Module 4: Team Composition Strategy (13 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach the value of balanced teams
- Show the archetypes and why they matter
- Help managers avoid hiring clones

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Homogenous teams are fragile
- Diverse teams are resilient
- You need hunters, farmers, analytical, relational, fast-movers, deep-thinkers
- Balance creates strength

**The Archetypes:**
- Hunters vs. Farmers (prospecting vs. nurturing)
- Analytical vs. Relational (data vs. people)
- Fast vs. Slow (velocity vs. depth)

**Why It Matters:**
- Hunters open doors; farmers close complex deals
- Analytical reps catch bad deals; relational reps build trust
- Fast-movers create urgency; deep-thinkers prevent mistakes

**Real Example:**
- Andrew's first team: 5 hunters (all Andrew clones)
- Result: Great prospecting, terrible at nurturing long cycles; lost big deals
- Rebuild: Hired 2 farmers, 1 analytical rep
- Result: Balanced team; handled all deal types; quota improved

**Business Impact:**
- Team handles diverse opportunities
- Resilient when market shifts
- Less dependency on one approach

**Tension to Address:**
- "Doesn't diversity mean conflict?" (Yes, healthy conflict. That's where growth happens.)
- "Won't different styles slow us down?" (No, they fill gaps and make you stronger.)

### Sub-Module 5: Hiring Mistakes & Recovery (12 min)
**Objectives:**
- Help managers recognize hiring mistakes early
- Teach them how to recover (coach or exit fast)
- Show that hiring mistakes are normal, not fatal

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Everyone makes hiring mistakes
- The mistake isn't the hire; it's holding on too long
- Recognize it early (30-60 days), act fast
- Don't let a bad hire drag for 9 months

**Signs You Hired Wrong:**
- 60 days in, they're not ramping (still struggling with basics)
- Defensive or blaming external factors
- Not implementing feedback
- Team morale suffering
- You're spending all your time coaching them

**What to Do:**
- Assess coachability (do they want to improve?)
- Give structured coaching for 30 days
- If no improvement: Exit fast
- If improving: Keep coaching

**Real Example:**
- Andrew hired Marcus: Liked him in interview
- 60 days in: Marcus at 30% of quota, blaming leads
- Andrew coached for 30 days; no improvement
- Decision: Let him go at day 90
- Team relieved; territory given to new hire; performance improved

**Business Impact:**
- Fast recovery prevents 6-9 months of pain
- Team sees you address mistakes
- Leadership trusts your judgment

**Tension to Address:**
- "Doesn't firing someone after 90 days look bad?" (No, holding on for 9 months looks worse.)
- "What if I'm wrong?" (You gave them 90 days. That's fair.)

## Key Tensions to Address

- **"Doesn't structure make interviews feel impersonal?"** → No, it makes them fair and accurate.
- **"What if I hire someone less experienced?"** → Coachability matters more. They'll grow.
- **"How do I know if my team is balanced?"** → Map the archetypes. If everyone's the same, you're fragile.
- **"Isn't 90 days too fast to judge a hire?"** → For bad hires, 90 days is generous. For good hires, they're ramping by then.
