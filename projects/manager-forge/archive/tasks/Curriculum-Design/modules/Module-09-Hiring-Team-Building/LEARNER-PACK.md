- Hunters prospect and qualify: leads to pipeline
- Farmer nurtures and closes complex deals: bigger deal sizes
- Result: fewer but bigger deals, higher ACV, better customer fit

Day 90 Results:
- Capability: 2.5/5 (no improvement)
- Coachability: 2.0/5 (still defensive; "I don't know if discovery is my style")
- Engagement: 2.5/5 (withdrawn, missing team meetings)
- Average: 2.3/5 (WORSE)

Clear outcome: This is not working.
```

---

**YOUR EXIT CONVERSATION:**

**Setting:** Private office, 1-on-1, 30 minutes scheduled

**Your opening (direct, kind):**

```

and I want to be honest rather than drag this out.

Here's what I'm seeing:

Over the last 30 days, we worked on applying the discovery framework. 
And despite your effort – and I do see effort – the approach isn't clicking. 
You're still struggling with discovery, and when I give feedback, you're 
explaining why discovery isn't your style rather than trying the framework.

That tells me one of two things: Either this role isn't leveraging your 
strengths, or the feedback cycle isn't working for you. Either way, 
I don't think it's fair to you or to the team to keep trying.

Here's what I'm going to do: We're going to part ways this Friday.

# PART D: WEEKLY ZOOM DISCUSSION PROMPTS

## PROMPT 1: Your Biggest Hiring Mistake

**Duration:** 10-15 minutes

**Setup:** This is the opening discussion for the module. The goal is to normalize mistakes and get people thinking about patterns.

---

**Core Question:**

"What's the biggest hiring mistake you've made? I'm not asking to embarrass you – 
I'm asking because we all make them. I want you to think about: Who did you hire? 
What went wrong? And what would you do differently now?"

---

**Follow-up Questions (if the room is quiet):**

```
1. "Did you hire them based on experience, or did you also consider coachability?"

2. "If you had to do it again, what question would you have asked in the interview 
   that you didn't ask?"

3. "How long before you realized it was a mistake? And how long before you acted?"

4. "Did you try to coach them, or did you part ways? Looking back, was that 
   the right call?"
```

---

**What You're Listening For:**

```
Good signs:
- [ ] They own the mistake (not "the candidate lied")
- [ ] They're specific about what went wrong
- [ ] They've learned something for next time

Red signs:
- [ ] They blame the candidate entirely
- [ ] They say, "I should have trusted my gut"
  (because their gut was bias, not instinct)
```

---

**Facilitator Notes:**

If someone says: *"I hired for likability and it backfired"* → Gold. Use it as a teaching moment.

If someone says: *"I should have asked more coachability questions"* → Excellent. 
They're getting the framework.
