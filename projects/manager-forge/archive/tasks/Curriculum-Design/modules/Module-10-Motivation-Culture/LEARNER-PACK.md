## SUB-MODULE 2: REMOVING DEMOTIVATORS

### Worksheet 2.1: Identify Demotivators in Your Team

**Instructions:** For each demotivator, answer honestly. This is diagnostic, not judgmental.

---

#### CLARITY: Do people know if they're winning?

**Assessment questions:**

1. Can each person on your team tell you their top 3 metrics right now?
   - [ ] Yes, all of them
   - [ ] Most of them
   - [ ] Some of them
   - [ ] No, I'd have to tell them

2. When someone is underperforming, do they know it *before* you tell them, or are they surprised?
   - [ ] They usually know
   - [ ] Sometimes they're surprised
   - [ ] Often surprised

3. Is your performance standard the same for everyone, or does it vary?
   - [ ] Same standard for everyone
   - [ ] Varies based on [explain]: _______________
   - [ ] Unclear / I haven't thought about it

4. How often do you give real-time feedback (not annual reviews)?
   - [ ] Weekly (in 1:1s or on the spot)
   - [ ] Monthly
   - [ ] Quarterly
   - [ ] Once a year

**Scoring:** 
- Mostly "yes, weekly, same standard": Clarity is strong
- Mixed: Clarity is partial
- Mostly "no, surprised, varies, quarterly": Clarity is broken

**If clarity is broken, pick ONE fix:**
- [ ] Create a one-page "What Success Looks Like" document
- [ ] Start tracking metrics weekly (not monthly)
- [ ] Do one real-time feedback conversation this week (on the spot, 5 min)

---

#### FAIRNESS: Do your rules apply equally?

**Assessment questions:**

1. You have a documented performance standard (not just gut feel):
   - [ ] Yes
   - [ ] Partially
   - [ ] No

2. If Person A missed quota and got a warning, Person B would get the same treatment:
   - [ ] Yes, absolutely
   - [ ] Probably
   - [ ] Not sure / depends on context
   - [ ] No, some people are held to different standards

3. Do you have a "star player" who operates under different rules than others?
   - [ ] No
   - [ ] Maybe (I'm not sure)
   - [ ] Yes

4. Compensation differences (if they exist) are explainable:
   - [ ] Yes, and people understand why
   - [ ] Yes, but people don't understand it
   - [ ] No, it's kind of arbitrary

**Scoring:**
- Mostly "yes, all consistent, no stars": Fairness is strong
- Mixed: Fairness is partial
- Mostly "no, varies, stars exist, arbitrary": Fairness is broken

**If fairness is broken, pick ONE fix:**
- [ ] Document your actual standards (write them down this week)
- [ ] Have a conversation about compensation differences (explain the "why")
- [ ] Identify your "star" and make decisions about whether they really are exempt

# SECTION C: ROLE-PLAY SCENARIOS (5 Total)

## SCENARIO 1: Culture Conversation (A Rep Is Thinking of Leaving)

**SETUP:**

You're in a 1:1 with Marcus, a solid performer on your team (been with you 18 months). He's been a bit quiet lately. His numbers are fine, but he seems disengaged. He says: "I've been thinking about looking for something else."

Your gut reaction: panic, then trying to convince him to stay.

**CONTEXT:**

- Marcus is not your biggest revenue driver, but he's solid and reliable.
- He's been consistent, no real problems, but no major growth either.
- You know he has two kids and a mortgage.
- You're not sure why he's thinking about leaving. You haven't had a career conversation in probably 10+ months.

---

### YOUR JOB

Instead of trying to convince him to stay, you coach him. You listen. You understand what he's really looking for. And *then* you decide whether to ask him to stay and what that means.

**Do NOT:** "We'll give you a raise." "Things are going to get better." "You're a critical part of the team."

**DO:** Listen first. Understand what's missing. Then decide.

---

### DIALOGUE (Your first 10 minutes)

**WHAT TO SAY:**

"I hear you. Before you make a decision, I want to understand what's going on. What's making you think about leaving?"

*Listen. Don't interrupt. Let him tell you.*

"Help me understand. Is it [compensation / growth / fit / something else]?"

*Listen to his answer.*

"I want to be honest with you. I don't think we've had a real career conversation in a while. I'm sorry about that. Let's talk about what you actually want. What would make you want to stay?"

*Listen.*

"Okay, so if we could fix [X], would you want to stay?"

*Listen. If yes, talk about what that looks like. If no, respect his choice.*

---

### DEBRIEF QUESTIONS

After the roleplay (or after a real conversation like this):

1. **What did you learn about what was really missing?**
   
   Was it money? Growth? Belonging? Clarity? (Probably not what you assumed.)

2. **Did you listen more than you talked?**
   
   If not, practice that next time. Listening = respecting.

3. **Did you offer a fix that *he* said would help, or did you try to convince him?**
   
   If you offered something he didn't ask for, you missed the point.

4. **If he decides to leave, what will you do differently with your next person?**
   
   This is your signal. Fix the culture issue before the next person thinks about leaving.

## SCENARIO 2: Tough Feedback (Someone's Behavior Is Toxic)

**SETUP:**

You're managing a team of 5. One of them, Sarah, is a strong performer (good numbers), but her behavior is toxic. She:

- Doesn't help teammates
- Makes sarcastic comments in team meetings
- Dominates conversations
- Has rolled her eyes at feedback

Your team has started avoiding her. One person hinted they might leave because of her. She's also smart and knows her job, so you don't want to lose her.

**CONTEXT:**

- She's been on the team 2 years.
- You've given her feedback once (6 months ago); she dismissed it.
- You're worried that if you push too hard, she'll leave (and take knowledge/relationships with her).
- You're also worried if you don't address it, more people will leave.

---

### YOUR JOB

Address the behavior without blame, shame, or ultimatums. Make it about impact, not character. Give her a real chance to change. If she doesn't, then you make bigger decisions.

**Do NOT:** "You're a jerk." "You're hurting morale." "Everyone hates working with you."

**DO:** "Your behavior is having an impact I need to talk to you about. Here's what I'm seeing."

---

### DIALOGUE (Your first 10 minutes)

**WHAT TO SAY:**

"Sarah, I want to talk about something I've been noticing. In team meetings, I've noticed [specific behavior: eye roll, sarcasm, not engaging]. I also notice [other behavior: not helping teammates]. I want to understand what's going on and talk about how we move forward."

*Pause. Let her respond.*

"Here's what I'm seeing as impact: [specific impact]. I value you as a performer, and I also need you to be someone people want to work with. That's not happening right now."

*Listen.*

"Here's what I need to see change: [specific behaviors]. I'm going to support you in that. Here's how: [offer support]. Can you commit to that?"

*Listen. Get agreement or resistance.*

"If you're struggling with something, let me know. But I need to see change. We'll check in weekly on this."

---

### DEBRIEF QUESTIONS

1. **Did you focus on behavior, not character?**
   
   (Not "you're toxic" but "this specific behavior is having this impact")

2. **Did you give her a real chance and real support?**
   
   (Not a trap, not a PIP, just clear feedback and support)

3. **If she doesn't change, what's your next step?**
   
   (Be clear on yourself: Are you okay moving her out? Or are you hoping she quits?)

4. **What role did *you* play in allowing this to happen?**
   
   (Did you avoid feedback early? Did you tolerate behavior you shouldn't have?)

## SCENARIO 3: Ritual Kick-Off (Launch a New Team Ritual)

**SETUP:**

You've decided to start a Friday Closing Huddle (10 minutes, celebrate wins, close the week strong).

Your team:
- Is remote (5 different cities)
- Has been burned by meetings before (too many Zooms)
- Is skeptical of "culture stuff"
- Is busy (usually wrapping up calls at 4:45 PM Friday)

You want to start this ritual next Friday at 4:45 PM for 10 minutes.

---

### YOUR JOB

Introduce it in a way that:
- Makes it clear why (not just "we're doing this for culture")
- Shows it's worth the 10 minutes
- Gives them agency (not forced)
- Builds momentum (so it sticks past week 2)

**Do NOT:** Make it sound like busywork. Don't make it compulsory. Don't make it cheesy.

**DO:** Make it real, brief, and tied to something they care about.

---

### DIALOGUE (Introducing to the team)

**WHAT TO SAY:**

"I want to try something new starting next Friday. We're doing a 10-minute closing huddle at 4:45. Here's why: We hustle all week, close deals, handle problems. But we never stop to acknowledge what we did. I want to change that.

Here's how it works: We hop on a quick call. I call out something someone did well this week. Then we're done. 10 minutes, then you're off to your weekend.

I'm not asking you to prepare anything. Just show up. I'm going to do this for the next month. If it matters, we keep it. If it doesn't, we stop.

Fair?"

*Pause. Gauge reaction.*

"Anyone have concerns? Questions?"

*Listen. Adjust as needed.*

"First one is Friday at 4:45. See you then."

---

### WEEK 1 EXECUTION

**Friday, 4:45 PM:**

"Hey everyone. Thanks for wrapping up and jumping on. Week's almost over.

I want to call out a few things:

[Person A]: You closed the TechCorp deal. That was persistence and creativity. 

[Person B]: I heard from [Customer] how much you helped them this week. That's relationship building.

[Person C]: You helped two people on the team. That's collaboration.

I'm proud of what we did this week. Go have a good weekend."

*That's it. You're done.*

---

### DEBRIEF QUESTIONS

1. **Did people show up? Did they engage?**
   
   (If not, why? What was the barrier?)

2. **Was your celebration specific and genuine?**
   
   (If it felt fake, they'll sense it.)

3. **Did you protect the time? (10 min, not 20?)**
   
   (Respect their time, or they'll resent the ritual.)

4. **Are you going to do it again next Friday?**
   
   (Consistency is what builds rituals. Once is a meeting. Twice is a ritual.)

## SCENARIO 4: Remote Connection (Build Connection in Async-First Team)

**SETUP:**

You're managing a fully remote team across 4 timezones. You're 4 months in, and people feel disconnected. 

- Async standup happens (people post to Slack).
- But there's no real connection. It's all task, no relationship.
- One person mentioned they felt "invisible" in a 1:1.
- You don't want to do back-to-back Zoom calls (everyone's already tired).

**CONTEXT:**

- Team is 8 people, good performers, but quiet
- No spontaneous help or collaboration
- No celebration of wins
- People are working in isolation

---

### YOUR JOB

Build connection without requiring a ton of synchronous time. Mostly async, but intentional and visible.

**Do NOT:** "Let's do more Zoom calls." "Let's have a weekly happy hour." (That's more of the same burnout.)

**DO:** Create visible connection points (wins, celebrations, learning) that don't require everyone in a room.

---

### THREE CHANGES YOU'LL MAKE

**CHANGE 1: Wins Channel**

Create a #wins channel in Slack (or shared doc, whatever).

Every Friday, you post: "Week of [date] wins:"

People post their wins. Anything counts: deal, customer feedback, learned something, helped a teammate.

Then you celebrate each one: ✨ reaction, or a comment like "Love that you helped [person]—that's collaboration."

**Why it works:** Visible. Async. Everyone sees everyone. Celebration is real.

---

**CHANGE 2: Weekly Written Shout-Out**

Every Monday morning, you send an email or Slack message:

"Team, one person I want to call out this week: [Name] did [specific thing]. That mattered because [impact]. Thanks [Name]."

Rotate who gets called out. Make it specific and genuine.

**Why it works:** People feel seen. It's not a meeting. It's personal.

---

**CHANGE 3: Monthly Async Learning Post**

Once a month, you post a 2-3 minute video OR a written post about something you learned.

Example: "This month I learned about [negotiation technique]. Here's why it matters. Have you used this? Reply with your experience."

Invite responses. Make it conversational. 

**Why it works:** Learning together. Connection through shared growth.

---

### IMPLEMENTATION

**Week 1:** Introduce #wins channel. Post first win.

**Week 2:** Send first weekly shout-out.

**Week 4:** Post first learning post.

**Month 2:** All three are running. See if it's working.

---

### DEBRIEF QUESTIONS

1. **Are people posting to #wins?**
   
   (If not, why? Do they not feel safe? Do they not see the point?)

2. **Do people react/comment?**
   
   (Engagement = connection. If it's silent, adjust.)

3. **Are you seeing more collaboration or help?**
   
   (Wins channel often surfaces who's struggling—then others help.)

4. **Do people feel more connected?**
   
   (Ask in 1:1s: "How are you feeling about the team lately?")

## SCENARIO 5: Growth Conversation (Someone Has No Path Forward)

**SETUP:**

You're in a 1:1 with Jamie, a solid performer (18 months in). They say: "I love this job, but I'm not sure where it goes. Am I stuck here?"

**CONTEXT:**

- Jamie is good at their current role but not yet ready for a promotion.
- Your company is small; there are only 2 levels (where they are, and your role).
- There's no traditional "up" path for them.
- You don't want to lose them, but you also can't promise something that doesn't exist.

---

### YOUR JOB

Be honest about limitations. But also help them see growth *within* their role and beyond (even if it's not promotion in your company).

**Do NOT:** "You have nowhere to go. This is as far as you can go." "Let's wait and see if we grow."

**DO:** "Here's what's realistic at our company. Here's what I see in you. Here are some paths forward."

---

### DIALOGUE (Your first 15 minutes)

**WHAT TO SAY:**

"Great question. I'm glad you asked. Let me be honest: our company is small. There isn't a promotion path right now. But that doesn't mean you don't have a path forward."

*Pause.*

"Here's what I see in you: [skills]. Here are some ways I think you can grow:

1. **Deepen:** Become an expert in [skill]. Be the person people come to.

2. **Expand:** Take on [new area]. I'd support you learning that.

3. **Lead:** Help develop someone else on the team. That's a different kind of growth.

4. **External:** If you wanted to take on a big project outside the company—conference talk, open source, writing—I'd support that too."

*Listen.*

"What sounds interesting to you?"

*Listen.*

"Here's what I can commit to: We'll revisit this every quarter. If we grow and there's a real path, I want it to be for you. In the meantime, let's make sure you're growing. What matters to you?"

*Listen. Make a real commitment.*

---

### DEBRIEF QUESTIONS

1. **Did you lie or make a false promise?**
   
   (Honesty = trust. Fake promises = resentment.)

2. **Did you offer real growth options, even if not a promotion?**
   
   (Mastery, autonomy, leadership—these matter too.)

3. **Did you make a commitment and follow through?**
   
   (If you said "quarterly check-in," make it happen.)

4. **If they still decide to leave, what did you learn?**
   
   (Sometimes honest "this isn't the right path" is the right answer.)

1. How is it showing up?
2. What's the impact (who's it affecting)?
3. What's one thing you're going to do to fix it?"

**LISTENER TIPS:**

- Help them be specific (not just "clarity is broken" but "people don't know if they're winning")
- Ask: "If you fixed that, what would change?"
- Offer parallels from your own experience

## WEEK 3: INTRINSIC MOTIVATION & RITUALS

- Offer ideas if they're stuck

**TIME:** 20–30 minutes

## WEEK 4: BELONGING

**PROMPT:**

"Here's a harder question: If you left your job tomorrow, what would you miss about your team?

Not the work. The *team*. The people.

**LISTENER TIPS:**

- Let people be vulnerable here
- If someone says "I'd miss nothing," don't judge—that's honest
- Ask: "What would it take to make that different?"

**TIME:** 20–30 minutes (this one goes deeper)

## WEEK 5: REMOTE/HYBRID CULTURE

**PROMPT:**

"If your team is remote or hybrid: What's one specific thing you're doing to build connection without Zoom burnout?

- Ask: "How do people respond?"
- Problem-solve if something isn't working

**TIME:** 20–30 minutes
