# Time Management & Scaling — Module Brief

**Module Status:** Ready for Content Development  
**Module #:** 11 of 12  
**Position in Curriculum:** Scaling & sustainability (follows Motivation & Culture; precedes Leadership Growth)

---

## Executive Overview

**Time Management & Scaling** teaches the most critical skill for manager survival: how to multiply your impact without working 70-hour weeks. This module covers delegation, automation, prioritization, and how to scale yourself so you're not the bottleneck.

The core insight: **You cannot scale your personal effort. You have to scale your team's effort. That means doing less, delegating more, and getting comfortable with people doing things differently than you would.**

---

## Core Teaching Objective: THE AHA MOMENT

**What we want managers to realize:**

Most new managers try to do everything themselves. They coach every rep, attend every call, write every forecast email, manage every deal. They burn out in 6 months. The path to sanity is: delegate like crazy, automate what you can, and protect time for the things only you can do (coaching, strategy, 1:1s).

**More specifically:**

1. **Your personal bandwidth is limited.** You have ~5 hours/week of actual "manager work" time (coaching, strategy, 1:1s). Everything else should be delegated or automated. If you're spending 20+ hours on admin, you're in the wrong business.

2. **Delegation is how you scale.** You can't coach 10 people if you're doing the work of 2 people. You have to push work to the team—deal coaching, new hire onboarding, training, forecasting. They grow, you scale, everyone wins.

3. **The wrong delegation kills people.** Delegating busywork (data entry, formatting reports) doesn't develop people. Delegating decisions (who to coach, which deals matter) develops people AND scales you.

4. **Automation is your secret weapon.** A 30-minute weekly forecast call could be a 5-minute Slack update. A manual forecast email could be a dashboard. A monthly training could be an async video. Automation buys you time and often creates a better experience.

5. **You'll be slower at first.** Teaching someone to do something takes 3x longer than doing it yourself. Then they do it 2x faster than you. Then they do it on repeat. That's the math of scaling.

6. **Burnout is a performance issue.** If you're burned out, you can't coach, you can't strategize, you can't make good decisions. Taking time for recovery is not selfish—it's professional responsibility.

**By the end of this module, managers should feel:**
- Clear on what they should actually be doing (and what they're wasting time on)
- Equipped to delegate decision-making (not just tasks)
- Confident using automation to save time
- Understanding that doing less = scaling more

## The Problem We're Solving

### The Mistake Most New Managers Make

New managers typically do one of two things with time—both unsustainable:

**Mistake 1: The Bottleneck**
"I'm the manager. Everything should go through me. I need to be in every deal, every call, every decision."

What happens: You're at capacity within 3 months. You're working weekends. You can't coach because you're too busy doing. You're making worse decisions because you're exhausted. Burnout is 6 months away.

**Mistake 2: The Absentee**
"I'm going to delegate everything. I'm just going to focus on 1:1s."

What happens: Reps get confused (who's accountable?). Quality suffers (no one's checking things). You lose visibility (you don't know what's happening). Team thinks you don't care.

### The Real Issue

**You have to be selective about what you do AND do it well.**

Most new managers don't have a framework for time, so they either micromanage everything (burnout) or neglect everything (chaos). Neither works.

The way out: **Identify your core manager work (5-10% of your time). Delegate decision-making (not just busywork). Automate what you can. Protect focused time.**

## Key Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. **The Time Audit: Where Your Time Actually Goes**

Most managers don't know where their time goes. The first step is audit.

**Track your time for one week:**
- Monday-Friday, log every 30 minutes (what were you doing?)
- Be honest (not aspirational)
- Categories: 1:1s, coaching, admin, meetings, calls, email, planning

**Then Categorize:**

**Core Manager Work (should be 30-40% of time):**
- 1:1s (coaching, feedback, growth)
- Strategy (quarterly planning, goal-setting)
- Hiring & onboarding
- High-stakes coaching (underperformers, top talent)

**Important But Delegatable (should be 30-40%):**
- Deal coaching (teach reps to do this)
- Forecasting (can team members do initial input?)
- Training (can senior reps teach?)
- New hire onboarding (can peers onboard?)

**Automatable (should be <10%):**
- Reports (can a dashboard replace it?)
- Status updates (can Slack/email template replace it?)
- Scheduling (can Calendly replace it?)
- Data entry (can CRM do it?)

**Random (should be <10%):**
- Unexpected issues, interruptions

**What Most Managers Find:**
- "I'm spending 30% on admin that could be automated"
- "I'm coaching deals when reps should be coaching each other"
- "I'm in 15 meetings/week that don't need me"
- "I have zero time for strategy"

### 2. **The Delegation Framework: What to Delegate & How**

Not all delegation is equal. Some builds people; some just offloads busywork.

**Low-Value Delegation (busywork):**
- Data entry, formatting, scheduling, report collation
- **Problem:** Doesn't develop people; just offloads your work
- **Use sparingly:** Only for operational stuff

**High-Value Delegation (decision-making):**
- Deal coaching (teach your top rep to coach others)
- New hire coaching (senior rep mentors new person)
- Pipeline review (team members present their own)
- Training delivery (someone leads skill share)
- Forecasting (team members do input, you validate)

**Why It Matters:**
- High-value delegation develops your people AND scales you
- Low-value delegation just offloads drudgery

**The Delegation Process:**

**Step 1: Choose the Right Task**
- Something that's repeatable
- Something that doesn't require your expertise
- Ideally, something that develops the person

**Step 2: Choose the Right Person**
- Who could benefit from this development?
- Who has capacity?
- Who shows readiness?

**Step 3: Train Them**
- First time: You do it together (show them)
- Second time: They do it, you watch (give feedback)
- Third time: They do it (you step back)
- After that: Spot check (occasional feedback)

**Step 4: Let Go**
- They'll do it differently than you
- It's OK if different = good enough
- Don't nitpick if it's 85% as good as you'd do it

**Example:**
- **Task:** Deal coaching (you coach 15 reps; takes 5+ hours/week)
- **Delegate to:** Your top performer (Sarah)
- **Training:** Week 1, you coach together; Week 2, Sarah coaches, you watch; Week 3, Sarah coaches, you spot-check
- **Result:** Sarah develops coaching skills; you get 2 hours/week back; other reps get peer coaching

### 3. **The Automation Framework: What to Automate**

Automation buys you time and often creates a better experience.

**Candidates for Automation:**

**Forecasting:**
- Manual: You send email Friday 4pm asking for pipeline; reps respond via email; you manually create Excel; 3 hours/week
- Automated: Dashboard with live data; reps see real-time; you just validate; 15 min/week

**Status Updates:**
- Manual: Weekly email asking for wins/challenges; you copy/paste into Slack; 1 hour/week
- Automated: Template in Slack on Monday; people fill in; you just read; 10 min/week

**Scheduling:**
- Manual: Back-and-forth on calendar, emails, "Does Tuesday work?"; 30 min/week
- Automated: Calendly or Slack, people self-schedule; 5 min/week

**New Hire Onboarding:**
- Manual: You create custom doc for each new hire; you explain product; 8 hours/hire
- Automated: Onboarding checklist/video; peer mentoring; you do final review; 2 hours/hire

**Training:**
- Manual: Monthly in-person training; 3 hours/month plus prep
- Automated: Recorded video available async; monthly live Q&A; 1 hour/month plus one-time recording

**The Rule:**
- If it's repeatable and takes >1 hour/week, automate it
- Use tools: Zapier, Slack workflows, dashboards, templates, recorded videos

### 4. **The Priority Matrix: What Only You Can Do**

You have limited time. Spend it on high-impact work.

**High Impact, Only You:**
- 1:1 coaching (especially struggling reps, top talent)
- Strategy & goal-setting (quarterly planning)
- Hiring (you need to assess)
- High-stakes performance management
- Building culture & relationships

**High Impact, Delegatable:**
- Deal coaching (teach your best rep; they coach others)
- Training (record it; have senior rep deliver)
- New hire onboarding (team-based, you spot-check)
- Pipeline review (team members present)

**Low Impact, Automatable:**
- Forecasting compilation
- Status reporting
- Scheduling
- Data entry

**Spend your time on high-impact, only-you work. Delegate or automate the rest.**

### 5. **The Burnout Prevention Framework**

Burnout is a performance issue, not a personality trait. Prevent it.

**Signs of Burnout:**
- Working >50 hours consistently
- Cynicism about your job
- Poor decision-making (you're tired)
- Avoiding hard conversations (too exhausted)
- No time for your own development
- Dreading Monday

**Prevention:**
- Block focused time (no meetings, no Slack; only deep work)
- Protect your evening/weekend (don't check Slack after 6pm)
- Delegate ruthlessly (you can't do it all)
- Automate bureaucracy (don't waste time on busywork)
- Regular 1:1 with your manager (tell them you're drowning before you drown)
- Small breaks (even 15 min walk helps)

**The Truth:**
- If you're burned out, you're a bad manager (you're exhausted, making poor decisions, snapping at people)
- Prevention is your job, not your company's
- Saying "no" to stuff is a strength, not a weakness

## Sub-Module Breakdown

### Sub-Module 1: The Time Audit (12 min)
**Objectives:**
- Help managers understand where their time actually goes
- Reveal time-wasters and automatable tasks
- Identify what's not getting done because of time poverty

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Track honestly (not aspirational)
- Categorize: Core manager work vs. delegatable vs. automatable
- Most managers have 30%+ time on automatable stuff
- If you have no time for strategy, you're doing someone else's job

**Real Example:**
- Manager tracked time: 15 hours admin, 10 hours meetings (many not needed), 5 hours 1:1s
- Realization: Only 5 hours on actual coaching/strategy
- Plan: Automate admin (4 hours), eliminate unnecessary meetings (3 hours), protect 10 hours for core work

**Business Impact:**
- More time on coaching → team performance improves
- Less time on busywork → better strategic thinking

**Tension to Address:**
- "If I don't do it, who will?" (Delegate it. That's the whole point.)
- "I don't have time to set up automation." (Takes 2 hours upfront; saves 50 hours/year.)

### Sub-Module 2: Delegation That Develops People (14 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach the difference between busywork and high-value delegation
- Show how to delegate decision-making (not just tasks)
- Help managers build coaching depth

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Delegate decision-making, not just tasks
- Teach via the 3-time method (show, watch, step back)
- They'll do it differently—OK if it's 85% as good
- High-value delegation = scales you + develops them

**High-Value Delegation:**
- Deal coaching (teach top rep; they coach others)
- Peer onboarding (senior rep mentors new hire)
- Training delivery (someone leads a skill share)
- Team forecasting (members present their own)

**Real Example:**
- You coach 15 reps; it's unsustainable
- Delegate to Sarah: Teach her to coach 5 reps
- Result: Sarah develops leadership; you get 2 hours/week; team gets peer coaching

**Business Impact:**
- Builds bench strength (someone could cover for you)
- Develops top talent (leadership experience)
- Frees you up to coach coaches (not just reps)

**Tension to Address:**
- "What if they do it wrong?" (They might. Give feedback. Most people get good at it.)
- "What if they leave?" (Even better—they'll be a better hire elsewhere. Plus, you're not dependent on one person.)

### Sub-Module 3: Automation Strategy (13 min)
**Objectives:**
- Show what automation can do (save time, improve experience)
- Teach how to identify automatable tasks
- Help managers implement low-effort automations

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Repeatable tasks >1 hour/week = automate
- Tools: Zapier, Slack workflows, templates, dashboards, recorded videos
- Automation often improves experience (self-service is faster)
- Start small (one automation at a time)

**Candidates for Automation:**
- Forecasting (dashboard instead of email)
- Status updates (Slack template instead of email chain)
- Scheduling (Calendly instead of back-and-forth)
- Onboarding (checklist + video instead of manual)
- Training (recorded + async instead of monthly live)

**Real Example:**
- Forecast took 3 hours/week (email, collation, Excel, distribution)
- Automate: Dashboard with live data; reps update CRM; you validate
- Result: 15 min/week; reps have visibility; better data

**Business Impact:**
- Time saved (15 hours/month = 180 hours/year)
- Better experience (real-time vs. weekly stale data)

**Tension to Address:**
- "Setting up automation is complicated." (It's not. Zapier + templates can do 80% of it.)
- "What if people don't use the automation?" (They will if it's easier than the old way.)

### Sub-Module 4: Protecting Your Time (12 min)
**Objectives:**
- Teach how to say "no" to time-wasters
- Show how to block focused time
- Help managers prioritize deep work

**Key Teaching Points:**
- No meetings unless there's an agenda and you're needed
- No 1:1s without feedback/coaching (status can be Slack)
- Block 5 hours/week for coaching, strategy, 1:1s
- Protect your morning (deep work) or whatever time you think best
- Say "no" to meetings that don't have you in them

**The Schedule:**
- 9-10am: Focused time (no meetings, no Slack)
- 10-12pm: 1:1s (coaching, feedback)
- 12-1pm: Lunch/break
- 1-2pm: Focused time (admin, planning)
- 2-5pm: Meetings (only if needed) + open office hours

**Why It Works:**
- Focused time → better coaching, better strategy
- 1:1s back-to-back → momentum, deeper conversations
- Open office hours → people know when you're available (vs. constant interruptions)

**Real Example:**
- Manager had no focused time (constant Slack, interruptions)
- Blocked 10-10:30am no-Slack rule; email/Slack checked twice/day
- Result: Could actually think; better decisions; less reactive

**Business Impact:**
- Better strategy → clearer goals → better team performance
- Less reactive → proactive coaching

**Tension to Address:**
- "What if something urgent comes up?" (It will. You'll be available 2x/day plus office hours.)
- "Won't people think I'm not responsive?" (No. Delayed response is better than bad response.)

### Sub-Module 5: Burnout Prevention & Recovery (11 min)
**Objectives:**
- Help managers recognize burnout signals
- Teach prevention strategies
- Show that burnout is a performance issue

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Burnout = bad decisions, short-term thinking, poor coaching
- Prevention: Delegate, automate, protect time, say no
- If you're burned out, tell your manager (it's not weakness)
- Small breaks > long vacation

**Prevention Checklist:**
- [ ] Delegating ruthlessly (high-value tasks)
- [ ] Automating busywork
- [ ] Blocking focused time
- [ ] Not checking Slack after 6pm
- [ ] Taking at least one day/week fully off
- [ ] Regular coffee with your manager (not just in 1:1s)

**Signs of Burnout:**
- Working >50 hours/week consistently
- Cynicism about your job
- Poor decision-making
- Avoiding hard conversations
- No time for your own development

**If You're Burned Out:**
- Tell your manager immediately
- Delegate immediately (this is now your priority)
- Automate immediately (audit and implement)
- Protect time immediately (block your calendar)

**Recovery Timeline:**
- First month: Stabilization (stop bleeding)
- Months 2-3: Rebuilding (restore confidence)
- Month 4+: Moving forward (growth mindset returns)

**Real Example:**
- Manager working 60+ hours/week; cynical; making bad decisions
- Talked to manager: "I'm drowning"
- Plan: Delegated deal coaching (5 hours/week), automated forecast (2 hours/week), blocked focus time
- Month 2: Back to 50 hours; feeling better
- Month 3: Feeling normal; back to strategic thinking

**Business Impact:**
- Better decisions → better team performance
- Manager retention (don't lose good people to burnout)
- Role modeling (team sees healthy boundaries)

**Tension to Address:**
- "Isn't working 60 hours/week just what managers do?" (No. It's a sign you're not scaling.)
- "Will my team think I'm lazy if I'm not working weekends?" (No. They'll think you're smart and sustainable.)

- **Time Allocation Assessment:** Are you spending time on high-impact work? (diagnostic)

1. **Saying no:** "Can you sit in this meeting?" → "No, here's why."
2. **Delegation:** Assign high-value task; teach the 3-time method.

4. **Protecting time:** Block a focus time and defend it against interruptions.

3. "Share one automation that saved you time."
