# CRO Discovery Framework

> Use this when you get a meeting from the Gojiberry campaign. Goal: diagnose which gap is loudest, not pitch the program. If the CRO names the problem themselves, you've won.

**Meeting length:** 30-45 min
**Your posture:** Practitioner talking to practitioner. You manage a team with these systems in production. You're not selling a course — you're evaluating fit.

---

## Phase 1: Qualify the team (5 min)

Confirm this is the right team before diagnosing.

**You're looking for:**
- SaaS/software sales managers (not AEs, not directors-only)
- Aware something is changing, but no structured response yet
- Managers with direct reports, not player-coaches

**Questions:**
- "How many managers are we talking about, and what's their average tenure in the role?"
- "Have any of them gone through formal training in the last 18 months?"

If they've done Sandler/Force Management — fine, that's foundations, different layer. If they've done an AI-specific program already, probe what it covered before continuing.

---

## Phase 2: The six diagnostic questions

One question per gap. You don't need all six — stop when you find the loudest one.

### 1. Job clarity
> "If I asked your managers what their job is now vs. three years ago, what would they say?"

**Fit signal:** They can't articulate the difference, or they say "basically the same but with AI tools." That means they've added tools to the old operating model without redefining the role.

---

### 2. Pipeline reviews
> "Walk me through how your managers run their weekly pipeline reviews. What does the AI do and what does the manager do?"

**Fit signal:** Manager is still asking "what's in your pipeline?" — doing the data gathering themselves. Or they have AI-generated summaries but aren't sure how to interrogate them. The tell: "the rep explains the deal" instead of "the manager reads the health and asks why."

---

### 3. Coaching the new rep archetypes
> "Are you seeing reps who over-rely on AI outputs without adding judgment? Or reps who refuse to use AI at all and are falling behind?"

**Fit signal:** Yes to either. The three types are the over-reliant, the AI-avoider, and the AI-native with other gaps. If the CRO names these without prompting, they're feeling the pain. If they look blank, managers aren't coaching for it yet.

---

### 4. 1:1 and meeting quality
> "Are your managers' 1:1s more structured than they were a year ago, or more chaotic?"

**Fit signal:** "More chaotic" — too much data, not enough synthesis. Or the AI prep exists but managers don't know what to do with it. The tell: managers showing up to 1:1s with reports rather than leading them.

---

### 5. Hiring criteria
> "Has AI fluency shown up in your hiring criteria yet? How are your managers assessing for it?"

**Fit signal:** "We haven't figured that out yet." Most haven't. If they're still hiring for territory experience without testing AI workflow, the gap is real.

---

### 6. Team mood
> "What's the general mood on your team about AI — anxious, excited, or checked out?"

**Fit signal:** "Mixed" or "anxious." If the CRO doesn't know how their managers' teams feel about it, that's its own signal.

---

## Phase 3: The urgency question

After you've found the loudest gap:

> "When you imagine your best competitor's sales managers a year from now — what do you think they'll be doing that yours aren't yet?"

Don't answer this for them. Let them sit with it. They either surface real pain or hear themselves say "I don't know" out loud. Both are useful.

---

## Evaluation scoring

**Strong fit — move toward enrollment:**
- Can't articulate the manager's new job description
- Pipeline reviews are still rep-driven status updates
- Coaching AI-anxious or AI-overconfident reps without a framework
- No AI fluency criteria in hiring

**Soft fit — worth a follow-up:**
- CRO is aware but thinks "training" is the answer, not "operating system"
- Team has some AI tools but no coherent workflow
- Managers are junior and may not have authority to implement change yet

**Not a fit — don't force it:**
- Team is fewer than 3 managers
- Already running a structured AI ops program
- CRO wants a vendor, not a coach — shopping for software, not a cohort

---

## After the meeting

- Update this doc with what questions actually landed and which fell flat
- Note which gap came up most — that becomes your follow-up email anchor
- If strong fit: propose enrollment with the founding cohort framing ($2,500, mid-July start)
- If soft fit: send the program one-pager and schedule a follow-up in 3 weeks
