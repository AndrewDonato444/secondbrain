# Module 02 — Managing Up Without Micromanaging Down
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*This module teaches that managing up is intentional work — not something that happens by accident. You're a translator between two worlds with different needs. Results are table stakes. Trust is the currency that actually pays. And when AI is in the picture, every one of those conversations has new pressure points: unauthorized tools, AI-generated forecasts you don't trust, leadership asking you to champion things you haven't figured out yet.*

---

## Core Frameworks

**The Translator Role** — You sit between leadership (wants accurate forecasts, confidence in you, early visibility into problems) and your reps (need resources, autonomy, advocacy, clarity). You don't choose a side. You manage the tension.

**Inform → Advocate → Execute** — The three-part framework for how you show up:

| Step | What it is | What it sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| **Inform** | Accurate, contextual data. Not emotions. Not venting. | "Here's what's happening, here's the context, here's what it means." |
| **Advocate** | Make the case for what you think is right. Disagree with data and respect. | "I think we should do X because of Y. Here's the risk if we don't." |
| **Execute** | Once a decision is made, commit fully. Don't undermine it with your team. | "Here's the decision. Here's how we're running it. Questions to me, not around me." |

**Trust Capital** — How much your manager believes in your judgment, not just your numbers. Built through consistency, ownership, early bad news with solves. Destroyed by surprise, blame-shifting, and sandbagged forecasts.

**The Forecasting Paradox** — Reps want low (less pressure). Leadership wants high (growth). Your job: call the real number with high confidence in your plan to hit it. Sandbagging and inflating both cost trust.

## Role-Play Scenarios

*Work with a peer, cohort member, or mentor. One manager, one leader/rep, debrief after.*

---

### RP-02-01: The Bad News

**Setup:** Your team is tracking 10% below quota with two weeks left in the month. Your manager pulls you aside before the weekly exec pipeline call and asks, "How are we really tracking?"

**Manager goal:** Deliver the real number + the specific plan + the risks + your confidence — in under 90 seconds. No spin.

**Leader's starting position:** Under pressure from their own boss. Needs to know if you've got it or not.

**Debrief:**
- Did you open with the number or with context?
- Did you bring a solve?
- Did you state your confidence honestly?

---

### RP-02-02: The Compensation Pushback

**Setup:** New comp plan is reducing commission on sub-$50K deals. Your reps hate it. Your manager wants you to roll it out and get consensus.

**Manager goal:** Execute the plan without undermining it with your team, AND make the advocate case to leadership where the plan is genuinely broken.

**Leader's starting position:** "This is done, don't make this harder than it needs to be."

**Debrief:**
- Did you execute publicly while advocating privately?
- Did you fold immediately, or did you make the specific case for where the plan breaks?
- Did the leader feel like you were on their team or fighting them?

---

### RP-02-03: The Forecast Disagreement

**Setup:** Your manager wants a higher forecast than what your pipeline math supports. Specifically asking: "Can we call $650K instead of $500K?"

**Manager goal:** Hold the real number. Don't move without new data. Protect your credibility.

**Leader's starting position:** Pressured from above. Looking for help. Not asking you to lie — asking you to stretch.

**Debrief:**
- Did you hold the line without being defensive?
- Did you give them a way to talk to their boss?
- Did you give up any trust capital?

---

### RP-02-04: The Sally Situation

**Setup:** Sally is at 62% of quota two months running. Your manager asks, "Is Sally going to make it?"

**Manager goal:** Honest read + clear plan + specific checkpoint. Don't throw Sally under the bus. Don't hide the problem.

**Debrief:**
- Did you share the honest read?
- Did you bring the plan?
- Did you protect Sally's recovery chance while being transparent with leadership?

---

### RP-02-05: The Resource Ask

**Setup:** You need a new SDR. Budget is frozen. Your manager says "not this quarter."

**Manager goal:** Make the specific case with ROI math, accept the no gracefully if you get one, and set up the re-ask.

**Debrief:**
- Did you lead with the business case or with your team's frustration?
- Did you accept the no without losing the relationship?
- Did you propose what would change the answer next quarter?

## Quick Reference — Inform, Advocate, Execute

```
INFORM    → Data. Context. No emotion. No venting.
ADVOCATE  → Make your case with data. Disagree AND commit.
EXECUTE   → Own the decision publicly once it's made.
```
