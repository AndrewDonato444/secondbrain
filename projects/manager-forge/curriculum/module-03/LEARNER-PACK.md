# Module 03 — Difficult Conversation Choreography
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*This module teaches that conversations are cumulative — the hard conversation you're worried about was decided in the 50 conversations before it. Trust is built over time, not in a moment. Adults can handle truth. Structure beats winging it.*

---

## Core Framework

**The Data-First Framework** is the structure for every difficult conversation:

| Step | What It Is | What It Sounds Like |
|---|---|---|
| **DATA** | Observable facts only | "Your close rate is 15%. The expectation is 25%." |
| **STORY** | Your interpretation of what the data means | "The data shows a qualification problem — deals are moving in that shouldn't be." |
| **AGREEMENT** | Get on the same page before moving forward | "Do you see it that way? Is there context I'm missing?" |
| **OUTCOME** | State what success looks like | "We need to get your close rate to 25%." |
| **EXPECTATIONS** | The plan — what you'll do, what they'll do, timeline, consequences | "Here's the 60-day plan. Here's what I'll coach. Here's what happens if we don't see progress." |
| **EMOTIONS** | Acknowledge feelings — but last, not first | "I know this is hard to hear. The data is the data. Let's focus on what we can control." |

## Role-Play Scenarios

### RP-03-01: The Underperformer Before the Plan

**Setup:** Your rep has been below quota for 2 months — 75% in month 1, 68% in month 2. Close rate is 11% against a 20% team average. You've noticed but haven't said anything formally yet. This is your next 1:1.

**Manager goal:** Apply the Data-First Framework. No softening. Get to a clear plan with consequences stated.

**Rep's starting position:** Defensive. Has a story ready about lead quality and "the territory is tough."

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager lead with DATA, or did they start with interpretation?
- Did they get AGREEMENT before moving to OUTCOME?
- Were EXPECTATIONS specific — timeline, milestones, what happens if it doesn't improve?
- How did they handle the lead quality deflection?

---

### RP-03-02: Firing Someone

**Setup:** This rep has been on a 60-day performance plan. They didn't hit the targets. You're ending their employment today. HR is available immediately after.

**Manager goal:** Complete the entire conversation in under 3 minutes. Direct. Kind. No extended explanation. Hand off to HR.

**Rep's starting position:** Doesn't know what this meeting is about.

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager get to the point in the first 60 seconds?
- Did they say anything that centered themselves ("this was hard for me")?
- Did they avoid the temptation to over-explain or apologize for things outside their control?
- Was it possible to finish in under 3 minutes?

---

### RP-03-03: Comp Plan Explanation (With Math)

**Setup:** Leadership just changed the comp plan. Commission on deals under $50K drops from 10% to 8%. Deals over $50K goes from 10% to 13%. The rep you're talking to primarily closes smaller deals. For them, this is effectively a pay cut unless they move upmarket.

**Manager goal:** Tell the truth about the impact. Have the math done. Give them a strategy to adapt.

**Rep's starting position:** Already heard rumors. Already upset. Asking you directly: "Is this plan worse for me?"

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager tell the truth when asked directly?
- Did they have actual numbers — or did they approximate?
- Did they have a specific strategy for how this rep can adapt?
- Did they avoid the trap of hyping the plan or saying "it's actually better"?

---

### RP-03-04: Formal Performance Plan Delivery

**Setup:** This rep has been underperforming for 3 months. You've had two previous 1:1 conversations about it. Today you're formalizing the improvement plan. The plan is documented.

**Manager goal:** Deliver the plan in a way that feels like a continuation of what's already been said — not a surprise. Get the rep's commitment.

**Rep's starting position:** Already knows things are serious. Anxious. Half expecting this. But also hoping it's not as formal as it looks.

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the plan feel like a surprise to the rep?
- Were the specific metrics, timeline, and consequences clear?
- Did the manager get explicit commitment from the rep?
- Did they balance accountability with support?

---

### RP-03-05: The Data Pushback

**Setup:** You've led with data on a close rate problem. The rep disagrees — they think the data is wrong or being misread.

**Rep's starting position:** "Those numbers don't account for the enterprise deals I'm working. Three of my biggest deals are in late stage and none of them are in the data yet."

**Manager goal:** Stay with the Data-First Framework. Investigate whether the pushback has merit. If it does, update the story. If it doesn't, hold the line on the data without getting personal.

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager genuinely consider whether the rep's context changes the picture?
- Did they hold firm without dismissing the pushback?
- Did they reach actual AGREEMENT before moving forward?
- Did the conversation stay data-focused or drift into "effort" and "attitude" territory?

---

### RP-03-06: Post-Plan Check-In

**Setup:** The rep has been on an improvement plan for 3 weeks. This is a check-in. The metrics show modest improvement — not enough to be comfortable, but trending in the right direction.

**Manager goal:** Give honest feedback on progress. Acknowledge what's working. Be clear about what still needs to happen and the stakes.

**Rep's starting position:** Hopeful. Feels like they're working hard and making progress. Looking for validation that things are okay.

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager give honest feedback — not just validation?
- Did they use data to describe where things stand?
- Were the stakes still clearly stated, even in a positive check-in?
- Did the manager "rescue" the rep from the discomfort of the plan by overselling the progress?

---

### RP-03-07: Critical Feedback on Quality

**Setup:** A rep's written proposals are consistently poor — too long, vague on ROI, not tailored to the prospect. You've seen this in three deals this quarter. You haven't raised it before.

**Manager goal:** Give specific, data-backed feedback on a behavior problem. Not "your proposals need work" — specific, observable, with examples.

**Rep's starting position:** Doesn't know this is coming. Thinks their proposals are fine.

**Debrief questions:**
- Was the feedback specific to observable behaviors — or did it stay vague ("you need to improve your proposals")?
- Did the manager have examples ready?
- Did they have a clear ask for what changes?
- Did the rep understand what "good" looks like when the conversation ended?

---

### RP-03-08: Explaining a Difficult Decision

**Setup:** You've reassigned one of the rep's accounts to a more senior rep. The business reason was solid — the account needed a different level of relationship. But you didn't loop the rep in before making the call.

**Manager goal:** Explain the decision transparently. Acknowledge you could have communicated it better. Don't manufacture a reason that sounds better than the real one.

**Rep's starting position:** Feels blindsided. Upset. Thinks they were being passed over or punished.

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager explain the actual reason — or did they give a polished, spin-heavy version?
- Did they acknowledge that their communication could have been better?
- Did they avoid being defensive?
- Did the rep feel heard by the end of the conversation?

---

### RP-03-09: The Emotional Rep

**Setup:** You're in the underperformer conversation. The rep is not defensive — they're upset. They're close to tears. They feel like they've been working as hard as they can and they're failing anyway. It's clearly affecting them personally.

**Manager goal:** Hold the Data-First Framework without being cold. Acknowledge the emotional reality without abandoning the plan or rescuing them from accountability.

**Rep's starting position:** Vulnerable. Emotionally raw. Not defensive — defeated.

**Debrief questions:**
- Did the manager make space for the emotion without letting it derail the conversation?
- Did they hold the factual framework even in a high-emotion moment?
- Did they avoid the trap of "backing off" on expectations because the rep seemed distressed?
- Was there a moment of genuine human connection in the conversation?

---

### RP-03-10: Proactive Foundation-Building 1:1

**Setup:** No crisis. No underperformance. A regular 1:1 with a rep who's hitting quota. The goal is to run a 1:1 that builds the foundation — one where the rep gets real feedback, feels known, and understands where they stand.

**Manager goal:** Give specific positive feedback that's earned (not generic praise). Name one specific thing to continue and one thing to develop further. Leave the rep knowing where they stand — even when everything's going well.

**Rep's starting position:** Doing well. Comfortable. Expecting a smooth 1:1.

**Debrief questions:**
- Was the positive feedback specific and earned — or generic ("you're doing great!")?
- Did the manager name something to develop even in a good performance conversation?
- Did the rep know where they stood in specific terms by the end?
- What would make this manager safe to be honest with if something went wrong later?

## Quick Reference — Data-First Framework

**Pocket version for your next difficult conversation:**

```
DATA       → Facts only. Numbers. What's observable.
STORY      → What the data means. Your diagnosis.
AGREEMENT  → Do we see the same thing? What am I missing?
OUTCOME    → What success looks like. Specific, measurable.
EXPECTATIONS → The plan. Your role. Their role. Timeline. Consequences.
EMOTIONS   → Acknowledge. Don't let them lead. Don't rescue.
```

**What not to say:**
- "You're doing great, just keep pushing!" (when data says otherwise)
- "This comp plan is actually better!" (without doing the math)
- "This was hard for me too." (centers you during a firing)
- "I'm sure you'll land on your feet." (minimizes their reality)
- "Let's see how it goes." (not a plan)

**The one question to ask yourself before any hard conversation:**  
*What foundation have I built with this person? And is the conversation I'm about to have a surprise?*

If it's a surprise — the foundation work isn't done yet.
