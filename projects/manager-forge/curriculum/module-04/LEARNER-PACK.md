# Module 04 — Manager Identity Shift
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*This module is about what you have to let go of to become a manager — and the grief that nobody warned you about. The old identity that made you a great rep is now in the way. And there's a second identity crisis the archive didn't anticipate: your reps are using AI tools better than you are, some of the work you were proud of is now done in seconds by a model, and the question underneath everything is "what am I actually for now?"*

---

## Core Frameworks

**The IC-to-Manager Identity Continuum** — You don't become a manager the day you get promoted. You become one over 6–18 months of letting go of the rep identity. Most managers are stuck somewhere on the continuum without realizing it.

**The Grief Cycle of Manager Identity** — Denial → Anger → Bargaining → Depression → Acceptance. These aren't metaphors. They're the literal phases of losing the work that used to define you.

**The Delegation Framework** — Outcome vs. Method. You own the outcome. They own the method. The day you start prescribing method is the day you've failed to transition.

**The New Value Proposition** — Team wins > individual wins. The highlight reel is no longer yours. What you're good at has to change. And now, in the AI era, it has to change twice.

## Role-Play Scenarios

---

### RP-04-01: The Deal I Want to Close Myself

**Setup:** Your biggest deal of the quarter is stuck in legal. You're convinced you could close it in a single call. The rep is struggling. Your instinct is to take it.

**Manager goal:** Coach the rep through the close without taking the work. Define outcome, let them own method, be there if they need you.

**Debrief:**
- Did you resist the takeover?
- What did it cost you emotionally to not close it yourself?
- What would you have learned about the rep if you'd taken it?

---

### RP-04-02: The Rep Who Asks You to Do Their Job

**Setup:** A rep comes to you with a half-drafted proposal and says, "Can you clean this up? I'm slammed."

**Manager goal:** Decline the takeback. Coach the actual skill gap. Send them back to do the work.

**Debrief:**
- Did you help without doing it for them?
- Did you name the pattern if this is recurring?
- Did you leave them better able to do it next time?

---

### RP-04-03: The Team Meeting Where You Miss Being a Rep

**Setup:** A team meeting where reps are sharing their wins. You find yourself about to tell the story of a deal you closed last year.

**Manager goal:** Notice the urge. Don't take the spotlight. Redirect to the rep's story.

**Debrief:**
- Did you catch the urge in real time?
- What was the underlying need — recognition, competence, belonging?
- How could that need get met in a way that's appropriate for your role now?

---

### RP-04-04: The Boss Who Calls You "Too Soft"

**Setup:** Your manager says, "You're too invested in your team's feelings. You need to be harder." You don't agree.

**Manager goal:** Hold your identity without defensiveness. Don't collapse into imitating a style that isn't yours. Don't bristle and dig in either.

**Debrief:**
- Did you hold your ground without being defensive?
- Did you ask for the specific behavior they're critiquing?
- Did you change anything? Should you have?

---

### RP-04-05: The Rep Who Says "I Could Do Your Job"

**Setup:** A high performer on your team says, casually, "Honestly, I could probably do your job if I had to."

**Manager goal:** Don't take it personally. Don't play down the comment. Don't agree defensively. Hold your value without proving it.

**Debrief:**
- Did you stay grounded?
- Did you get curious about where the comment came from?
- Did you find a way to name the difference between their job and yours without condescension?

---

### RP-04-06: The AI Tool Your Rep Teaches You (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A rep shows you a prompting workflow they've built that produces better outreach than your company's template. They offer to teach you.

**Manager goal:** Accept the teaching without ego. Build credibility by being a learner in public.

**Debrief:**
- Did you let them actually teach you?
- Did you name what you learned?
- Did you share it with the rest of the team and credit them?

---

### RP-04-07: "What's Your Job Now That AI Does That?" (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A peer manager asks you, "If AI can do the pipeline summary and the call analysis and the email drafting — what are you actually for anymore?"

**Manager goal:** Answer with your real, lived value. Not a LinkedIn answer. Not a defensive answer. Your answer.

**Debrief:**
- Did you have a real answer?
- Did you dodge or overclaim?
- Would your team confirm what you said?

---

### RP-04-08: The Identity Gut-Check Conversation (AI Layer)

**Setup:** Your manager, in a 1:1, says, "I want to be honest with you. I'm not sure what the manager role looks like in 18 months with where AI is going. Are you thinking about it?"

**Manager goal:** Engage honestly without either panicking or performing certainty. This is a real conversation, not a test.

**Debrief:**
- Did you admit what you don't know?
- Did you share what you're thinking?
- Did the conversation end with you closer to or further from your manager?

## Quick Reference — The Identity Shift

```
THE OLD IDENTITY        →  THE NEW IDENTITY
Closing deals           →  Coaching the closer
Being the expert        →  Building experts
Individual wins         →  Team wins
"Watch me do it"        →  "Tell me how you'd do it"
Owning method           →  Owning outcome
Being the smartest      →  Being the clearest
```

**The AI-era additional shift:**

```
THE PRE-AI MANAGER      →  THE AI-ERA MANAGER
Synthesizes the data    →  Validates the AI synthesis
Drafts the update       →  Edits the AI draft
Knows every tool        →  Learns from the rep who knows the tool
Value = expertise       →  Value = judgment + synthesis + reading the unsaid
```

**The two grief cycles:**
1. Losing the rep identity — Denial, Anger, Bargaining, Depression, Acceptance
2. Losing parts of the manager identity to AI — same five phases, happening in parallel

**The one question to ask yourself monthly:**
*What am I still doing manually that I could delegate — to a rep, to AI, or to both — and what am I doing with the time I get back?*

**Andrew's note:** You're not losing yourself in this transition. You're losing the version of yourself that made sense in a different job. Let it go. Grieve it properly. Then go build the new one — twice now, because the ground is still moving.
