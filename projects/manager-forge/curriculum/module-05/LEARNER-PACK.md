# Module 05 — Operating Rhythm
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*Consistency beats heroics. The manager who runs a steady weekly rhythm outperforms the manager who works harder in bursts — every time. This module gives you the three-tier rhythm (1:1s, team meeting, forecast call), the early-signal detection habits, and the meeting hygiene that keep it all from degrading. The AI layer: if your rhythm still has you spending an hour on Sunday compiling pipeline data by hand, you're running a 2019 operating system in a 2026 job.*

---

## Core Frameworks

**The Three-Tier Operating Rhythm** — Weekly 1:1 with each rep, weekly team meeting, weekly forecast/pipeline review. These three are non-negotiable. Everything else is optional.

**Consistency Over Perfection** — A mediocre 1:1 every week beats a great 1:1 every six weeks. The rhythm is the work.

**Early Signal Detection** — You don't need to wait for the number to slip to know something's off. The signals are earlier than the results. Your rhythm is what lets you catch them.

**Meeting Hygiene** — Every recurring meeting needs a purpose, an agenda, a decision/outcome mechanism, and a reason to cancel it if those are absent. Meetings that survive without purpose are how your week gets eaten.

## Role-Play Scenarios

---

### RP-05-01: The 1:1 That Got Canceled

**Setup:** You canceled your 1:1 with a rep two weeks in a row because your calendar exploded. Today's the next one. The rep is visibly checked out.

**Manager goal:** Own the cancellation. Don't over-apologize. Recommit to the rhythm in specific terms.

**Debrief:**
- Did you own it cleanly?
- Did you commit specifically or vaguely?
- Did the rep leave the conversation more engaged or less?

---

### RP-05-02: The Team Meeting Mutiny

**Setup:** A rep says, in the team meeting, "Honestly, this meeting is a waste of time. Can we cut it?" Other reps look uncomfortable — they agree but wouldn't say it.

**Manager goal:** Don't defend it. Acknowledge what's off. Redesign it with the team's input or kill it.

**Debrief:**
- Did you get defensive?
- Did you take the feedback as data or as attack?
- What did you change about the meeting?

---

### RP-05-03: The Early Signal You Want to Ignore

**Setup:** Your best rep's energy was flat in her last two 1:1s. She hasn't missed a number. You're tempted to let it go.

**Manager goal:** Raise it directly in the next 1:1. Not an interrogation — a real question. "Your energy's been different the last two weeks. What's going on?"

**Debrief:**
- Did you raise it or let it slide?
- Did you use the signal as data or accuse her of something?
- What did you learn?

---

### RP-05-04: The Forecast Call That Became a Status Update

**Setup:** Your weekly forecast call has degraded into a 45-minute status update. Reps walk away knowing nothing they didn't already know.

**Manager goal:** Redesign the meeting in under 10 minutes with the team. Shift the purpose from status to decision.

**Debrief:**
- Did you fix it with the team or alone?
- Is the new format one that'll survive contact with a stressful week?

---

### RP-05-05: The Rep Who's Reverting to Manual (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A rep who was a power user of your AI tools has quietly stopped using them for the last three weeks. Outputs are still fine. You noticed it in the usage dashboard.

**Manager goal:** Raise it in the 1:1 as a signal, not an accusation. Find out if it's a tool issue, a workflow issue, or a rep issue.

**Debrief:**
- Did you treat it as data or as a problem?
- Did you find out why?
- Did you make the rep feel surveilled or supported?

---

### RP-05-06: Killing a Meeting That Became AI-Obsolete (AI Layer)

**Setup:** Your weekly pipeline review meeting used to be where everyone compiled and shared their deal status. Now Clari does that in 30 seconds. The meeting hasn't been useful in a month but you keep holding it.

**Manager goal:** Either kill it or give it a new purpose. Don't let it limp along.

**Debrief:**
- Did you make the decision cleanly?
- Did you announce the decision with the reasoning?
- If you kept it, is the new purpose real or a rationalization to keep the meeting alive?

---

### RP-05-07: The Leadership-Imposed Meeting (AI Layer)

**Setup:** Your VP has added a new weekly "AI adoption review" to your calendar. You don't need it. You're already running your team's AI adoption well. But killing it is politically hard.

**Manager goal:** Navigate the political reality without letting a bad meeting eat your rhythm. Either reshape it into something useful or push back respectfully.

**Debrief:**
- Did you push back, comply, or reshape?
- Did you make the case with data?
- Is your rhythm intact?

## Quick Reference — The Three-Tier Rhythm

```
TIER 1 — WEEKLY 1:1s            (non-negotiable, 30 min each)
  Open → Their agenda → Your agenda → Commitments

TIER 2 — WEEKLY TEAM MEETING    (non-negotiable, 60 min)
  Wins → The number → Deal deep-dive → Teaching → Commitments

TIER 3 — WEEKLY FORECAST CALL   (non-negotiable, 30–45 min)
  AI first-pass data → Synthesis → Deals needing judgment → Actions
```

**Consistency rules:**
1. The rhythm holds in heavy weeks. That's the whole point.
2. You're the last to cancel, not the first.
3. Mediocre every week beats brilliant every six weeks.
4. If it's not blocked on the calendar, it's not real.

**Meeting hygiene test** (apply to every recurring meeting):
- Does it have a clear purpose?
- Does it have an agenda?
- Does it produce a decision or action?
- Could AI now do the data part of this, freeing the meeting for judgment?

**The AI-era redesign:**
- AI does the first-pass data work.
- You do the synthesis.
- Meetings are for the judgment calls, not the status updates.
- Kill anything that survived only because the data-gathering was manual.

**The one question to ask yourself every Friday:**
*Did my rhythm hold this week? If it didn't — was it because of real fire, or because I let something else matter more?*
