# Module 07 — Pipeline Review & Forecasting
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*Pipeline is a story. The number tells you the shape; the rep tells you what's inside it. This module gives you the pipeline architecture, the health diagnostic, the forecasting math, and the coaching patterns that turn a pipeline review from a status update into a coaching conversation. The AI layer: if your weekly pipeline review is still a data-gathering exercise, you're doing AI's job. Your job is interpretation.*

---

## Core Frameworks

**The Pipeline Stage Framework** — Every stage has a definition, an entry criterion, and a healthy/unhealthy signal. Reps who push deals to later stages than they belong are sandbagging-in-reverse: trying to look better than they are. Your job is to hold the line on stage definitions.

**Pipeline Health Diagnostic** — Four common health patterns, each with a specific coaching response:
- Too many early-stage deals, thin late stage → qualification / discovery issue
- Stuck deals at proposal → closing / next-step discipline issue
- Wrong deal sizes → targeting or territory issue
- Low close rate across stages → either pipeline quality or sales skill issue

**Forecasting Math** — Qualified pipeline × historical conversion rate = forecast. Vibes are not forecasts. If you can't do the math, you can't defend the number.

**The Sandbagger vs. Hyper-Caller** — Reps who under-call to avoid pressure, reps who over-call to impress. Both are trust-destroying. Your job is to know each rep's bias and coach against it.

**Pipeline Is a Story** — The shape tells you what's happening. Heavy early-stage means the reps are generating but not closing. Heavy late-stage with no recent movement means deals are stalling. Flat shape means nothing new is coming in. Read the shape.

## Role-Play Scenarios

---

### RP-07-01: Reading a Pipeline

**Setup:** A rep's pipeline is heavy late-stage but no deal has advanced in three weeks. Everything's "at proposal." She's confident about the month.

**Manager goal:** Use the Data-First Framework from Module 03 applied to pipeline. Lead with the shape, ask the coaching questions, don't accept vague "it's fine."

**Debrief:**
- Did you lead with data (the shape) or with judgment ("you're stuck")?
- Did you ask open questions or leading ones?
- Did you leave with a specific action?

---

### RP-07-02: The Sandbagger

**Setup:** A rep consistently calls a forecast 20% below what his pipeline math supports. He's been doing this for a year. He beats his number every month.

**Manager goal:** Name the pattern. Coach toward calling the real number. Make the case that sandbagging costs the team credibility with leadership.

**Debrief:**
- Did you hold the line without threatening him?
- Did he see why it matters?
- Did he commit to calling the real number next month?

---

### RP-07-03: The Hyper-Caller

**Setup:** A rep calls a forecast that never lands. Every month: $450K called, $280K delivered. He believes every deal will close.

**Manager goal:** Walk the pipeline with him deal by deal. Make him defend each one with math. Get him to call a real number.

**Debrief:**
- Did you coach vs. accuse?
- Did the conversation produce a lower forecast or just hurt feelings?

---

### RP-07-04: Low Conversion Rep Coaching

**Setup:** A rep's conversion rate is 12% against a team average of 22%. She has plenty of qualified pipeline. The math says she has a skill problem, not a volume problem.

**Manager goal:** Name the data. Diagnose where in the funnel she's losing deals. Build a specific coaching plan.

**Debrief:**
- Did you lead with the math or with the opinion?
- Did you find the stage where she's losing deals?
- Did you leave with a specific coaching focus (one stage, one skill)?

---

### RP-07-05: The Forecast Call With Leadership

**Setup:** Your VP asks in the exec call: "Why are you only calling $500K this month?"

**Manager goal:** Hold the math. Name the risks and upsides. Don't move without new data.

**Debrief:**
- Did you defend with math or with emotion?
- Did you give a reason the number could move, without actually moving it?

## Quick Reference — Pipeline & Forecast

```
THE HEALTH PATTERNS

Heavy early stage     → coach qualification & discovery
Stuck at proposal     → coach closing & next steps
Wrong deal sizes      → coach targeting & ICP
Low close rate        → coach skill gaps at specific stage
Stage inflation       → re-qualify with stage definitions

THE FORECAST MATH

Qualified pipeline $ × historical conversion % = forecast
(No vibes. Show the math.)

THE AI-AUGMENTED REVIEW

Step 1  →  AI generates first-pass pipeline summary (auto)
Step 2  →  Manager synthesizes, names AI blind spots, flags 2–3 deals
Step 3  →  Meeting focuses on judgment, not status

AI BLIND SPOTS TO WATCH FOR

Recency bias           (overweights last activity)
Stage inflation        (trusts rep-reported stage)
Size distortions       (large + low-activity = low score)
Committee deals        (penalized vs. short-cycle)
```

**The opening question that changes a pipeline review:**

*Before:* "What's in your pipeline?"
*After:* "The AI flagged three deals stuck at proposal. Walk me through why."

**The one question to ask yourself before every pipeline review:**
*Am I going to interpret this, or compile it? If I'm compiling, I'm doing AI's job.*
