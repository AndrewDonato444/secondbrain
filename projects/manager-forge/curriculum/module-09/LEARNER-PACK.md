# Module 09 — Hiring & Team Building
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*Hiring is the highest-leverage decision you make. One bad hire costs you 6–12 months of team productivity and your credibility with leadership. This module gives you the Capability-Coachability-Fit framework, the 4-part interview structure, the coachability red flags, and the 30-60-90 onboarding plan. The AI layer: you're now hiring into a role that's reshaping in real time. If you're not screening for AI fluency as a fourth dimension, you're building a team that's already behind.*

---

## Core Frameworks

**Capability-Coachability-Fit** — Every hire needs all three:
- **Capability** — Can they do the job today?
- **Coachability** — Can they grow in the job?
- **Fit** — Will they thrive in your team's context?

Missing any one is a bad hire. Coachability weighs most heavily — you can teach capability, you can build fit, but you can't teach someone to be teachable.

**The 4-Part Interview Structure** — Four sequential interviews or sections:
1. **Experience deep-dive** — What have they done? Specifics, not resumes.
2. **Skill assessment** — Can they actually do the thing? (role-play, exercise, mock call)
3. **Coachability check** — How do they receive feedback? How do they talk about past mistakes?
4. **Fit & motivation** — Why here? Why now? What do they want?

**Coachability Red Flags** — External blame for past failures, defensiveness, no self-awareness, job-hopping without growth story, "I already know how to sell."

**The 30-60-90 Day Onboarding Plan** — Specific milestones at each checkpoint. Day 30: understands the product, ICP, and process. Day 60: running discovery calls, managing pipeline. Day 90: closed something, on track for ramp.

*These are the questions that surface coachability. Not "are you coachable?" — people always say yes.*

**The questions that work:**

| Question | What good looks like | What red flag looks like |

| "Tell me about a time you got hard feedback. What was it and what did you do?" | Specific feedback, owns it, specific change | Vague, defensive, "my manager didn't really know me" |

### WS-09-11: AI Fluency as a Hiring Dimension
**AI Layer — The Fourth Dimension**

*AI fluency joins Capability, Coachability, and Fit as a required screen.*

**AI fluency is not:**
- Power user of every AI tool
- Technical / engineering knowledge
- Able to recite prompting best practices

**AI fluency is:**

| Component | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **Curiosity** | Genuinely interested in trying new tools; doesn't dismiss AI as "not real selling" |
| **Judgment** | Knows when AI output is good enough to send, and when it's not |

### WS-09-12: AI Fluency Interview Probe
**AI Layer — The Specific Questions**

*Add these questions to the skill assessment or coachability section.*

**Probe 1: The prompt-to-output question**

> "Tell me about the last time you used an AI tool for work. What was the task? What did you actually prompt? What did it produce? What did you change before using it? What did you decide not to use?"

**What I'm listening for (good):**
- Specific task, specific tool, specific prompt
- Ownership of the output (edited, iterated)
- Judgment about what not to send
- No apology, no hype

**Red flags:**
- Vague ("I use ChatGPT sometimes")
- Prompt was generic ("write a sales email")
- No editing — sent first output
- Treats AI as a mystery

---

**Probe 2: The discipline question**

> "You're using an AI tool to draft outreach. The first output is usable but generic. What do you do?"

**What I'm listening for (good):**
- Iterate the prompt with more context
- Edit manually for the specific prospect
- Combine AI draft with personal research
- Says: "I'd never send first output"

**Red flags:**
- "I'd send it — it's close enough"
- "I'd use it as a template and send it to a bunch of people"
- "I'd probably just write it myself if it's not good"

---

**Probe 3: The judgment question**

> "When is AI the wrong tool for a sales task?"

**What I'm listening for (good):**
- Specific examples (first call with a champion, negotiating with a senior buyer, a high-emotion moment)
- Articulates when human judgment matters more than efficiency
- Shows they've thought about this

**Red flags:**
- "It's always useful"
- "I don't know"
- "When the prospect is important" (too vague)

---

**The AI fluency score:**

| Dimension | 1–5 |
|---|---|
| Curiosity | |
| Judgment | |
| Discipline | |

**Hire decision consideration:**
- All three at 3+ → AI-fluent, net positive signal
- Mixed (some high, some low) → coachable if curiosity is high
- All below 3 → AI-resistant, likely team composition problem

### WS-09-13: AI Fluency Red Flags
**AI Layer — New Red Flags**

*Two new red flags to add to the existing red flag list (WS-09-04).*

| New red flag | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| "Treats AI as cheating" | "I prefer to do real selling." "AI writes better than me — that's a problem, not a solution." |
| "No curiosity about tools" | "I use whatever the company gives me, I don't really go looking." "I don't see why I'd need to learn another tool." |

**The nuance:** These are red flags, not automatic passes. A rep who says "I'm skeptical of AI but I'm curious to learn how your team uses it" is not red-flagging. A rep who says "AI isn't really sales" is.

### WS-09-14: The AI Ramp Track for 30-60-90
**AI Layer — Onboarding Adds**

*Extend the 30-60-90 plan with an AI tool ramp track.*

**Day 30 — AI tooling basics:**
- Logged into all team AI tools
- Completed the team's AI tool orientation
- Used AI for at least 3 real tasks (with manager review)
- Articulates the team's "good AI use" vs. "lazy AI use" standards

**Day 60 — AI integration into workflow:**
- AI is integrated into their prospecting workflow
- Editing and iterating on AI outputs, not sending first drafts
- Brings AI-generated call analysis to 1:1s without prompting
- Asks good questions about where AI helps and where it doesn't

**Day 90 — AI judgment:**
- Can articulate when AI is the right tool and when it isn't for their territory
- Teaching other reps specific AI techniques they've figured out
- Quality control on AI output is automatic, not a manager prompt
- Contributes to team's prompt library / best practices

**What "lazy AI use" looks like for new hires** (watch for this at every checkpoint):
- Sending AI-generated emails without personalization
- Using AI to generate calls that sound robotic
- Avoiding discovery by "asking AI"
- Pasting AI outputs into CRM without reading them

## Role-Play Scenarios

---

### RP-09-01: Full 90-Min Sarah Interview Walkthrough

**Setup:** Sarah, experienced rep from an adjacent company, interviewing for a senior role on your team. She has strong capability signals. You need to assess coachability and fit in depth.

**Manager goal:** Run all four parts. Probe for coachability specifically. Don't let "capability strong" blind you.

**Debrief:**
- Did you probe coachability with real questions?
- Did her answers hold up under specifics?
- Would you hire her?

---

### RP-09-02: Red Flag Marcus Interview

**Setup:** Marcus looks great on paper. In the interview, he blames a former manager for a quota miss. He deflects a weakness question. When asked about hard feedback, he describes one episode where "the feedback wasn't really accurate."

**Manager goal:** Name the red flags in real time (to yourself). Decide if they're dealbreakers or coachable.

**Debrief:**
- How many red flags did you catch?
- Which were dealbreakers, which were conversations?
- Did you probe further or let them slide?

---

### RP-09-03: The Skill Assessment

**Setup:** A candidate is running a mock discovery call with you. They're asking good questions but they're generic — they could be asking any company.

**Manager goal:** Give live feedback. See how they receive it. Adjust scoring based on how they adapt.

**Debrief:**
- Did you give specific feedback?
- Did they adjust in real time?
- What did the feedback response tell you about coachability?

---

### RP-09-04: The Reference Call

**Setup:** You're calling a former manager of a candidate. The manager says "she was great." You need specifics.

**Manager goal:** Ask questions that surface real information. Don't settle for rubber-stamps.

**Debrief:**
- Did you get specific examples, not generic praise?
- Did you ask what she needed to work on?
- Did you catch any hesitation or diplomatic phrasing?

---

### RP-09-05: Exit at Day 90

**Setup:** A new hire is at 42% of ramp quota at day 85. You've had weekly coaching. The gap isn't closing. You're ending employment this week.

**Manager goal:** Run the firing conversation (Module 03 frameworks). Brief, kind, clear. No over-explanation.

**Debrief:**
- Was it under 3 minutes?
- Did you center yourself or them?
- Did you have HR lined up?

---

### RP-09-06: AI Fluency Probe — The Curious Skeptic (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A candidate, when asked about AI, says: "Honestly, I'm skeptical of it. I think most of my peers are using it as a crutch. But I'd want to understand how your team uses it before I said it didn't work."

**Manager goal:** Distinguish between real skepticism (good) and resistance (red flag). Probe further.

**Debrief:**
- Did you probe for curiosity under the skepticism?
- Did the candidate demonstrate openness to learning?
- Hire or pass?

---

### RP-09-07: AI Fluency Probe — The Overconfident User (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A candidate, when asked about AI, says: "Oh I use ChatGPT for everything. I have like 50 prompts saved. AI writes better than I do."

**Manager goal:** Probe for judgment and discipline. Check whether the claimed fluency is real.

**Debrief:**
- Did you get past the performance into real examples?
- Did the candidate demonstrate editing discipline or just volume?
- Hire or pass?

---

### RP-09-08: The AI-Avoider Rep in an Interview (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A candidate with strong traditional sales skills dismisses AI: "I prefer to do real selling. I've been doing this 15 years and I don't need a bot to write my emails."

**Manager goal:** Decide if this is a dealbreaker or a conversation. If it's a conversation — have it.

**Debrief:**
- Did you name the concern?
- Did you probe whether it's identity ("I don't want to") or capability ("I haven't learned it")?
- What would have to be true for this to be coachable vs. a pass?

---

### RP-09-09: New Hire 30-Day Check-In with AI Ramp Track (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A new hire is at day 30. She's doing well on product and process. She's still drafting outreach fully manually and hasn't used any of the team's AI tools.

**Manager goal:** Raise it as a coaching conversation, not a mandate. Find out what's under it (unfamiliarity, skepticism, avoidance).

**Debrief:**

- Did you find out what's under it?

### RP-09-10: The Hiring Debate with a Peer Manager (AI Layer)

**Setup:** You're debriefing a candidate with a peer manager on your hiring panel. Your peer wants to hire. You're uncomfortable — candidate's AI fluency was weak. Your peer says "we can teach that."
