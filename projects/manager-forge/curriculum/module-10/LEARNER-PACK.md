# Module 10 — Motivation & Culture
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*Culture is what happens when you're not in the room. Motivation is what drives the work when no one's watching. This module gives you the Five Demotivators framework, the stated-vs-actual culture audit, the motivation spectrum, and the culture rituals that keep a team healthy. The AI layer adds a sixth demotivator — AI-related uncertainty — which is currently the single most common disengagement driver on SaaS sales teams and nobody's talking about it.*

---

## Core Frameworks

**The Five Demotivators** — The specific, diagnosable reasons people disengage:
1. **No clarity** — They don't know what's expected, what success looks like, or where they stand.
2. **Unfair treatment** — Different standards for different people, visible inequity, perceived favoritism.
3. **No growth** — The job stopped teaching them something two quarters ago.
4. **Disconnection** — They don't feel known, seen, or part of something.
5. **Lack of trust in leadership** — They've watched leadership say one thing and do another.

**Stated Culture vs. Actual Culture** — What you say on posters vs. what happens when the pressure's on. Actual culture is what survives a bad quarter.

**The Motivation Spectrum** — Intrinsic (the work itself drives them) → Extrinsic (money, recognition) → Toxic (fear, ego, avoidance). Each rep sits somewhere. Know where each one is.

**Culture Rituals** — The small, regular practices that define a team's operating norms. Without rituals, culture is just words.

### WS-10-13: The Fairness Question
**AI Layer — When AI Creates a Performance Gap**

*Rep A uses AI effectively. Rep B doesn't. Rep A is now at 140% of quota, Rep B is at 85%. What do you do?*

**Step 1: Diagnose the gap:**

| Possible cause | Applies to your team? |
|---|---|
| Effort gap — Rep B isn't trying to learn the tools | ☐ |
| Access gap — Rep B doesn't have the tools, training, or support | ☐ |
| Talent variance — Rep B tries but struggles with the tools | ☐ |
| Belief gap — Rep B doesn't think AI is legitimate sales work | ☐ |
| Ambiguity — You actually don't know which of the above it is | ☐ |

**Step 2: Different causes need different responses:**

| Cause | Response |
|---|---|
| Effort | Performance conversation — same standard as any other skill gap |
| Access | Fix the access problem — it's your responsibility, not theirs |
| Talent variance | Coach harder, adjust expectations, or exit — same as any skill gap |
| Belief | Identity conversation — address the belief directly |
| Ambiguity | Stop guessing; have the conversation |

## Role-Play Scenarios

---

### RP-10-01: The Rep Thinking of Leaving

**Setup:** Your high performer has been quieter than usual for three weeks. She just declined an optional team dinner. You suspect she's interviewing.

**Manager goal:** Raise it directly. Not an accusation — a real question. Find out what's under it.

**Debrief:**
- Did you raise it without being weird about it?
- Did you find the real issue?
- Did the conversation leave her more or less likely to stay?

---

### RP-10-02: The Toxic Star Performer

**Setup:** Your top rep is making numbers but treating teammates poorly. You've heard complaints. Two teammates have said they're considering leaving because of him.

**Manager goal:** Name the tension between results and culture. Hold him to both. Don't let results excuse culture damage.

**Debrief:**
- Did you lead with the behavior or the numbers?
- Did you hold the line or back down?
- Did you protect the team?

---

### RP-10-03: The Demotivated Middle

**Setup:** A solid mid-performer has plateaued. Numbers are fine but not growing. Energy is flat. She's been here 3 years.

**Manager goal:** Diagnose the demotivator. Name what's happening. Build a growth path or surface that the growth isn't here.

**Debrief:**
- Which demotivator did you identify?
- Did you have the real conversation or go around it?
- Did you offer growth that was actually growth?

---

### RP-10-04: The Culture-Breaking Decision

**Setup:** Leadership has made a decision that contradicts your stated culture (example: cutting comp without notice; firing without the process you'd promised). You have to communicate it to the team.

**Manager goal:** Don't lie, don't undermine leadership, don't pretend to agree. Tell the truth within what you can say.

**Debrief:**
- Did you tell the truth?
- Did you execute cleanly while being honest about your position?
- Did the team feel leveled with or played?

---

### RP-10-05: The Sixth Demotivator Conversation (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A rep, in a 1:1, finally says what they've been holding: "Am I going to be replaced by AI in two years?"

**Manager goal:** Don't false-reassure. Don't doom-cast. Engage with the real question.

**Debrief:**
- Did you give a real answer or a PR answer?
- Did you name what you don't know?
- Did the rep feel like you told them the truth?

---

### RP-10-06: The AI Fairness Conversation (AI Layer)

**Setup:** Two reps of equal tenure, one at 140% of quota (AI-fluent), one at 85% (still working manually). The 85% rep asks you: "Is it fair that we're on the same quota when he's using AI and I'm not?"

**Manager goal:** Diagnose the gap (effort, access, talent, belief). Respond to the specific cause. Don't blanket-apologize or blanket-dismiss.

**Debrief:**
- Did you diagnose before responding?
- Did you hold a fair standard?
- Did the rep leave with a real path forward?

---

### RP-10-07: The AI Skeptic on Your Team (AI Layer)

**Setup:** A long-tenured rep is loudly skeptical of AI. Makes comments in team meetings that undermine team experimentation. Other reps are starting to hold back.

**Manager goal:** Address the behavior without crushing the skepticism. Separate "you don't have to love it" from "you don't get to poison it for the team."

**Debrief:**
- Did you name the behavior clearly?
- Did you honor the legitimate skepticism under it?
- Did the rep leave the conversation with a workable position?

---

### RP-10-08: The Leadership AI Mandate You Don't Believe In (AI Layer)

**Setup:** Leadership has mandated that every rep use [Tool X]. You don't fully believe it's the right tool. You have to roll it out to the team Monday.

**Manager goal:** Use the Inform-Advocate-Execute framework from Module 02. Don't fake enthusiasm. Don't torpedo the rollout.

**Debrief:**
- Did you do the honest version?
- Did the team feel leveled with?
- Did you protect the mandate without pretending?

## Quick Reference — The Demotivators & The Layer Below

```
THE FIVE DEMOTIVATORS              →   THE FIX

No clarity                         →   Name expectations specifically
Unfair treatment                   →   Apply standards consistently
No growth                          →   Create a real growth path or say so
Disconnection                      →   Know each rep; run real 1:1s
Lack of trust in leadership        →   Tell the truth; execute cleanly

THE SIXTH (AI-ERA)                 →

AI-related uncertainty             →   Acknowledge; set expectations; build loops
```

**The Three-Phase AI Communication Framework:**
1. Acknowledge what you know and don't know
2. Set clear expectations about "good AI use" vs. "lazy AI use"
3. Build feedback loops — make it safe to raise concerns

**The Fairness Question Diagnostic:**
- Is the gap effort, access, talent variance, belief, or ambiguity?
- Different causes need different responses.
- Do the diagnosis before the conversation.

**The one question to ask yourself every month:**
*What have I not said out loud to my team about what's changing? Because culture is what I say AND what I refuse to say.*

**Andrew's note:** Reps don't leave because things are hard. They leave because they don't know where they stand. In the AI era, "where you stand" now includes "where you stand on the thing that's reshaping your job." Name it. Don't leave it to rumor.
