# Module 11 — Time Management & Scaling
## Foundations Library | Learner Pack

*The biggest lie in management is that you'll "find the time." You won't. You'll make the time by killing something else or you'll stay buried. This module gives you the Time Audit, the Three Time Buckets, the Priority Matrix, and the Delegation Method that actually scales. The AI layer changes the math: tasks that used to be "only you" are now "AI + your judgment," which means the "only you" column shrinks, and what you're left with should be the highest-leverage work of your career.*

---

## Core Frameworks

**The Three Time Buckets** — Every task falls into one of three:
1. **Core manager work** — Only you can do this. (Coaching, hiring, 1:1s, high-stakes conversations)
2. **Delegatable** — A rep or another person can do this if you teach them.
3. **Automatable** — A tool, template, or workflow can do this.

**The Priority Matrix** — A 2x2 grid: High impact + only you / High impact + delegatable / Low impact + delegatable / Low impact + automate. The only quadrant that should be eating your time is the first. If it isn't, the week's going to hurt.

**The 3-Time Delegation Method** — Show → Watch → Step back. You do it and they watch. They do it and you watch. They do it and you don't watch. Skip any step and delegation fails.

**The Time Audit** — Track your time for one week in 30-minute increments. Categorize. Be horrified. Change something.

**Consistency Over Heroics** — The manager who protects their time wins. The one who grinds through every week loses eventually — to burnout, to the team falling apart, to quality degradation, to all three.

### WS-11-03: High-Value vs. Low-Value Delegation
**Sub-Module 2 — Delegating Well**

*Not all delegation is equal. Some builds people. Some just offloads busywork.*

- Teaches a new skill
- Increases rep's autonomy
- Rep can grow into the role

## Role-Play Scenarios

---

### RP-11-01: The "I'll Just Do It Myself" Moment

**Setup:** A rep brings you a half-done deliverable. It would take 20 minutes to finish. It would take an hour to coach him through finishing it.

**Manager goal:** Don't take the work back. Coach him through it. Accept that it's worse this week in service of being better long-term.

**Debrief:**
- Did you coach or just take it?
- What was the actual cost of coaching vs. doing?
- Is the long-term bet worth it?

---

### RP-11-02: The Leadership Meeting Invite

**Setup:** Your VP invites you to a new weekly cross-functional sync. Low agenda, unclear value to you or your team.

**Manager goal:** Decline gracefully. Make the case with data, not complaint.

**Debrief:**
- Did you decline cleanly?
- Did you offer an alternative?
- Did the relationship survive?

---

### RP-11-03: The Burnout Check-In

**Setup:** Your manager notices you've been working weekends and sends you a "hey, how are you really doing?" message.

**Manager goal:** Be honest. Don't perform strength. Use it as data.

**Debrief:**
- Did you tell the truth?
- Did you surface what would actually help?

---

### RP-11-04: The Delegation That Isn't Working

**Setup:** You delegated something three weeks ago. The rep isn't doing it well. You're tempted to take it back.

**Manager goal:** Diagnose whether this is a "skip the Show step" problem, a "wrong rep" problem, or a "bad delegation" problem. Fix the real issue.

**Debrief:**
- Did you fix the delegation or end it?
- Did you learn something for next time?

---

### RP-11-05: The AI Automation That's Producing Garbage (AI Layer)

**Setup:** You set up an AI workflow to auto-generate weekly pipeline updates. They've been getting worse over the last month. Prompts are drifting. Nobody's been validating outputs.

**Manager goal:** Diagnose why it's degrading. Fix or kill it. Don't let a bad automation rot silently.

**Debrief:**
- Did you catch it before leadership noticed?
- Did you fix or kill?

---

### RP-11-06: The Hour You Got Back From AI (AI Layer)

**Setup:** AI-assisted pipeline prep is saving you an hour every Sunday. Your first instinct is to use it for catching up on Slack.

**Manager goal:** Use it for something actually higher-leverage. Name the thing. Protect the time.

**Debrief:**
- Did you use it for higher-leverage work or just spread your attention thinner?
- What did you commit the hour to?

**The one question to ask yourself every Friday:**
*What am I still doing manually that should be delegated, automated, or killed?*

**The AI-era corollary:** The "only you" quadrant just got smaller. That's not a threat — it's a concentration. What's left in that quadrant is the highest-leverage work of your career. Make sure you're spending your time there and not reconstituting the manual work AI freed you from.

**Andrew's note:** Nobody scales by working harder. They scale by being ruthless about what deserves their time. AI didn't change the principle. It made the stakes higher — because the managers who lean into it get back 5+ hours a week, and the ones who don't stay buried. Those hours compound.
