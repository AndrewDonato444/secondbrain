## The Old Job Description

*What "being a good manager" used to mean:*

| Activity | Where you spent your time |
|---|---|
| Run pipeline reviews | Asking reps "what's in your pipeline?" and tracking answers manually |
| Coach reps | Based on your gut and whatever the rep told you in the 1:1 |
| Forecast | Building the number yourself from rep-reported data |
| Monitor activity | Reviewing call logs, email counts, manually |
| Communicate upward | Summarizing your own summaries into management decks |
| Admin | CRM updates, data entry, activity logging |
| Write outreach | Helping reps draft or review cold emails |
| Meeting prep | Reading previous notes the night before |

---

## The New Job Description

*What "being a good manager" looks like in 2026:*

**What AI Should Own** *(your delegation list)*

- Cold outreach first drafts
- CRM data entry and stage updates
- Meeting summaries (Gong, Chorus, Otter)
- Pipeline data compilation and health alerts
- Rep activity tracking and flags
- First-pass forecast modeling
- Onboarding content generation and quizzes
- Email follow-up sequences
- 1:1 prep notes (initial pull from call data)
- Performance report compilation

→ *If you're doing any of these manually in 2026, you are doing AI's job. Every hour here is an hour you're not doing what only you can do.*

---

**Where Humans Still Win** *(your irreplaceable work)*

| Capability | What it looks like in practice |
|---|---|
| **Deal judgment** | Reading deal health from context the data doesn't capture — rep confidence vs. actual progress, relationship risk, political dynamics in the account |
| **Synthesis** | Taking AI output and turning it into a call — "the AI says X, but here's what I actually think, and here's why" |
| **Prompt engineering** | Knowing what to ask the AI, how to ask it, and what to do with the answer — the skill that determines how good your AI outputs are |
| **Coaching rep behavior** | Not just coaching output (numbers), but coaching the behaviors underneath them — and knowing the difference |
| **Reading the unsaid** | Picking up on what a rep isn't telling you, what a prospect isn't saying, what the hesitation actually means |
| **Managing dynamics** | Understanding team morale, peer tension, motivation gaps — none of which show up in the data |
| **Making calls under ambiguity** | When the data is contradictory, when the situation is novel, when there's no playbook — you decide |
| **Being a human** | Caring about reps as people, building relationships, being the person someone trusts enough to tell the truth to |

## The Abdication Risk

*The failure mode on the other side of this.*

Abdication happens when you delegate **judgment** instead of **execution**.

- The pipeline review where you read the AI summary and call it a coaching conversation
- The Gong summary that becomes the 1:1 instead of informing it
- The forecast that AI generates and you submit without a read
- The 1:1 prep that AI writes and you deliver without your own take

**The tell:** When's the last time your read of a situation was different from what the data said?

If the answer is "never" — you're abdicating.

## Your Three Archetypes (to recognize on your team)

You'll see these patterns in your reps. Your coaching approach is different for each.

| Archetype | What you see | Your job |
|---|---|---|
| **The Over-Reliant Rep** | Generic output, dropping quality, outsourcing thinking to the tool | Coach on what they added — "what did you put in this that AI couldn't?" |
| **The AI-Avoider** | Slower than peers, resistant, usually experienced and identity-attached to the old way | Find the one use case where AI makes their best skill sharper. Start small. |
| **The AI-Native with Other Gaps** | Fast, comfortable with tools, but gaps that were hidden before are now visible in the data | The data is telling you things it couldn't before. Look at what's surfacing. |

## The Both/And Operating Philosophy

This is not about AI OR human judgment. It's about knowing which is which.

```
AI handles:          You handle:
────────────────────────────────────────
Execution            Judgment
Volume               Synthesis
Speed                Context
First drafts         The read
Data compilation     What the data means
Pattern matching     What the pattern means
```

The manager who figures out this division — and actually lives it — has more time, better insights, and a more coachable team than anyone around them.

That's the job now.
