# FW-W2-01 — AI-Augmented Pipeline Review Protocol

**Manager Forge — AI Spine | Week 2**  
**Type:** Framework (one-pager)  
**Use:** Facilitator reference + participant handout

---

## The Core Idea

Your pipeline review has two jobs:

1. **Gather the status of every deal** — AI does this now
2. **Interpret what the data means and coach your reps** — that's your job

If you're still doing both, you're spending half your management time doing a machine's job.

This protocol gives you a three-step sequence that takes 30 minutes of prep and turns your pipeline review from a status update into a coaching session.

---

## The 3-Step Protocol

---

### STEP 1 — Let AI Run the First Pass
**When:** Day before the review meeting (or morning of, if weekly)  
**Time:** 5 minutes

Export your pipeline health summary from whatever tool you use:
- **Gong Forecast** → AI deal summary, activity flags, next-step predictions
- **Clari** → Deal health scores, stage progression, at-risk flags
- **Salesforce Einstein** → Win probability scores, engagement activity
- **No AI tool** → Sort your pipeline spreadsheet by days-in-stage and last activity

You're not analyzing yet. You're letting the tool sort by urgency. Your job in this step is to get the sorted list in front of you.

**Output:** A ranked list of deals — high activity → low activity, or high risk → low risk. Whatever the tool gives you. This is your raw material.

---

### STEP 2 — Manager Reviews for Systematic AI Gaps
**When:** 30 minutes before the review meeting  
**Time:** 25–30 minutes

The AI output is a starting point, not a verdict. Scan every deal the tool flagged (or every deal in your top-priority tier) through these three lenses:

**🔍 Recency Bias Check**  
*AI overweights recent activity, regardless of quality.*  
→ For any deal flagged "healthy" because of recent activity: what was that touchpoint, and was there a response?  
→ If the rep emailed last Thursday but hasn't heard back, the deal isn't healthy — it's quiet.  
→ Mark it: ⚠️ *Check the touchpoint*

**🔍 Stage Inflation Check**  
*AI treats stage as ground truth. Reps push deals forward too early.*  
→ For any deal at Proposal or Advanced for 20+ days: when did the rep actually send something, and what was the prospect's last response?  
→ A deck sent 3 weeks ago with no reply is not a Proposal-stage deal — it's a deal that needs to be moved or disqualified.  
→ Mark it: ⚠️ *Confirm stage is real*

**🔍 Size Distortion Check**  
*AI flags large deals with low recent activity as at-risk. This is often wrong for enterprise deals.*  
→ For any large deal flagged at-risk due to inactivity: is this a normal enterprise cadence or an actual problem?  
→ If you know the deal is in legal review, a 3-week gap isn't a risk signal — it's the process.  
→ Mark it: ✅ *Expected cadence — not a flag*

**Output:** 2–3 deals where your read differs from the AI output. These become your meeting agenda. Write one coaching question for each.

---

### STEP 3 — Meeting Opens With Your Synthesis
**When:** The pipeline review itself  
**Time:** Replaces "walk me through your pipeline"

You open the meeting. Not the rep.

**The opening sentence format:**  
*"I reviewed the AI summary before we got on. Here's my read: [your 2-3 observations]. I want to spend our time on [specific deals]. Let's start with [Deal A]. Tell me what happened after you [last touchpoint]."*

The meeting is now a coaching conversation, not a status update.

**What this does:**
- Reps stop coming to reviews as reporters and start coming as problem-solvers
- You never spend 10 minutes per rep on status you could have gotten from the tool
- Every rep leaves with a specific next move on a specific deal

---

## The Time Math

| Old format | New format |
|---|---|
| 0 min of prep | 30 min of prep |
| 60 min of status-gathering | 15 min of synthesis + 45 min of coaching |
| Manager learns what's in the pipeline | Reps leave with next moves |

You're not adding time. You're moving it.

---

## Where AI Misses — Quick Reference

| Failure Mode | What AI Does | What the Manager Does |
|---|---|---|
| **Recency Bias** | Flags "healthy" if recent activity exists, regardless of quality | Checks whether the touchpoint got a response |
| **Stage Inflation** | Treats stated stage as accurate | Confirms the prospect's behavior matches the stage |
| **Size Distortions** | Flags large deals with slow cadence as at-risk | Distinguishes enterprise timing from actual stall |

---

## When You Don't Have an AI Tool

The protocol still works. Run it with a sorted spreadsheet:
1. Sort by **days in current stage** (longest first)
2. Sort by **days since last activity** (longest first)
3. Look for deals where those two numbers don't match what you'd expect
4. Those are your 2-3 coaching questions

The failure modes exist in every pipeline. The tool just makes them easier to spot — or easier to miss.

---

## One-Line Summary

*AI generates the first-pass health summary. You review for systematic AI gaps. The meeting opens with your synthesis and 2-3 coaching questions — not "walk me through your pipeline."*
