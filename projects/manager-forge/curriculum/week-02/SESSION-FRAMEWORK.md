# Week 2 — Session Framework
## "Pipeline Reviews That Don't Waste Everyone's Time"

**Program:** Manager Forge — AI Spine  
**Session:** Week 2 of 6  
**Format:** Live, 90 minutes via Zoom  
**Date:** July 20 (or 27), 2026 — Cohort 1  
**Facilitator:** Andrew Donato

---

## Session Goal

By the end of this session, every manager has redesigned their weekly pipeline review — from a status-update meeting into a coaching conversation where AI does the first pass and they do the synthesis. They leave with a concrete one-page format they're running with their team next Monday.

**The shift:** Stop being the person who asks "what's in your pipeline?" Start being the person who opens with "the AI flagged three deals stuck at proposal — let's talk about why."

**Monday morning artifact:** WS-W2-01 completed — "My AI-Augmented Weekly Review Template"  
*(AI first-pass summary section + manager synthesis section + 2-3 coaching questions pre-written)*

## 90-Minute Run of Show

---

### BLOCK 1 — Opening Frame (15 min)

**[0:00 – 3:00] Poll read-back + the real question**

Andrew reads the pre-session poll results. If half the room walked into their last pipeline review without reviewing the data: "That means you walked in to do the thing AI was supposed to do. You were the data-gatherer. That's the problem we're solving tonight."

If most did review the data first: "Good. But I want to challenge how you reviewed it. Because reviewing data the old way is still doing AI's job — just faster."

Either way, land on the same frame: **The pipeline review has two jobs. AI can do one of them. Most managers are still doing both.**

---

**[3:00 – 10:00] The two versions of the same meeting**

*Tell it as a story, not a list.*

Version 1 — The status update:
> "Manager opens with a rep. 'Walk me through your pipeline.' Rep walks through every deal. Manager asks clarifying questions. Rep explains. Manager takes notes. Repeat for every rep. 60 minutes gone. Manager now knows what's in the pipeline. Meeting ends. No one is better at selling."

Version 2 — The coaching conversation:
> "Manager opens with a rep. 'I ran the AI summary before we got on. You've got three deals stuck at proposal for more than 21 days — these ones. I want to spend our time on those. What's the story on [Deal A]?' Rep explains. Manager probes. Coaching happens. That rep leaves the call with a next move. Repeat. Meeting is 45 minutes. Every rep leaves with something."

The difference isn't the rep's preparation. It's whether the manager did the AI's job or their own.

---

**[10:00 – 15:00] Show the actual format — Andrew's real pipeline summary**

Screen share: Andrew's actual weekly AI-generated pipeline summary (or a sanitized version showing the structure).

Walk through what it gives you:
- Deals by stage + days in stage
- Activity-flagged accounts (no email/call in X days)
- Pipeline velocity vs. prior period
- Rep-by-rep health score

Then: "Here's what it *can't* tell you. And this is important — because if you don't know this, you'll trust the output in exactly the places you shouldn't."

Tee up the next block.

---

### BLOCK 2 — Where AI Misses in Pipeline Analysis (20 min)

*This is the most important teaching block of the session. It's what separates a manager who uses AI tools from a manager who understands them.*

**[15:00 – 35:00] The three failure modes**

**Failure Mode 1: Recency Bias**

AI tools weight recent activity heavily. A rep who sent one email last Thursday after two weeks of silence looks better than a rep who had a deep discovery call three weeks ago and has been strategically waiting.

The AI sees: "Email sent 4 days ago. Activity: normal."  
The manager knows: "That rep spammed a one-liner to restart a deal that's probably dead."

**How to catch it:** Any time the AI shows "recent activity" on a deal that felt stale, ask the rep: "Tell me about that touchpoint. What was the response?" If there's no response, the deal is still stale.

**The coaching question:** "The AI shows activity here. Walk me through what that conversation actually was."

---

**Failure Mode 2: Stage Inflation**

Reps push deals to later stages too early — not always intentionally. A discovery call that went well lands in "Qualification" before the rep has actually confirmed budget, decision-maker, or timeline. The AI treats stage as ground truth.

The AI sees: "$80K deal at Proposal stage. High value."  
The manager knows: "My rep moved that to Proposal the day they sent a deck. The client hasn't responded in two weeks."

**How to catch it:** Stage inflation shows up as age. If a deal has been "at Proposal" for 30+ days and there's no back-and-forth, it probably shouldn't be there.

**The coaching question:** "That deal's been at Proposal since [date]. What happened after you sent it?"

---

**Failure Mode 3: Size Distortions**

AI health scores often treat large deals with low activity as risky and small deals with high activity as healthy. This is backwards for enterprise reps — big deals move slowly by design, with strategic gaps between touches.

The AI sees: "$200K deal, 18 days since last activity. Risk: High."  
The manager knows: "That's an enterprise deal with a 6-month cycle. We're waiting on their legal review. That's normal."

**How to catch it:** Know your pipeline by deal type. Enterprise deals have different cadences than velocity deals. The AI doesn't know the difference unless you've configured it to.

**The coaching question:** "The AI flagged this as at-risk because of inactivity. What's your read on where this deal actually is?"

---

*Wrap this block with the point:*

"AI does the first pass. You do the interpretation. And interpretation requires knowing where the tool is blind. If you know these three failure modes, you will catch things that would otherwise slip through to a blown forecast."

---

### BLOCK 3 — The Protocol Walkthrough (15 min)

**[35:00 – 50:00] FW-W2-01 — The AI-Augmented Pipeline Review Protocol**

Screen share FW-W2-01. Walk through it live.

**Step 1 — AI generates the first-pass summary (day before the meeting)**
- Run your Gong Forecast, Clari, Salesforce Einstein, or equivalent
- Export the health summary, deal list by stage, activity flags
- Note: you're not reading it in detail yet — you're letting the tool sort by urgency

**Step 2 — Manager reviews for the three failure modes (30 minutes, morning of)**
- Scan each flagged deal through the three lenses: recency bias, stage inflation, size distortions
- Mark 2-3 deals where the AI output doesn't match your read
- Write one coaching question for each marked deal
- This is your agenda. Not a status walkthrough — a list of specific questions.

**Step 3 — Meeting opens with your synthesis**
- "I reviewed the AI summary. Here's my read. We've got three deals I want to spend time on." 
- The rep never leads. You lead with the AI synthesis, not with "walk me through your pipeline."
- Remaining meeting = coaching conversation on those 2-3 deals + anything the rep flags

*Andrew: "The prep takes 30 minutes. It saves 30 minutes of status-gathering in the meeting and turns the other 30 into actual coaching. That's the trade."*

---

### BLOCK 4 — Workshop: Your Real Pipeline (25 min)

**[50:00 – 75:00] Live workshop with actual data**

*Managers use their real pipeline data from WS-07-03 and WS-07-04 pre-work. If they didn't do pre-work, they estimate.*

**Round 1 — WS-07-01 + WS-07-02 (10 min)**
Map team pipeline by stage. What does the shape tell you?
- Prompt: "What pattern do you see? Which rep would the AI flag as healthy that you'd actually flag as at-risk?"
- Breakout rooms optional (2 min each): share one pattern you noticed

**Round 2 — WS-07-08: Coaching Opportunities from Pipeline (8 min)**
For each health pattern the AI would surface: what's the coaching question the manager now asks?
- Prompt: "Don't answer the pattern. Write the question you'd ask the rep about it."
- Key reminder: AI gives you the diagnosis. You provide the interpretation and the coaching.

**Round 3 — WS-07-10: Coach the Conversation Script (7 min)**
Pick one rep. Write out the actual opening you'd use in your next pipeline review — using the 3-step protocol.
- Prompt: "Write your actual first sentence. Not 'I'd open by reviewing the AI summary.' Write the sentence."

*Debrief: 2-3 volunteers share their opening sentence. Andrew provides real-time coaching on each one.*

---

### BLOCK 5 — Role-Play + Close (15 min)

**[75:00 – 88:00] RP-07-01 — Reading a Pipeline (group scenario)**

Andrew plays the rep. Cohort members play the manager.

Setup: "I'm a mid-level rep. The AI summary shows I've got 4 deals in Proposal, two of which have been there 25+ days. My overall pipeline health score is 72 — medium. My conversion rate is 18%, below team average. You've done your pre-work. You've flagged two of those Proposal deals as stage inflation. Go."

*Run for 8 minutes — rotate in 2-3 cohort members to try different opening approaches. Andrew responds as the rep would. Debrief what worked.*

The point: the manager who opens with "walk me through your pipeline" gets a 40-minute status update. The manager who opens with "I see Deal B has been at Proposal for 25 days — what happened after you sent the deck?" gets a coaching conversation in 10 minutes.

"WS-W2-01 is your takeaway. Fill it in this week. Before your next pipeline review — whether that's Monday or Thursday — run Step 2 from FW-W2-01. Thirty minutes. Write your 2-3 coaching questions. Open with your synthesis. See what happens to the meeting."

## Facilitator Notes

### Common objections to prep for:
- **"My company uses [tool] and it doesn't give me this kind of summary."** → The protocol applies regardless of tool. You're learning to review the data you do have with a different lens — looking for recency bias, stage inflation, size distortions. Gong, Clari, Salesforce, even a spreadsheet — the three failure modes exist in all of them.
- **"My reps do their own pipeline reviews. I just sit in."** → That's the problem. The pipeline review is your coaching tool, not their reporting mechanism. If the rep is running it, you've handed over the most powerful management lever you have.
- **"I don't have time to review the pipeline for 30 minutes before each review."** → Right now you're spending 60 minutes in status-gathering. We're trading 30 minutes of prep for 30 minutes of coaching. That's not more time — it's different time.

### Energy management:
- The "Where AI Misses" block is conceptually heavy. Keep it grounded in stories, not theory.
- The workshop block is the payoff — don't cut it short to accommodate a long opening.
- Role-play always runs longer than expected. Cap it hard at 88:00 to leave time for the Monday commitment.
