# FW-W4-01 — AI-Integrated 1:1 Prep Protocol

**Manager Forge — AI Spine | Week 4**  
**Type:** Framework (one-pager)  
**Use:** Facilitator reference + participant handout

---

## The Core Idea

AI gives you better pre-meeting data than any manager had five years ago. The failure mode is letting that data run your meeting.

The protocol has three steps: what to ask AI to generate, what to verify manually before you walk in, and what to never let AI generate. The result is a 1:1 that's better informed and more human — not one or the other.

---

## What AI Gets Right in 1:1 Prep

Use AI (or your CRM/call analytics tool) to pull:

**Performance trends**
- Quota attainment over the last 30, 60, 90 days
- Week-over-week activity volume (calls, emails, meetings booked)
- Pipeline health: deals by stage, days in stage, any movement since last 1:1

**Deal-specific signals**
- Deals that moved unexpectedly (forward or backward) this week
- Deals with no activity in 10+ days
- Your highest-value deals: what's the current status?

**Call analytics (if you have them)**
- Talk time ratio: is the rep listening or presenting?
- Any calls flagged for key moment absence (no clear next step, no timeline established)
- Tone/engagement signals, with the caveat below

**What to ask your tool:**  
*"Summarize [rep name]'s performance trends for the last two weeks — pipeline movement, activity volume, any deals with significant changes. Flag anything you'd recommend I review before our 1:1."*

---

## What AI Gets Wrong — Know Before You Walk In

**Recency bias**
A bad week can make a strong trend look broken. A strong week can mask a declining quarter. Before your 1:1, check the trend, not just the latest data point. If this week looks dramatically different from the last four, ask why before you ask the rep.

**Inferences about why**
Any output that sounds like: "This rep seems disengaged" or "This rep may be losing motivation" — that's not data, it's inference. Your tool generated it from patterns. It may be right. It is not a fact. Don't treat it as one.

**The rep's personal context**
AI has no idea your rep's parent just got sick, that they're going through a difficult negotiation with their landlord, or that last week's difficult call shook their confidence in a way that doesn't show up in call analytics. You know things the tool doesn't. Those things change how you open the conversation.

**The Safety Pyramid reading**
AI cannot tell you which layer of the Safety Pyramid your rep is currently at — and that's the most important piece of context for any 1:1. If your rep is at Layer 2 (they feel heard but aren't fully trusting yet), leading with data is the wrong move. The tool doesn't know that. You have to.

---

## What to Verify Manually (Before the Meeting)

Spend 3–5 minutes on this. Not data review — observation.

**Energy check from last week:** Did you notice anything in the last meeting, a Slack exchange, or a call that felt off? Flat? Lighter than usual? Tense? Write it down. That might be where you open.

**What you left on the table last week:** Did the rep start to say something and stop? Did they give you the "everything's fine" answer to something that felt unresolved? If yes, that's your opening — not the briefing.

**The one thing you're most curious about:** After reading the AI summary, what's the one thing you most want to understand — not confirm, understand? That's your first coaching question. Write it before you walk in.

---

## What to NEVER Let AI Generate

### The opening question

The opening question sets the tone for the entire conversation. It has to be human, specific to this person, and grounded in what you actually know and observed — not what the tool flagged.

**The test:** Could this question have been generated without knowing this person?  
If yes — don't ask it.

Good opening questions are specific:
- "How are you feeling about the quarter?"
- "Last week you seemed like something was on your mind. Want to pick that back up?"
- "You closed [deal] Thursday. What did that feel like?"
- "I saw you had two discovery calls back-to-back Tuesday. How did those go?"

**Not good opening questions:**
- "Your pipeline is down 15% — what happened?"
- "The AI flagged this deal — can you explain it?"
- "I want to go through your numbers first."

### Questions that require emotional safety to answer honestly

If you're asking a rep about something that requires trust — why they've been quieter, whether they're burned out, what's actually getting in the way — the AI briefing has no role in those questions. Those come from observation, relationship, and the decision to open the door.

### Your opinion about this person

The briefing tells you patterns. It does not tell you what you think of this rep's potential, where you see them in six months, or what you believe they need to hear. Those are yours. Don't outsource them to the tool.

## The Prep Sequence (Time Guide)

| Step | What you do | Time |
|---|---|---|
| AI generates first pass | Run your tool prompt; get pipeline, activity, deal summary | 2 min |
| You review the briefing | Read it. Highlight 2-3 things you want to understand better. Note what it's inferring vs. what it actually knows. | 5 min |
| Manual check | Energy from last week. What you left on the table. One thing you're genuinely curious about. | 3 min |
| Write your opening question | One sentence. Not generated. Yours. | 1 min |
| Close the briefing | Don't bring it into the meeting. You've already extracted what you need. | — |

**Total prep: 11 minutes.** If your 1:1 used to start cold or with a data review that consumed the first 10 minutes, you've just traded that for a meeting that starts with a human question and goes somewhere real.

---

## The AI Habits Coaching Moment (for when it comes up)

Your 1:1 is where AI habits get coached — not a separate conversation, not a policy reminder.

**When to raise it:** When you've seen a pattern in the data (response rate dropping while volume climbs, inconsistency between AI summaries and rep status updates, sudden return to fully manual processes) or when the rep surfaces it.

**How to open:** Name the observable pattern. Don't name the AI. "Your response rate is down while your volume is up. Help me understand what's changed in your outreach approach."

**What good looks like:** The rep leaves with a specific behavior to try or track — not a judgment about their tool use. Coaching AI behavior is coaching results behavior. Same standard.

---

## One-Line Summary

*AI generates your prep context. You generate the questions. The briefing closes before the meeting opens.*
