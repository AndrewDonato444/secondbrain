# CRO Outbound — Email Sequence

**Play:** Senior-leader outbound ("evaluate your managers" wedge)
**Source doc:** `gtm/senior-leader-outbound.md` + `cro-discovery-framework.md`
**Goal of sequence:** Book a 20–30 min diagnostic call. Not to pitch Manager Forge. The manager evaluation IS the wedge.
**Voice:** Practitioner to practitioner. VP of Sales who builds his own AI systems. Short. Specific. No warmup, no "hope this finds you well," no sign-off flattery.
**Format:** Plain text. Four emails + one LI DM variant. Spacing defaults to day 1 / day 4 / day 9 / day 16 — adjust by ICP response signals.

---

## Framing — read before using

The whole sequence is built on one observation: a CRO does not have time to develop their frontline managers, and the cost of that is showing up in pipeline rigor, rep retention, and forecast quality. The wedge is a free (for now) read on their manager bench. If the eval surfaces a gap, enrollment is a natural next step. If it doesn't, the CRO got a useful diagnostic. Either way, Andrew earns the conversation by showing up like an operator, not a vendor.

**What these emails never do:**
- Mention Manager Forge in emails 1–2. The program is downstream of the eval.
- List pricing, cohort dates, or "what's included."
- Use the word "training." The CRO has bought training. It didn't work.
- Claim insight they'd get from a blog post. Every email has to contain something a vendor wouldn't say.

## Email 1 — day 1

**Subject line A:** Who's developing your managers?
**Subject line B:** Your managers and the AI shift — quick question
**Subject line C:** 20 minutes on your manager bench

---

{First},

You probably don't have time to develop your frontline managers. Getting them to develop their reps is already a full-time stress.

The part nobody talks about: most of them are running a 2022 playbook on a 2026 team and it's already showing up in pipeline rigor, not just tool adoption.

I'm a VP of Sales running ~10 reps on systems I built myself (pipeline review protocol, coaching archetype framework, AI fluency hiring rubric — all live in production). Before I pitch anything, I'm offering senior leaders a free read on their manager bench: where each manager is on AI fluency, coaching maturity, pipeline rigor, hiring judgment. 20 min call plus a short questionnaire. You get the written read either way.

Worth 20 minutes?

Andrew

---

**CTA pattern:** Single question. No calendar link in email 1 — force the reply. Calendar link shows up in email 2 once they've engaged or in email 3 if they haven't.

## Email 2 — day 4

**Subject line A:** Re: who's developing your managers?
**Subject line B:** The read I'd do on your bench

---

{First},

Following up on the note from Monday.

Quick color on what the bench read actually surfaces — it's six diagnostic questions, one per gap:

- Can each manager articulate their own new job description, or are they still describing the 2022 version?
- Are pipeline reviews still status updates, or are managers reading AI-generated deal health and synthesizing?
- Are they coaching the three rep archetypes (AI-over-reliant, AI-avoider, AI-native with other gaps) differently, or running one playbook on all three?
- Are 1:1s more structured or more chaotic than a year ago?
- Is AI fluency in your hiring rubric yet?
- What's the mood on the teams — and do the managers know?

I can usually tell within 20 minutes which of the six is loudest for a given bench. That read is the deliverable.

Here's the calendar: {link}. 20-min slot. I do 2-3 of these a week.

Andrew

## Email 3 — day 9

**Subject line A:** One thing to watch on your managers
**Subject line B:** If now's not the right time

---

{First},

If now's not the time for the bench read, fine — here's what I'd watch for on your managers this quarter, take it or leave it:

The tell isn't whether they're using AI. It's whether their 1:1s are more structured than a year ago, or more chaotic. Managers who added AI prep without changing the meeting format almost always report "more chaotic." That's the leading indicator. Pipeline quality follows it by a quarter.

If that resonates — {calendar link}.

If it doesn't — appreciate the read and I'll get out of your inbox.

Andrew

## Spacing recommendations

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | Email | Name the problem, name the wedge, ask the question |
| LI connect | 1 | LinkedIn | Fire same day as email 1 |
| LI DM | 2 | LinkedIn | Only if connect accepts |
| 2 | 4 | Email | Show the framework — six questions, specific enough to earn the call |
| 3 | 9 | Email | Give away a tell (1:1 chaos signal) — proves the eval isn't generic |
| 4 | 16 | Email | Clean break — "closing the thread" |

**After day 16:** drop out of active cadence. Move to a quarterly nurture — send one BOL episode link per quarter with a single-sentence context line. No re-pitching the eval. If they come back, they come back warm.

---

## What to calibrate after the first 10 sends

- Which subject line opens best — "Who's developing your managers?" tends to win in testing but read the data.
- Whether email 2's six-question list is the right length. If reply rate is low, cut to three.
- Whether the day 9 "give away a tell" should be a different gap than the 1:1 chaos signal — swap to whichever one is landing hardest in live discovery calls.
- Update `cro-discovery-framework.md` with what's landing on the live calls. The email copy inherits from what works in the room.
