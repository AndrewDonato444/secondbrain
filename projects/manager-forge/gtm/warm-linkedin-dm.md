# Warm LinkedIn DM — Founding Cohort

**Audience:** VP-and-below sales leaders already in Andrew's network
**Goal:** Get on their radar for the Founding Cohort. Not a hard pitch — an invitation with a reason.
**Voice:** Peer-to-peer. The relationship does the work the copy doesn't have to.
**Rule:** Every DM goes out with a personal first line referencing something real. The templates below start at line two.

---

## Judgment notes

- **Never send all three at once to the same segment.** If you're not sure which tier someone is in, default down a tier. Sending a tier A message to a tier C contact reads as overfamiliar and burns the relationship.
- **The personal line is non-negotiable for tiers A and B.** If you can't write a specific, real first line, the DM doesn't go. Move them to tier C or skip.
- **No "hope you're well."** Every tier A and B DM opens on something concrete.
- **The link goes at the end.** Not in the first line. The ask is soft — "take a look if it's interesting" — not "click here."
- **No emojis. No "hey 👋".** Voice mismatch.
- **Follow-up rule:** If they don't respond within 10 days, one short nudge is fine for tier A ("Bump in case this got buried — no stress if not"). For tiers B and C, let it go.

---

## What to adapt for specific relationships

- If you know they just took a new manager role: swap in "especially given you just stepped into the {role}" as the relevance line in tier A/B.
- If they've explicitly talked about AI struggle on LinkedIn: reference that post specifically in the opener (not in a flattering way — a "yeah, I've been chewing on the same thing" way).
- If they've got a manager bench and you could also pitch them the CRO play: use the warm DM for them personally, and mention the eval at the end as a separate offer. Do not bundle.
