## Gaps — What's Missing for AI Spine Sessions

The following are capabilities the new AI spine sessions need that have no usable precursor in the archive:

**1. AI-Augmented Pipeline Review (AI as co-analyst)**
Nothing in the archive addresses how to run a pipeline review when AI has pre-processed the data. The existing Module 07 tools assume manual analysis. Need: a new asset that teaches the manager to interpret AI-generated pipeline summaries, spot where the AI missed context, and overlay judgment. WS-13-07 is adjacent but not purpose-built for this.

**2. Managing Rep AI Anxiety vs. AI Overconfidence (two failure modes)**
The archive has WS-13-01 (AI baseline) and RP-13-05 (over-reliance), but there's nothing for the rep who is terrified AI will take their job and is therefore refusing to engage with it. This is a distinct coaching scenario that needs its own role-play and coaching sequence.

**3. AI Tool Evaluation for the Sales Stack**
RP-13-04 covers recommending AI investment to leadership at a high level, but there's no structured decision framework for evaluating and selecting AI tools (Apollo, Gong, Clay, Outreach AI features, etc.). Managers need a real evaluation rubric, not just a conversation practice.

**4. Coaching Reps on Prompt Quality**
The archive has WS-13-08 (prompt engineering iterations for managers), but nothing that teaches a manager how to coach a rep to write better prompts. This is a new management skill with no analog in the archive.

**5. AI Workflow Design for the Sales Team**
Nothing in the archive addresses designing an AI-augmented sales workflow at the team level — which tasks get AI-first treatment, what the human review gates are, how to roll it out without disrupting production. This is a pure gap.

**6. Difficult Conversations (Module 03 Gap)**
As noted above, Module 03 was never built. This is a non-AI gap but critical for the new program. The archive has adjacent material (WS-06-08 uses Data/Diagnosis/Collaboration, RP-08-01 through RP-08-05 cover coaching and exit conversations) but there's no standalone difficult conversations module. For the relaunch, this either needs to be built from scratch or the adjacent assets need to be restructured into a coherent unit.

**7. Change Management for AI Rollout**
When a company rolls out a new AI tool and the manager is responsible for adoption, there's no material on how to lead that change — what to expect, how to handle resistance, how to set milestones. This is an increasingly common scenario that has no home in the archive.
