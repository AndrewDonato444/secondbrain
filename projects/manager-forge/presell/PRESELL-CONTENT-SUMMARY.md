## Judgment calls made

- **BOL thread is 5 episodes, not 4–6 on the high end.** Density matters less than leaving space for BOL's normal non-program episodes in the same window — the series keeps its positioning because it's not turning into a Manager Forge drip campaign.
- **Episode 5 carries the Manager Forge mention, not Episode 4.** Needed the mention to land the same week as the enrollment announcement post, not a week before.
- **CRO sequence has 4 emails + 1 LI DM.** Went with the longer end of the 3–5 range because the "evaluate your managers" wedge needs email 2's framework specificity to earn the call. Cutting to 3 would collapse email 2's six-question breakdown.
- **Warm DM tiers are close/warm/loose (not by role/seniority).** Relationship strength drives voice more than title — a warm VP gets the tier B copy whether they're at a Series B or an enterprise.
- **Convergence Post 3 (hiring / interview questions) overlaps thematically with BOL Episode 4.** Kept it because the open, angle, and landing line are distinct enough to pass the "two pieces of thinking, not one served twice" test. Flagged as the post to swap first if audience feedback says recycled.
- **Show Your Work example is WS-W1-01, not a later worksheet.** It's the first artifact the cohort hits and therefore the one that sets the tone for every Show Your Work post that follows.
- **Andrew's filled-in worksheet uses real-shape TrackForce examples with names/details generalized.** If any judgment call is too close to a live deal to post publicly, Andrew should swap specifics before going live.
