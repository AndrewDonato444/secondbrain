# Building Out Loud — Manager Forge Pre-Sell Window

**Window:** ~May 4 – June 15, 2026 (six weeks before enrollment opens)
**Cadence:** 3–4 walks per week in normal rotation; this plan maps 5 episodes inside that cadence that thread Manager Forge themes without pitching
**Voice:** Full BOL — walking, handheld, "things I'm learning that might help you." No decks, no frameworks-on-screen.
**Rule:** One and only one of these (Episode 5) names Manager Forge, and only at the very end. Everything else is pure value.

---

## Thread logic

The job of a sales manager is changing in a specific way, and most managers can feel it but haven't named it yet. These episodes name small pieces of the shift — one per episode. By the time enrollment opens on ~June 15, anyone who watched three or four of these should already be nodding along when Manager Forge shows up.

The through-line is a managerial identity question, not a tools question. That matters — "which AI tool should I use" is a saturated space; "what is my actual job now" is not.

---

## Episode 1 — "The part of my job AI didn't touch"

**Target post date:** Week of May 4

**Core question / hook:** I went through everything I did last week and tried to figure out which parts AI helped with, which parts it didn't, and which parts it actively made worse. The answers surprised me.

**Through-line to Manager Forge (implicit):** Installs the Week 1 frame — "where humans win / what AI owns" — without naming it as a framework. Gets the audience asking the question Manager Forge answers.

**Landing point:** The judgment calls didn't get easier. If anything, they got harder, because now I have more data pointing in more directions and I'm still the one who has to decide. That's actually the job now.

**Rough structure:**
1. Open: walking, "I did an experiment this week" — audit of last week's work
2. The three categories that emerged: AI did it well, AI did it badly, AI wasn't even in the room
3. The surprise — the "AI wasn't even in the room" column was bigger than expected
4. One specific example (anonymized — a coaching moment or a pipeline call)
5. Land: the irreplaceable stuff isn't going away. It's getting sharper.

## Episode 2 — "Why my 1:1s got worse before they got better"

**Target post date:** Week of May 11

**Core question / hook:** I added AI prep to my 1:1s and for about a month they got noticeably worse. Here's what I think happened and what I had to change.

**Through-line to Manager Forge (implicit):** Teases Week 4 — AI prep without flattening the human conversation. Names the failure mode most managers are going to hit.

**Landing point:** The AI can do the prep. It can't do the meeting. If you let it do the meeting, you get a very efficient waste of everyone's time.

**Rough structure:**
1. Open: "I'm going to admit something kind of embarrassing"
2. What I added — AI prep briefs before every 1:1
3. What went wrong — I was showing up with a report instead of a conversation
4. The moment I noticed — specific rep, specific meeting, what they said
5. What I changed — the prep is for me, not for the meeting
6. Land: AI prep is upstream of the meeting, not inside it.

## Episode 3 — "The three kinds of reps AI is creating"

**Target post date:** Week of May 18

**Core question / hook:** I'm seeing three different patterns on my team around AI, and each one needs a completely different coaching conversation. Most of the coaching advice I'm reading doesn't account for this.

**Through-line to Manager Forge (implicit):** Directly previews Week 3 — the three AI coaching archetypes. Names the problem without naming the framework.

**Landing point:** "Use AI more" is not coaching. It's a slogan. If you can't name which archetype your rep is, you don't have a plan, you have a hope.

**Rough structure:**
1. Open: "I've been watching my team and I keep seeing three patterns"
2. Pattern 1: the rep who's over-relying — AI wrote the email, they didn't read it
3. Pattern 2: the rep who's avoiding — still doing it the 2022 way
4. Pattern 3: the rep who's AI-native but missing something else — discovery, pushback, depth
5. Honest beat: I've miscoached all three of these in the last six months
6. Land: the conversation is different for each one. Same speech doesn't work.

## Episode 4 — "I rewrote my interview questions"

**Target post date:** Week of May 25 or June 1

**Core question / hook:** I've been interviewing sales candidates for a decade with roughly the same questions. I changed them this quarter. Here's what I'm asking now and what the answers are telling me.

**Through-line to Manager Forge (implicit):** Previews Week 5 — AI fluency as a hiring dimension. Makes the abstract concrete.

**Landing point:** I'm not screening for AI skills. I'm screening for the thing underneath it — whether they'd go figure something out or wait for someone to tell them. AI just happens to be the best forcing function I've found for that.

**Rough structure:**
1. Open: "I did a rep interview last week and I realized halfway through my questions were from 2019"
2. The old question I cut — and why it stopped telling me anything
3. The new question I'm asking — and the specific answer that made me change it
4. What a good answer sounds like vs. what a "pass" answer sounds like
5. The underneath-it thing — initiative, not tools
6. Land: you're not hiring an AI user, you're hiring a person who can operate in a new environment.

## Episode 5 — "What I wish I'd had two years ago" *(the one Manager Forge mention)*

**Target post date:** Week of June 8 (the week enrollment opens ~June 15)

**Core question / hook:** Somebody asked me last week what I wish I'd had when this AI shift first started hitting my job. I've been thinking about the answer all week.

**Through-line to Manager Forge (implicit → explicit):** This is the moment — the episode ends with the Manager Forge mention. The whole video earns the right to name it.

**Landing point:** I wish I'd had a room with other managers going through the same thing, a set of tools I could actually use the next Monday, and someone who was honest about what they'd tried and what blew up. That's what I've been building for the last few months.

**Rough structure:**
1. Open: "A friend asked me this question and I've been chewing on it"
2. Beat 1: I wish I'd had other managers to compare notes with — not a conference, a room
3. Beat 2: I wish the stuff I was reading had been built by people actually managing, not just talking
4. Beat 3: I wish someone had told me which parts of my job were not negotiable
5. Pivot: "That's basically what I've been building"
6. The single mention: "It's called Manager Forge. Six-week cohort for sales managers working through this shift. Founding cohort opens this week at www.manager-forge.com if you want to look at it. Back to the point —"
7. Return: one last beat reinforcing the real message — the shift is still underway, and whatever you do, don't do it alone.

**The mention rule:** One sentence plus a URL. No price, no agenda, no "here's what's included." The rest of the video is the pitch. The mention is just where to find it.
