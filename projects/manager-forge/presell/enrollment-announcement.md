A few months ago I tried to write down exactly what my job as a sales manager is now, versus what it was three years ago.

It was harder than I thought.

Not because the work is mysterious — I do it every day with ten reps across two countries. But because so much of what I used to do, AI does now. First-pass pipeline analysis. Deal summaries. Follow-up drafts. A real percentage of the thing I used to call "managing."

What's left is the part I can't hand off. The judgment calls. The coaching conversation that can't be a template. The read on a candidate in the room. The moment you decide to push a rep versus hold back.

That's the actual job now. And most of the advice I can find for it is either written by people who haven't managed in a decade, or by people selling me a tool.

So I built something. Six-week cohort for sales managers working through exactly this shift. I teach from inside the seat — I'm still VP of Sales at a SaaS company, I still run the pipeline reviews, I still miscoach reps and figure out why after. Every session ends with one concrete thing you run Monday morning. No decks. No "AI-powered" anything. Small room — ten managers.

I'm calling it the Founding Cohort because the first ten will genuinely shape what the program becomes, and the pricing reflects that. July 13 start.

www.manager-forge.com if you want to look.

I'm not entirely sure what I think the program will be a year from now. I'm pretty sure about what it is right now, which is the thing I wish I'd had two years ago.
