# Skool Welcome Video — Script

**Use:** Pinned video at the top of the Manager Forge Skool community. Auto-plays (or obviously-sits-first) when a new enrollee lands in the community on day one.
**Target length:** 2:00 – 2:45
**Format:** Andrew talking to camera. Plain background, desk, no slide overlays, no edits except maybe a single lower-third with his name + role the first time he appears.
**Voice:** Conversational. Spoken cadence. Short sentences. The reader should be able to imagine Andrew actually saying these words without rehearsing.

---

## Cue legend

- `[OPEN ON CAMERA]` — Andrew framed chest-up, eye contact
- `[BEAT]` — small pause, no edit
- `[HALF SMILE]` — warmth, not performed
- `[LEAN IN]` — slight posture shift, camera notices it
- `[CLOSE TAB CUE]` — the "stop watching the video and go do this" moment
- `[END ON FRAME]` — hold still one second before cutting

---

## Script

`[OPEN ON CAMERA]`

Hey. Welcome in.

If you're watching this, you're enrolled in the Manager Forge Founding Cohort. Ten of you. Which means this community right now is pretty small, and that's on purpose.

`[BEAT]`

I want to spend about two minutes telling you why this cohort exists, what the next six weeks are going to do, and what to do first. In that order.

`[BEAT]`

Why this cohort exists.

A few months ago I tried to write down what my job as a sales manager is now versus what it was three years ago. I couldn't do it cleanly. Too much of what I used to do, AI does. Pipeline analysis, call summaries, first-pass coaching notes — that stuff is gone from my week, and it's not coming back.

What's left is harder to name. Judgment calls. Reading a rep in the room. Knowing when to push and when to hold. The coaching conversation that can't be a template.

That's the job now. And most of what's out there for managers is either written by people who haven't sat in the seat in a decade, or it's somebody selling you a tool.

`[HALF SMILE]`

So I built this instead. I'm still in the seat. I manage ten reps. I run the pipeline reviews. I miscoach reps and figure out why afterward, the same way you do.

`[LEAN IN]`

What the next six weeks are going to do.

We're going to rebuild the operating model of your manager job around what's actually there now. One session a week, ninety minutes, live. Six of them. Every session ends with one specific thing you run Monday morning. Not homework. Not frameworks for the back of your notebook. An actual artifact.

By Week 6 you'll have six of those stacked — pipeline review protocol, coaching plan, 1:1 format, hiring process, culture read, thirty-sixty-ninety roadmap. That's the stack.

`[BEAT]`

The room matters as much as the material. When I say "cohort," I mean it. You're going to see the other nine managers' work. You're going to post yours. The reason the room is ten and not a hundred is because at a hundred, nobody reads each other. At ten, you do.

`[CLOSE TAB CUE]`

What to do first.

Three things, today.

One — post in the Introductions space. Name, role, team size, and one sentence on what you're hoping is different after six weeks. Takes you three minutes. I read every one of them.

Two — accept the calendar invite for Session 1. Block it now before something else takes the slot.

Three — open the Foundations Library. Start with Module Six, Effective One-on-Ones. Not because it's first in sequence — because it's the layer Week Three and Week Four are going to sit on top of.

`[BEAT]`

That's it. Don't try to binge the library. Don't read ahead. The pre-work I'll send you every Monday is the pre-work I actually want you to do. Everything else is available if you want it — but you don't owe it to me.

`[HALF SMILE]`

One more thing. The "For Andrew" space in the community — that's where you ask me things between sessions. I check it on weekdays. I answer before the next live session. That's a real commitment, not a line in a welcome video.

`[BEAT]`

Alright. See you Sunday.

`[END ON FRAME]`

## Production notes

- **Do not teleprompter this verbatim.** Andrew should read the script through twice, then record from memory with bullet points on a phone. The goal is that it sounds spoken, not read. If a line reads awkwardly out loud, cut or change it on the fly.
- **No B-roll.** Single take, single frame. One camera.
- **No music.** Background silence is the voice cue.
- **No branded intro card or outro card** — the welcome video is the first thing they see in the community; it doesn't need a brand wrapper.
- **Pinned post copy (goes above the video in the community):**

> Start here. 2-minute video from me. Three things to do today are at the end. — Andrew
