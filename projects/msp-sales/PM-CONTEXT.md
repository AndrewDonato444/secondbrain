# MSP Sales — PM Context

> Read this first every session. Contains everything needed to continue without re-litigating decisions already made.

---

## What This Is

A joint venture consulting business targeting Managed Service Providers (MSPs). Andrew and Russ Gregory as the core team.

**The core thesis:** MSPs have nice little businesses — recurring revenue, loyal clients — but they're almost universally afraid to grow because they don't know how without breaking what's working. Most are also being sold by people who don't understand the technology, and managed by engineers who don't understand sales. The JV addresses the full picture.

## The Russ Framework: MSP Maturity

Russ's positioning is maturity = scalability. Key deliverables he brings:

- **One SOW that covers everything** — in-scope, out-of-scope, edge cases. No more custom SOWs per client
- **MSA + SLA templates** — plug-and-play language, legally sound
- **Partner program design** — incentivize right behavior through transparency (MSRP on website, sell at discount) rather than mandates
- **Recurring revenue model** — shift from bucket-of-hours/break-fix to proactive maintenance with predictable MRR
- **Sales-tech alignment** — stop sales from overselling capabilities engineers can't deliver; stop tech from being invisible to the sales motion

## GTM: The Two Problem Lists

**List 1 — Known problems (what MSPs admit to):**
- Sales team isn't performing
- Can't scale without things breaking
- No consistency in how they package or price services
- Losing deals they should win

**List 2 — Hidden problems (surface only after talking to us):**
- Overutilizing engineers because sales oversold capabilities — drives out senior talent in ways that don't show on KPIs
- No partner incentive structure → partners don't push their services
- Sales and tech don't speak the same language → constant custom work, no scalability
- Managers promoted from IC with no ramp → they know the tech, not the people management

Marketing and outreach leads with List 1. Consulting value is unlocking List 2 once we're in.

**Inbound > outbound.** Russ's POV (strong): MSPs cannot rely on cold calling or door-knocking. 80-90% inbound is the target. Andrew's LinkedIn automation + content is the engine.
